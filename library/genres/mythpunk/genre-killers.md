## Failure Modes

- ✓ Common Pitfalls – 8 major pitfalls with solutions (in table format)
- Avoid positioning characters' problems as purely personal/psychological when systemic dimensions exist
- Avoid tokenism—ensure marginalized characters drive plot and thematic development
- Avoid generic or stock descriptive language
- Avoid redemption arcs that flatten complexity; growth should increase rather than decrease moral ambiguity
- Signal perspective shifts clearly to avoid reader confusion
- Silence and ellipsis: What characters don't say carries meaning
- Avoid naturalistic speech if mythological or postmodern registers better serve your narrative
- Avoid blending multiple mythological traditions superficially; either commit to specific traditions or deliberately interrogate cultural syncretism
- Avoid treating contemporary setting as mere backdrop; let it shape character options and thematic concerns
- Avoid over-explanation; allow symbols to operate ambiguously
- Avoid excessive footnoting or explanation; let attentive readers discover layers
- Mythpunk often invents language elements that don't exist in contemporary English.
