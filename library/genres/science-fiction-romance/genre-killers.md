Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- Content Presentation Rules — Clear do's and don'ts distilled to essential guidance.
- Register: Contemporary and accessible, avoiding excessive technobabble; explain speculative concepts through character discovery rather than exposition dumps
- Pacing of Emotion: Allow emotional beats breathing room; don't rush revelations or connection moments for plot momentum
- Humor: Employ character-based humor to create intimacy and prevent melodrama, though avoid undermining high-stakes scenarios
- Cultural Specificity: When depicting alien species or off-world cultures, establish distinct behavioral patterns, values, and communication styles; avoid making aliens function as humans with different appearances
- Hierarchy of Detail: Prioritize worldbuilding elements relevant to the romantic plot; avoid extensive exposition about peripheral systems
- Agency: Characters drive plot through their decisions; avoid having romance happen to them
- Communication: Include explicit conversations about feelings, fears, and boundaries; resistance to emotional expression should serve character development, not authorial avoidance
- Specificity: Dialogue should reveal character voice, backstory, and emotional stakes; avoid generic exposition
- Emotional Sequencing: Avoid jumping between emotional extremes without transition; show characters processing significant developments
- Restraint in Description: Avoid excessive adjectives; let specific details carry weight
- Avoid reducing character agency; show them choosing the bond even when biologically inclined toward it
- Deep POV: Avoid narrative distance; show events through character perception and interpretation
- Don'ts
- ✗ Don't subordinate romance to worldbuilding exposition
- ✗ Don't anthropomorphize alien species without establishing unique communication/perception
- ✗ Don't rely on miscommunication as primary plot driver
- ✗ Don't force romantic connection without credible catalyst (forced proximity, vulnerability, interdependence)
- ✗ Don't employ technological or species difference as mere window dressing
- ✗ Don't violate consent through biological compulsion without addressing character agency
- VIII. Do’s and Don'ts
- DON'T: Let political maneuvering or space battles occupy more than 40% of the word count. The couple is the focus.
- DON'T: Create aliens that are too human. Keep them culturally or physically distinct enough to feel exotic.
