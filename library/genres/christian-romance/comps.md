- Christian romance is distinguished not by the mere presence of religious characters, but by how faith informs the entire narrative arc. The protagonist's emotional transformation is intertwined with their spiritual journey[2]. Readers expect more than attraction between characters; they seek stories where two people experience growth guided by Christian principles and Biblical understanding.
- Before the formal Christian romance genre emerged, 19th-century "sentimental fiction" laid the groundwork. Authors like Harriet Beecher Stowe created stories "for and about women that celebrated conservative Protestant piety as well as love and home." This tradition established the precedent for linking romance with faith and conservative values[3].
- Pioneering authors like Janette Oke and Francine Rivers established the genre's conventions through works like Love Comes Softly (1979) and Redeeming Love (1991), which remain influential today.
