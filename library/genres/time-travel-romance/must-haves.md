# Time Travel Romance Must-Haves

Non-negotiable elements for this genre. Each item below should be verifiable in the finished manuscript.

- [ ] The temporal mechanics are internally consistent. The rules governing how, when, and why travel occurs must be established early and honored throughout. Retroactive rule changes that rescue the plot invalidate reader trust.
- [ ] The romance is the primary arc. Even during the most active time-travel plot sequences, the emotional trajectory of the relationship must remain the throughline readers are tracking. When readers lose the romance thread for more than a chapter, they lose the book.
- [ ] The love interest appears early enough to matter. The central romantic relationship must be established before the first major temporal disruption. A love interest introduced after the primary temporal twist has too little page time to earn the HEA.
- [ ] The historical or future setting exerts real pressure on the relationship. Period social structures, gender norms, class constraints, or technological absence must shape what the characters can and cannot do — not merely provide scenic backdrop.
- [ ] Both characters have clear agency and interiority. Neither protagonist nor love interest exists solely to be rescued by, or to facilitate the arc of, the other.
- [ ] The cost of temporal separation is shown, not asserted. Longing, grief, and the passage of experienced time must be dramatized through scene, not summarized in retrospect.
- [ ] The HEA or HFN is emotionally earned despite temporal separation. The resolution must feel like something the characters fought for across time, not a convenient loophole or deus ex mechanic that removes all prior sacrifice.
- [ ] Sacrifice is present and credible. At least one character must stand to lose something irreversible — a life in another era, a relationship, an identity — for the stakes to register as real.
- [ ] Period language and detail are accurate enough to sustain immersion. This does not require academic precision, but anachronistic speech patterns or culturally impossible behavior will pull informed readers out of the story.
- [ ] The time-travel device or trigger is introduced, not assumed. Whether it is a stone circle, a family heirloom, a government program, or an unexplained compulsion, the mechanism must be acknowledged within the story's logic.
