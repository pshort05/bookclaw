*Romantic comedies typically follow this structure, building connection and tension in equal measure.*

1. **Meet-Cute/Inciting Incident** — protagonists encounter each other in a memorable, setting-appropriate way that establishes both chemistry and conflict
2. **Attraction Coalesces** — initial spark deepens; banter reveals character; forced proximity drives escalating awareness of the other's appeal
3. **External Obstacles Introduced** — career pressure, geographic distance, family obligations, social circumstances, or competing life goals create tension
4. **Deepening Connection Through Vulnerability** — protagonists begin revealing authentic selves, fears, past wounds, insecurities beneath protective personas
5. **Comedic Escalation** — relationship complications intensify; misunderstandings, third-party interference, or situational comedy generate laughs while raising stakes
6. **Internal Conflict Peaks** — protagonist(s) confront fears about vulnerability, commitment, or whether love is possible given their circumstances
7. **Crisis/Separation** — misunderstanding, miscommunication, or unresolved fear creates temporary separation; protagonists must reckon with how much the other matters
8. **Grand Resolution** — protagonist(s) choose each other explicitly; obstacles are overcome through character growth and mutual commitment; happy ending lands
