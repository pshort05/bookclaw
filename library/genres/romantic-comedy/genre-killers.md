*Avoid these patterns—they undermine the delicate balance that makes romantic comedy work.*

- ✗ **Forced Humor Without Authenticity** — trying too hard to be funny; joke-per-page pacing exhausts readers; humor that doesn't stem from character
- ✗ **Unearned Happy Ending** — protagonists don't overcome obstacles or demonstrate growth; love resolves through circumstance rather than choice
- ✗ **Chemistry-Free Romance** — protagonists are incompatible, don't understand each other, or lack genuine attraction beneath surface conflict
- ✗ **Perfect Protagonists** — flawless characters with no real challenges; readers don't invest in love stories when characters have no authentic growth
- ✗ **Heavy-Handed Contemporary Issues** — tackling serious themes (racism, abuse, disability) performatively without depth; cheapens both humor and gravity
- ✗ **Mean-Spirited Comedy** — protagonists mock or belittle each other; cruelty masquerading as banter destroys romantic appeal
- ✗ **Stale Trope Execution** — enemies-to-lovers where enemies don't have genuine conflict; fake dating where pretense feels arbitrary; underdeveloped trope foundation
- ✗ **Tone Whiplash** — abruptly shifting between heavy drama and light comedy; inconsistent emotional register confuses reader investment
