*These titles exemplify how contemporary romantic comedy succeeds; study their dialogue, character development, and tonal balance.*

**Book Lovers by Emily Henry:** Rival literary agents engaged in professional sparring gradually discover mutual respect and genuine attraction through witty exchanges, forced collaboration, and meaningful vulnerability about their careers and personal ambitions. Henry's masterful banter and smart protagonists exemplify contemporary rom-com success.

**The Love Hypothesis by Ali Hazelwood:** A fake dating premise becomes genuine love as a grad student and her fake boyfriend navigate workplace dynamics, family obligations, and authentic connection within STEM settings. Hazelwood's scientific authenticity and character-driven humor demonstrated paranormal rom-com potential.

**People We Meet on Vacation by Emily Henry:** A will-they/won't-they friendship-to-romance story across multiple vacations spanning years, revealing internal conflict and character growth while maintaining witty banter and contemporary relationship negotiation.

**Just for the Summer by Abby Jimenez:** A fake summer fling between longtime friends becomes complicated and steamy as emotions escalate. The contemporary premise and character-driven comedy exemplify high-concept rom-com success.

**You Should Be So Lucky by Cat Sebastian:** A contemporary romance featuring gay protagonists, forced proximity through professional collaboration, and witty dialogue that reveals character while advancing attraction—demonstrating rom-com's expanded representation and inclusive success.
