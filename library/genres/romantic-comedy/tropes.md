*The genre thrives on playful, recognizable relationship dynamics; readers want the comfort of knowing how the journey will end, but the joy is in discovering the unique path.*

- **Enemies-to-Lovers** — adversarial banter gradually reveals emotional vulnerability; romantic attraction coexists with genuine conflict
- **Fake Dating** — performers attempt romantic pretense; awkward couple situations become real feelings as pretense crumbles
- **Friends-to-Lovers** — long-standing emotional intimacy transforms into romantic partnership; the vulnerability of risking established friendships
- **Second Chance Romance** — former flames reunite after separation, misunderstanding, or bad timing; history-laden attraction with unfinished business
- **Meet-Cute** — charming, memorable first encounters in contemporary contexts: dating apps, workplace, social media, chance collisions
- **Forced Proximity** — trapped elevators, shared office space, temporary cohabitation, or circumstantial togetherness generates attraction and vulnerability
- **Opposites Attract** — contrasting protagonists possess genuine functional synergy and mutual respect despite surface differences
- **Workplace/Forbidden Romance** — professional ethics tension, power dynamics, hybrid work normalization creating opportunity for connection
