# genre-killers

#### Tropes Declining

##### The Yellow Peril Sorcerer-Villain

An antiquated, racist trope where the antagonist is an inscrutable, ancient Asian wizard/engineer scheming to destroy the West with exotic magic. Modern Silkpunk inverts or completely avoids this by centering Asian perspectives and creating nuanced antagonists with culturally coherent motivations. This trope is now a marker of poorly researched, Orientalist fiction and is rejected by the core readership.

##### The Monolithic 'Eastern' Culture

Worldbuilding that treats a vast, diverse continent as a single, homogeneous culture with one language, one fashion, and one philosophy. Informed readers now expect and demand the same complexity found in European-inspired fantasy---internal rivalries, regional differences, diverse ethnic groups, and ideological schisms within the imagined world. Generic 'pseudo-China' or 'pseudo-Japan' settings feel lazy and outdated.

##### The White Savior in a Silk Robe

A narrative where an outsider (often coded as Western) arrives, quickly masters the local esoteric technology/magic better than the natives, and becomes the chosen one to save the culture from itself. This is being replaced by narratives of endogenous change, where heroes arise from within the culture, leveraging their deep, inherited understanding of its systems to enact reform or revolution.
