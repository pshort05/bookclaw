Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- | Pitfall | Fix |
- | :--- | :--- |
- | **The Info-Dump Prologue:** Starting with pages of climate history. | **Start *In Media Res*:** Begin with the climate-catalyzed crime. Weave world-building into action and dialogue.
- | **The Generic Wasteland:** A setting that's just "hot and dead." | **Define Specific Stressors:** Pick 2-3 dominant, researched stressors (e.g., perpetual acid rain, pandemics from permafrost melt, killer pollen seasons). Show their daily impacts.
- | **The Pure Evil Corp:** A mustache-twirling villain who loves pollution. | **Create Nuanced Antagonists:** The CEO might believe his geoengineering is saving humanity, or the water-hoarder is trying to save his genetically vulnerable child. Their logic is compelling.
- | **The Techno-Fix Deus Ex Machina:** A magic tech solves everything in the end. | **Embrace Hard Choices:** Solutions should be partial, costly, and require sacrifice. Technology can be a tool, not a savior.
- | **The Disconnected Mystery:** A standard murder mystery that happens to have a dusty backdrop. | **Integrate Cause & Effect:** Re-conceive the murder weapon, motive, and hiding of evidence so they are impossible without the climate context.
-    **Enclave Elite (Corporate/Government):** Sanitized, technical language. Uses euphemisms ("managed relocation," "resource reallocation"). Speech is calm, controlled, aiming to project stability. Motives: preservation of power, order, and capital.
-    **Technical/Professional Class (Engineers, Scientists):** Precise, data-driven. May be ethically conflicted. Switches between jargon and frustrated plain speech. Motives: solving technical problems, personal ethics, career survival.
-    **Adapted Locals/Communal Survivors:** Pragmatic, observational. Rich with slang and references to specific past disasters. Knowledge is experiential, not theoretical. Suspicious of outsiders. Motives: community survival, protection of local resources.
-    **Displaced/Refugees:** Speech marked by trauma and loss. May be monosyllabic or fiercely poetic. Focus on basics: shelter, water, safety. Motives: immediate survival, finding a place, bearing witness.
-    **Radicals/Eco-Activists:** Passionate, ideological. May use militant or spiritual rhetoric. Frame events in moral absolutes. Motives: systemic change, retribution, prevention of further harm.
