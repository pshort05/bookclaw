## Obligatory Scenes

-  [ ] What emotional beat does this scene serve?
-  [ ] What does each character want in this scene? (Not the same thing often.)
-  [ ] What does each character fear in this scene?
-  [ ] What does the reader *not yet know* that this scene reveals?
-  [ ] Does this scene move the romance or external plot forward?
-  [ ] Open with action or dialogue, not description or internal monologue
-  [ ] Use sensory details sparingly but specifically
-  [ ] Vary dialogue and internal thought; don't over-explain emotions
-  [ ] Show subtext through what characters don't say
-  [ ] Include at least one small moment of vulnerability or recognition
-  [ ] End with a shift: character perspective changes, relationship deepens, or conflict escalates
-  [ ] Does this scene serve a narrative function or is it filler?
-  [ ] Is the emotional tone consistent with story phase?
-  [ ] Does dialogue sound like adults, or is there residual teen-voice patterns?
-  [ ] Are backstory elements necessary here, or can they be cut/moved?
-  [ ] Does the scene end on a beat that makes readers want the next scene?
