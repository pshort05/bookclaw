Every book in this genre needs these essential elements.

## Essential Checklist

-  [ ] Protagonist introduced in life crisis (divorce, career transition, empty nest, mortality prompt)
-  [ ] External goal established separate from romance
-  [ ] Hint at what will change dropped early
-  [ ] Voice and tone established; world feels lived-in
-  [ ] Secondary characters (children, friends, ex-partners) introduced naturally
-  [ ] Underlying wound or fear suggested (not explained)
-  [ ] Love interest introduced in their own context; they have agency and goals
-  [ ] Meet-cute (or meet-complicated) creates forced proximity or shared challenge
-  [ ] Chemistry builds through dialogue, proximity, recognition of the other
-  [ ] Small moments of emotional opening before major vulnerability
-  [ ] Physical attraction acknowledged but grounded in emotional connection
-  [ ] Complications hinted at (external obstacles, past trauma, timing issues)
-  [ ] Intimacy escalates gradually; emotional before physical
-  [ ] External conflict peaks (job offer, family crisis, logistics)
-  [ ] Internal conflict dominates (fear of repetition, unworthiness, sabotage)
-  [ ] One or both characters pull away or choose past safety
-  [ ] Explicit conversation about what separates them
-  [ ] Night-of-soul moment where hope seems lost
-  [ ] Secondary characters' reactions deepen stakes
-  [ ] Sensory details shift: world feels colder, smaller, diminished
-  [ ] Character realization rooted in personal truth, not external rescue
-  [ ] Active choice to recommit despite costs
-  [ ] Direct conversation addressing the core conflict
-  [ ] Physical reunion grounded in emotional breakthrough
-  [ ] Secondary plot threads resolved
-  [ ] HEA shows practical life compromise, not all problems solved
-  [ ] Epilogue (optional) demonstrates relationship's sustainability
