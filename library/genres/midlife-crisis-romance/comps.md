## Comparable Titles

- Narrative Voice:**
- Close third-person present or first-person present. Voice should feel intelligent, warm, occasionally self-deprecating. Respect reader intelligence; don't condescend when referencing complex adult realities (finances, health, adult children, professional identity).
- Prose Tone (Examples):**
-  ✓ "She'd spent three decades being competent. Now she was competent and horny. It was exhausting."
-  ✓ "He wasn't young, but he was alive in ways she'd forgotten possible."
-  ✗ "She hated her aging, wrinkled body." (Apologetic; avoid.)
-  ✗ "He dominated her with his masculine power." (Generic; specify.)
- Description Style:**
-  Reveal physical traits through action and self-perception, not catalogue. Distribute across scenes.
-  Use sensory specificity: textures, smells, weight of attention. Since characters often rediscover physical sensation, make it concrete.
