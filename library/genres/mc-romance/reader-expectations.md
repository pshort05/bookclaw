MC romance delivers a dark, intense love story set inside a motorcycle club's tight hierarchy and outlaw code, where danger and loyalty are inseparable.

## Tone & Mood

The tone is raw, gritty, and unapologetically masculine, balanced by genuine warmth within the club family. Darkness is not gratuitous — it serves character stakes. Violence, criminal enterprise, and moral ambiguity are present but never glorified without consequence. Humour surfaces through banter and brotherhood, cutting tension without undermining it. The emotional register swings wide: from menace and threat on one side to fierce protectiveness and tenderness on the other. Readers expect that roughness and loyalty coexist in the same characters.

## Pacing

Opening chapters establish the MC world and its rules before the central romance ignites. The middle act alternates between relationship escalation and club-level external threat — one feeds the other. Never let either thread go cold for more than two or three chapters. The black moment typically involves the club's danger spilling directly onto the heroine, forcing the hero to choose between brotherhood obligations and the woman he loves. Resolution closes both the club threat and the emotional wound simultaneously.

## Setting Conventions

The clubhouse is the heart of the world: a physical space with strict hierarchy (President, VP, Sergeant-at-Arms, Prospects) that readers want rendered in authentic detail. Bikes are character props, not decoration — brand, sound, and the act of riding carry emotional freight. Territory, runs, and rival clubs or law enforcement create the external pressure map. The found-family atmosphere of the MC — church meetings, parties, old ladies, prospects earning their patch — must feel lived-in and specific, not like backdrop.

## Character Archetypes

The hero is typically a high-ranking club officer: President or VP, with authority that isolates him. He is physically formidable, emotionally guarded, and loyal to his brothers above all — until the heroine breaks that order. The heroine is often an outsider (a local woman, a runaway, someone with her own dangerous past) who sees beneath the leather and ink. Secondary characters — the loyal VP, the fierce club mother, the dangerous rival club member — carry subplots that deepen the world. Redemption arcs are expected but must be earned through action, not declaration.

## Length & Format

Standard length is 80,000–110,000 words. Series are the norm: each book follows a different club member, so readers invest in the ensemble from book one. Dual point-of-view (alternating hero and heroine chapters) is strongly preferred. Explicit intimate content is expected in mainstream MC romance; fade-to-black is the exception, not the rule. Cliffhangers within a series are tolerated but the central couple's arc should reach a satisfying resolution per book.
