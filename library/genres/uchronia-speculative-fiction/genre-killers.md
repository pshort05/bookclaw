Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- | Do | Don't |
- | :--- | :--- |
- | **DO** anchor your story with a precise, clear POD (e.g., "Alexander the Great survives his fever in 323 BC"). | **DON'T** start with a vague divergence or multiple unrelated PODs.
- | **DO** research the era deeply to understand the *context* your POD changes. | **DON'T** get bogged down in historical trivia that doesn't affect your plot or world.
- | **DO** follow the logical consequences of your POD through technology, society, and politics. | **DON'T** change only the big event (e.g., who wins a war) but leave everything else identical.
- | **DO** use viewpoint characters to explore and explain the world naturally. | **DON'T** rely on omniscient narrator info-dumps or "As you know, Bob..." dialogue.
- | **DO** consider how language, names, and place names might have evolved differently. | **DON'T** assume all modern nations and borders would exist unchanged.
- | **DO** let the altered history comment on themes relevant to our world. | **DON'T** write a thinly-veiled political rant; theme should emerge from story.
- Template:** Character-specific vocabulary + Period-appropriate concern/reference + Embedded world-building detail.
-    **Example 1 (Technological Divergence - Steampunk 1890s):** "Pass the aether-grease, would you? This condenser valve is chattering like a telegraph key. If the foreman from the Edison-Tesla Combine sees it, he'll have us on coal-shoveling duty for a month."
-    **Example 2 (Political Divergence - Roman Empire Survives):** "The Procurator's new edict on Galilean synagogues is causing unrest in the Syrian quarter. The Vigiles are doubling their patrols. It's bad for business."
-    **Example 3 (Social Divergence - Gender Roles Reversed):** "Aunt Eleanor expects your proposal of circuit-manageership by Tuesday, John. Don't dally; the Bank of Matriarchs has already approved her line of credit for the new loom-works."
-    **Pitfall: The Anachronistic Character.** A protagonist with 21st-century morals and knowledge in a pre-20th century setting.
-    **Fix:** Ground the character in their time's worldview. Their conflict can arise from challenging *specific* period norms, not all of them. Give them a period-appropriate skillset.
-    **Pitfall: The History Textbook.** Long passages describing the alternate world's history, politics, or technology.
-    **Fix:** Dramatize exposition. Show a parliamentary debate instead of describing the political system. Have a tool break down to explain how it works.
-    **Pitfall: The Static World.** The world only exists as a backdrop for the plot, not changing in response to it.
-    **Fix:** Let the world react. News reports, changing street graffiti, shifts in dialogue from minor characters should reflect the plot's progression.
-    **Pitfall: The Inconsequential Divergence.** The "alternate" history is indistinguishable from ours except for one labeled difference.
-    **Fix:** Brainstorm second and third-order effects. If the South won the Civil War, how does that affect US expansion, industrialization, and 20th-century global politics?
-    **Pitfall: Modern Dialogue in Period Costume.** Characters speak like contemporary Americans.
-    **Fix:** Research period letters, diaries, and novels. Use simpler syntax, period-appropriate idioms, and a more formal register for public speech.
-    **Aristocracy/Gentry:** More complex syntax, Latin or French phrases, references to classical literature, politics, and land management. Polite but pointed indirectness.
-    **Military/Officer Class:** Direct, imperative, uses jargon and tactical terminology. Hierarchy is constantly reinforced in address ("sir," "Lieutenant").
-    **Merchant/Middle Class:** Pragmatic, money-focused, aware of social nuance. Language mixes aspirational gentility with the bluntness of commerce.
-    **Working Class/Peasantry:** Simple, concrete vocabulary. Heavy use of trade-specific slang, local idioms, and superstitions. More direct expression of emotion and need.
-    **Intelligentsia/Scientists:** Precise, explanatory, given to analogy. May use specialized neologisms born from the timeline's advanced (or divergent) science.
