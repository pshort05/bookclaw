Tropes are the recurring patterns that readers expect and enjoy in this genre.

## Dominant Tropes

- Core Tropes (Recurring Plot & Character Conventions)
- Foundational Romance Tropes
- Enemies to Lovers: A classic trope heightened by cultural misunderstanding, prejudice, or historical conflict between groups. The "enmity" often stems from external societal pressures.
- Arranged Marriage/Marriage of Convenience: A trope rich with multicultural potential, exploring the tension between duty and desire, and the slow build of love across a cultural divide.
- Culture-Specific & Intersectional Tropes

## Rising Tropes

- The "White Savior" Subversion: Modern multicultural romance actively avoids or deconstructs this trope. The focus is on mutual growth, respect, and equality, not rescue.
- Motifs (Recurring Structural Elements)
- Tropes: Star-Crossed Lovers is dominant; high stakes of societal exile; often features power imbalances rooted in colonial or imperial contexts.
- Tropes: Focus on navigating modern family dynamics, workplace dynamics, and social media scrutiny. "Friends to Lovers" can be a buffer against external prejudice.
- Tropes: Fish out of Water is central; often involves a "stranger in a strange land" plot.
