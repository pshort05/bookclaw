# Superhero Action Must-Haves

Non-negotiable elements for this genre.

## Essential Checklist

- [ ] Action sequences are spatially legible: the reader can track who is where, which direction they are moving, and what physical object is at stake in each beat.
- [ ] Power system is consistent across every fight: the protagonist cannot solve a problem in chapter twelve with an ability that was not available in chapter three without explicit in-world reason.
- [ ] Each action beat changes the situation: characters end a fight scene in a different position — tactically, emotionally, or geographically — than they started.
- [ ] The antagonist poses a genuine physical threat: the protagonist must be capable of losing and must come close to losing.
- [ ] Civilian cost is felt, not ignored: damage to bystanders and infrastructure carries emotional weight; it is not reset between scenes.
- [ ] Escalating scale across the narrative arc: a fight in chapter two cannot demand the same stakes or spectacle as the climax; each confrontation must raise the bar.
- [ ] Combat banter is functional, not ornamental: quips must reveal character or deflect real fear — not exist simply to lighten the room.
- [ ] Team or partner dynamics create tactical variation: the ensemble must operate interdependently, with distinct power sets and roles that force cooperation rather than parallel solo action.
- [ ] The protagonist earns each victory through competence and cost, not luck or unexplained power surges.
