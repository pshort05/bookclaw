Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- Pitfall 1: Lore Dumping**
- Long explanations of deities, festivals, or history.
-  **Fix:** Break lore into small, scene-embedded pieces (gossip in a pub, half-heard hymn, graffiti, carved symbols).[cite:32]
- Pitfall 2: One-Note Cultists**
- Locals act like identical fanatics.
-  **Fix:** Give individuals personal motives (grief, debt, fear of losing land, ambition). Show at least one genuine kindness.
- Pitfall 3: Atmosphere Without Plot**
- Beautifully eerie setting, but nothing changes.
-  **Fix:** Tie each eerie moment to a rising **cost** or **constraint**: what becomes harder, riskier, or more morally compromised?
- Pitfall 4: Random Supernatural Events**
- Scares that don’t connect to belief or ritual.
-  **Fix:** Ensure every uncanny event relates to a rule, promise, or violation (even if readers only half understand it).
- Pitfall 5: Over-Explained Ending**
- A villain monologue that explains everything.
-  **Fix:** Let visuals, ritual, and consequences do the heavy lifting. A few lines of explanation, then trust the reader.
