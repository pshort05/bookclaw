*Private eye narratives rely on investigation independence and the morally gray cases PIs encounter.*

- **The Desperate Client** — vulnerable person needs help police won't provide; hires PI to pursue investigation outside institutional constraints
- **The Missing Person** — protagonist searches for disappeared individual; investigation leads through criminal underworld or dark truths about circumstances
- **The Affair Investigation** — protagonist hired to investigate infidelity or betrayal; uncovers secrets clients didn't anticipate
- **The Blackmail Case** — protagonist hired to find blackmailer or prove innocence against false accusation; investigation into personal secrets
- **The Cold Case** — PI pursues old case where official investigation stalled; amateur detective finds evidence professionals missed
- **The Dangerous Client** — investigation becomes dangerous as powerful people try to stop PI's progress; moral choice between safety and client justice
- **The Double Cross** — client lies about investigation's true purpose; PI discovers being used for unexpected purpose; loyalty tested
- **The Police Opposition** — official law enforcement actively opposes PI's investigation; jurisdictional conflict or corruption creates obstacles
