*Private eye fiction explores justice outside official systems, the cost of uncovering truth, and what investigators learn about human nature.*

- **Justice Beyond Law** — official system fails vulnerable people; PIs pursue justice when police won't; exploring limits of institutional accountability
- **The Cost of Truth** — investigating reveals ugly realities; truth-seeking damages relationships, changes perspectives, injures PI psychologically
- **Cynicism and Care** — PIs adopt protective cynicism masking genuine investment in helping vulnerable clients; emotional armor against accumulated trauma
- **Moral Ambiguity** — PIs bend rules, use ethically gray methods; exploring where investigators draw lines and what they're willing to do for clients
- **Systemic Injustice** — investigation reveals how power structures exploit vulnerable people; PIs work against institutional inequality
- **Solitude and Connection** — PIs are fundamentally alone (occupational hazard) but genuine client connections provide meaning
- **The Unchangeable World** — some truths can't be acted on; some injustices can't be solved; PIs learn acceptance alongside determination
- **Professional Ethics and Personal Survival** — balancing client loyalty, self-protection, legal limitations, and personal code
