*Avoid these patterns—they undermine the gritty authenticity private eye fiction requires.*

- ✗ **Action-Hero PI** — protagonist survives shootouts, hand-to-hand combat, high-speed chases; ignores actual PI work's quieter methodology
- ✗ **Magic Evidence** — convenient clue appears without investigation work; instant truth revealed through lucky break
- ✗ **Simplistic Justice** — investigation leads to straightforward good-guy/bad-guy resolution; ignores moral complexity
- ✗ **Fearless PI** — protagonist never scared, injured, or psychologically affected by dangerous investigation; removes investigation stakes
- ✗ **Client as Exposition** — client exists only to explain case and disappear; no genuine relationship development or surprise revelations
- ✗ **Ignored Professional Reality** — PI breaks laws without consequences; ignores that unlicensed investigation or evidence tampering has real legal costs
- ✗ **Naive PI** — protagonist constantly fooled or manipulated despite experience; undermines professional credibility
- ✗ **Instant Relationships** — PI and client instantly trust each other; ignores realistic suspicion and verification periods
