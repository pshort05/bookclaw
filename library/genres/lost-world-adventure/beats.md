Obligatory scenes and key beats drive this genre forward.

## Obligatory Scenes

-  [ ] **Objective:** What is the scene’s goal? (Discover a clue, escape a creature, cross a chasm)
-  [ ] **Setting:** Is the environment described with 2-3 striking sensory details? Does it pose an active challenge?
-  [ ] **Character Focus:** Whose perspective dominates? Does their expertise/ personality color the description?
-  [ ] **Wonder/Terror:** Does the scene contain at least one moment of awe, curiosity, or primal fear?
-  [ ] **Conflict:** Is there an external threat (beast, environment) or internal disagreement (argument over direction)?
-  [ ] **Cost/Change:** Does the scene deplete resources (ammo, food, trust) or alter the situation (a bridge collapses, a map is lost)?
-  [ ] **Pacing:** Does the sentence structure match the mood (long, flowing for wonder; short, sharp for action)?
-  [ ] **Dialogue:** Does each line serve character, plot, or tension? Is it period-appropriate without being stilted?
-  [ ] **Exit Line:** Does the scene end on a strong narrative beat (a revelation, a decision, a new threat looming)?
