Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- | Pitfall | Fix |
- | :--- | :--- |
- | **The World Feels Small:** Only one valley/creature is shown. | **Fix:** Use distant vistas, unexplored tunnels, and mysterious lights to imply vast, unseen depth. |
- | **Stakes Are Low:** No real danger or consequence. | **Fix** Someone must die or be irrevocably changed. The environment itself should be a lethal, active force. |
- | **Info-Dump:** Stopping the story to describe everything. | **Fix:** Seed descriptions through action (e.g., “She ducked under the pulsating frond of the…”), dialogue, and character observation. |
- | **Generic Creatures:** “A T-Rex shows up.” | **Fix:** Give creatures unique, plausible ecological niches and behaviors. A “T-Rex” that hunts via bio-luminescent lure is more memorable. |
- | **Easy Escape:** The return trip is trivial. | **Fix:** The world should fight to keep them, or their evidence should create new problems (e.g., a pursued egg is about to hatch). |
- | **No Internal Conflict:** The team agrees on everything. | **Fix:** Establish competing goals from the start. The scientist wants preservation, the hunter wants a trophy, the financier wants profit. |
- While strict Victorian class dialogue may not fit all settings, characters’ professional and motivational roles shape their speech.
-  **Academic/Leader:** Precise, declarative, sometimes patronizing. Uses technical terms confidently. (“The stratigraphy is unambiguous, Jennings.”)
-  **Practical Professional (Guide, Mechanic):** Terse, imperative, metaphor-rich. Speaks in terms of cause and effect. (“That sound means the river’s rising. We go now, or we don’t go at all.”)
-  **Commercial Backer/Profiteer:** Transactional, persuasive, focused on value and risk. (“My investment bought these supplies. What’s the return? Show me the vein.”)
-  **Local/Indigenous Guide (if used respectfully):** Speech may be indirect, using parable and metaphor tied to the land. Often warns in ways outsiders initially dismiss. (“The mountain is not dead. It dreams. Your loud machines are like shouts in its sleep.”)
