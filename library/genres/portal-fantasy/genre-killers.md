*Avoid these patterns—they turn portal fantasy into generic fantasy adventure lacking the transformative power the genre offers.*

- ✗ **Inconsistent Magic System** — magic rules change to serve plot; readers notice when protagonist can't do something on Day 5 that they did on Day 3
- ✗ **Fantasy World as Reskinned Earth** — different names but identical culture, values, geography; no genuine sense of otherness or cultural collision
- ✗ **Protagonist Never Really Learns** — remains confused about basic magic/culture even near end; protagonist incompetence as running gag rather than genuine growth arc
- ✗ **Convenient Portal Access** — protagonist can return anytime without consequences; world-choice feels arbitrary because stakes aren't real
- ✗ **Mentor as Exposition Machine** — guide character exists only to explain magic system and world rules through dialogue dumps
- ✗ **Mundane-World Skills Magically Solve Fantasy Problems** — protagonist's ordinary knowledge solves every problem; fantasy world never genuinely challenges them
- ✗ **Protagonist Stays Unchanged** — ends story with same perspectives, capabilities, and values as beginning; no transformation through experience
- ✗ **Predictable Chosen One Narrative** — protagonist exactly matches prophecy/expectations; no surprises, subversions, or complications to traditional hero narrative
