*Portal fantasy relies on the journey of discovery; readers want the protagonist learning and adapting while readers experience world wonder alongside them.*

- **The Unexpected Portal** — magical gateway opens through accident, mundane action, or seeming coincidence; protagonist transported involuntarily or by naïve curiosity
- **The Chosen One (Subverted)** — protagonist expected to be prophesied hero; gradually learns the prophecy may be wrong, manipulated, or misinterpreted
- **The Ordinary Person with Extraordinary Potential** — mundane-world protagonist possesses untapped magical ability, unexpected skill, or knowledge making them valuable in fantasy world
- **The Found Mentor** — protagonist encounters guide, teacher, or ally who explains fantasy-world rules and becomes trusted companion
- **The Fish-Out-of-Water Learning Curve** — protagonist's mundane-world knowledge and skills become both liability and asset; gradually learns to navigate fantasy politics and magic
- **The Return Question** — protagonist must choose between returning home or remaining in fantasy world; explores what belonging truly means across worlds
- **Magical Politics and Factions** — fantasy world contains competing groups, hidden agendas, complex politics; protagonist gradually learns true loyalties and conflicts
- **The Cost of Magic** — magical power requires sacrifice, has limitations, or carries consequences protagonist gradually discovers through use
