*These elements separate portal fantasy from simple magical adventure; without them, you lose the transformative power the genre requires.*

- **Functional Magic System** — readers must understand how magic works, its limitations, costs, and applicability; avoid treating magic as plot convenience
- **Internally Consistent Fantasy World** — world-building rules apply consistently; geography, politics, and social structures make logical sense within their context
- **Genuine Cultural Difference** — fantasy world isn't just prettier Earth; it has genuinely different values, practices, social structures; not a human skin on human society
- **Meaningful Fish-Out-of-Water Stakes** — protagonist's mundane-world knowledge actually creates problems or requires adaptation; language barriers, social faux pas, magical incompetence matter
- **Clear Portal Logic** — readers understand how/why portal exists, whether it's one-way or two-way, what triggers travel; avoid convenient portal handwaving
- **Transformation Through Experience** — protagonist visibly changes through fantasy exposure; discovers new capabilities, perspectives, or identity; grows beyond initial self
- **Consequential World-Choice** — protagonist's decision to return or remain must carry genuine cost and meaning; not an arbitrary ending but reflection of changed values
- **Wonder and Discovery** — maintain sense of magical amazement; avoid becoming so comfortable in fantasy that readers lose sense of witnessing something extraordinary
