- Gossip & Confession: Information comes from people, not databases. The investigator must exploit social hierarchies—servants are invisible eyes; clergy hold secrets.
- Titles & Hierarchy: Address is vital. Characters must show deference to superiors (My Lord, Father, Your Grace) and dismissal to inferiors.
- The King’s Man: An agent with royal authority (Coroner/Sheriff) who is hated by the locals but necessary for order.
- The "Miracle" Crime: A murder that appears supernatural (demonic possession, a curse) which the detective must debunk to find the human method.
- Monastic Mysteries: Remain popular but over-saturated; new entries must have distinctive angle
- The Poison/Medical Puzzle - Death by poison or disease; specialized knowledge required for solution; science-mystery hybrid
- Themes Essential to Successful Medieval Mysteries
- Integration: Best used when protagonist must make choice; resolution isn't "find the killer" but "what to do with knowledge"
- What can be known, must be known, is dangerous to know
- Brother Cadfael-Style Male Monastic Detectives - Iconic Cadfael creates shadow; new entries face constant comparison; must offer distinctive variation
- Sister Fidelma-Style Legal/Advocate Protagonists - Fidelma is category leader; followers must differentiate through cultural setting, historical period, or legal system variation
- Matthew Bartholomew-Style Physician Detectives - Established and successful series; market can absorb more but new entries must offer fresh angle
