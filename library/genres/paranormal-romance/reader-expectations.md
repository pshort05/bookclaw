Romance where one or both protagonists is supernatural — vampire, werewolf, witch, or fae — and the supernatural element is inseparable from both the central conflict and the erotic charge between leads.

## Tone & Mood

The dominant tone blends dark sensuality with emotional intensity. Readers expect an atmosphere that feels dangerous and seductive in equal measure — the supernatural love interest is compelling precisely because they represent a threat. Angst is expected and welcomed; readers want to feel the weight of the obstacle (immortality, species difference, clan obligation, a curse) bearing down on the couple. Humor may appear but should not undercut the core tension. The mood often oscillates between brooding dread and passionate longing.

## Pacing

Slow-burn romantic tension is the engine of the genre. The relationship arc is deliberately drawn out through forced proximity, misunderstandings rooted in species difference, and external threats that keep the couple apart. Readers expect several near-miss moments of connection before the first real breakthrough. Action sequences (clan wars, hunter pursuits, magical crises) punctuate the romantic arc but must not overshadow it. The climax typically resolves both the external supernatural threat and the internal emotional barrier simultaneously.

## Setting Conventions

The supernatural world exists in secret alongside the human world — a hidden society with its own hierarchy, laws, and politics. Common settings include gothic estates, urban underworlds, small towns with dark histories, and academy or coven environments. The masquerade — the secret kept from ordinary humans — is a recurring source of tension. Physical environments tend toward the atmospheric: perpetual autumn, ancient stone, candlelight, storm-threatened coastlines. The human world is rarely depicted as warm or safe; readers expect the supernatural world to feel more real and alive.

## Character Archetypes

The supernatural love interest is ancient, powerful, and shaped by centuries of isolation or trauma. They are drawn to the protagonist in a way that disturbs their own equilibrium. The human or newly-turned protagonist begins from a position of vulnerability but discovers inner strength — often a latent power that explains the supernatural's attraction. Supporting cast commonly includes a loyal best friend from the mortal world, a rival love interest from within the supernatural hierarchy, a mentor figure who withholds crucial information, and a faction antagonist whose threat forces the protagonists together.

## Length & Format

Paranormal romance typically runs 80,000–110,000 words for a standalone. Series are the dominant format, with each book following either the same couple through escalating trials or a new central couple within the same supernatural world. First-person present tense is common in the contemporary submarket; third-person limited (alternating POV between the two leads) is equally accepted and standard in longer, more plot-heavy entries. Chapter length skews short to medium, maintaining momentum through frequent scene transitions.
