Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- Language & Vocabulary – Guidance on medical terminology, sensory language, and biotech metaphors with DO/DON'T examples
- Common Pitfalls – A comparison table identifying 7 major writing mistakes to avoid with better approaches
- DON'T: "She had been changed by science."
- Avoid prettified or romanticized descriptions of bodies
- DON'T: "The implant activated, and she felt different."
- Avoid nature-based metaphors (biopunk nature is engineered)
- Avoid excessive exposition; let characters speak as they would naturally
- Avoid beauty-based descriptions unless ironic or subversive
- DON'T: "She was beautiful despite her modifications."
- Avoid the "clean neon" aesthetic of cyberpunk; embrace organic chaos
- Biopunk narratives don't require traditional linearity
- Don't shy away from the disgusting or disturbing
- Avoid sentimentality; let readers feel complexity without guidance
- Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Avoid being preachy about ethics; let readers draw conclusions
- Take a scene involving biotech procedure (implantation, modification, healing). Write it focusing exclusively on sensory details: what does the procedure feel like, taste like, sound like, smell like? Avoid explanation.
- Does the dialogue avoid unnecessary exposition?
- Discomfort Is Feature: Lean into the disturbing; avoid prettification; let readers feel visceral wrongness
- The Aesthetic of "Wetware": Technology is not metal; it is organic. It pulses, bleeds, breathes, and rots. Avoid "clicking/whirring" sounds; use "squelching/thrumming/gurgling."
- Banned/Avoided: Traditional "hard" sci-fi terms like android, robot, laser, microchip, motherboard. Replace with construct, synth, bio-luminescence, neural-mesh, ganglia.
