# Cozy Romance — Tropes

Shared vocabulary, chosen for warmth. Pick three to six that keep the angst low and the
charm high; avoid any trope whose whole engine is cruelty, betrayal, or sustained misery.

Cozy-friendly favorites:
- **Grumpy / Sunshine** — gentle thaw; the grumpiness is gruff, not abusive.
- **Friends-to-Lovers** — long warmth tipping sweetly into more.
- **Small-Town Return / Found Community** — coming home, being folded into a town that cares.
- **Forced Proximity, soft version** — snowed-in cabin, shared cottage, co-running a shop.
- **Second-Chance, low-bitterness** — old sweethearts, more nostalgia than wound.
- **Single Parent / Widower finds love again** — tenderness and a ready-made family.
- **Hobby / Trade Meet** — bakery, bookshop, garden, animal rescue, holiday market.
- **Fake Dating for a sweet reason** — a wedding date, a meddling family, low-stakes pretense.
- **Pen-pals / slow correspondence** — yearning without conflict.

Use sparingly or recast gently: **enemies-to-lovers** (keep it bickering, not loathing) and
**fake dating** (no high-stakes humiliation).

Avoid entirely — these break the cozy promise: dark/mafia romance, kidnapping, infidelity,
abuse, manufactured **miscommunication** as the plot, the angsty **love triangle**, and any
**big-misunderstanding** breakup.
