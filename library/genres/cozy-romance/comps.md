# Cozy Romance — Comparable Titles

Touchstones for calibrating warmth, low-angst pacing, and the communication-driven
resolution. The underserved-market sources point to the cozy *trend* and reader demand
(low-stakes, high-charm, communication over drama) rather than naming a settled canon, so
treat these as directional and fill live comps during book planning's market-analysis step.

- **Sarah Adams (small-town friends-to-lovers)** — deep history, slow burn, low-cruelty conflict; a clean model for the gentle register.
- **Abby Jimenez (the warm, charming end of her range)** — quirky meet-cutes and banter; use the lighter, lower-angst beats, not the heavier emotional ones.
- **Holiday / small-town category lines** (Hallmark-adjacent: cozy-community series, seasonal romances) — the purest expression of the comfort-read promise and the connected-community series shape.

When researching live comps, search the current "cozy romance" / "low-angst" /
"comfort read" shelves and BookTok cozy lists rather than the broad romance bestseller
charts — the contracts differ. Note each comp's gentle handling of the late-book wobble:
that resolution-by-conversation beat is what you're calibrating against.
