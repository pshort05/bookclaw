# Cozy Romance — Beats

The romance beat sheet, but **de-angsted**. The big structural difference from broad
romance: there is *no third-act breakup driven by manufactured drama*. The low point is a
**wobble**, not a rupture — a moment of doubt or vulnerability the leads resolve by
*talking*, which is precisely the satisfaction cozy readers come for.

- **Love-interest introduction (0–10%)** — the LI is present and charming early; warmth, not friction, is the first note.
- **Meet / catalyst (0–15%)** — a sweet, hobby- or community-anchored first encounter; chemistry obvious, conflict gentle.
- **Drawn together (15–30%)** — a soft reason to keep crossing paths (shared shop, neighbor, town event); the will-they hum begins.
- **Deepening warmth (30–50%)** — non-sexual touch and small vulnerabilities build; the leads become each other's comfort.
- **Midpoint sweetness (~50%)** — first kiss / quiet intimacy, met with shy hope rather than panic.
- **The wobble (60–75%)** — the gentle obstacle surfaces: an old fear, a busy season, a leaving-town worry. It creates doubt, not devastation.
- **The honest conversation (~80%)** — instead of a breakup, the leads *talk it through*. Communication resolves the conflict; this scene is the genre's payoff.
- **The choosing (85–90%)** — a warm declaration or quiet commitment; the leads pick each other, on purpose, eyes open.
- **Cozy resolution (90–100%)** — HEA/HFN inside the community: a settled, hopeful future and a glimpse of the warm ordinary life ahead.

## Obligatory Scenes (readers would feel cheated without these)
- **The charming meet** — sweet, often hobby- or place-driven, no humiliation.
- **The cozy set-piece** — a shared meal, festival, baking session, or rainy afternoon that *is* the relationship deepening.
- **The first turn** — a gentle kiss or confession.
- **The vulnerable share** — a quiet admission of the small fear or old hurt.
- **The honest conversation** — the leads resolve the wobble by communicating, not breaking up.
- **The earned, gentle HEA/HFN** — settled together within a community that's glad for them.

If you need more tension, raise it through *external* low-stakes stuff (the bakery's lease,
the festival deadline) — never by manufacturing a fight between the leads.
