# Cozy Romance — Must-Haves (non-negotiable checklist)

Skip these and it isn't cozy romance — it's just romance, or worse, angst in a sweater.
Treat as a revision checklist.

- [ ] A central love story with an HEA/HFN — the couple ends together and hopeful.
- [ ] Low angst and low stakes: no life-or-death threat, no villain to defeat, no looming catastrophe.
- [ ] Conflict resolved through communication and understanding — the leads talk like functional adults.
- [ ] An honest conversation *instead of* a manufactured third-act breakup; the resolution scene is the emotional payoff.
- [ ] Two genuinely kind, emotionally competent leads — relatable people with quirks, not broken people with trauma to excavate.
- [ ] A comforting, sensory setting (small town, shop, cottage, community) that wraps around the reader.
- [ ] A warm, supportive secondary cast and a sense of belonging / found community.
- [ ] Gentle relationship escalation, including non-sexual touch and small shared vulnerabilities.
- [ ] Heat in the sweet-to-moderate band, always playful and warm, set early and held.
- [ ] A net emotional effect of comfort and safety — the reader finishes soothed, not drained.
