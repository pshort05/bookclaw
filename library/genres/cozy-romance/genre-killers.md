# Cozy Romance — Genre Killers (avoid; these make readers DNF or one-star)

The cozy promise is *emotional safety*. Break it and you've delivered the exact stress the
reader picked this book up to escape.

- **A manufactured third-act breakup** — the angsty rupture is the genre's signature betrayal. Cozy readers chose this book to *avoid* it.
- **Miscommunication as the engine** — leads who won't just talk; the whole genre is built on the opposite.
- **High stakes or a villain** — kidnapping, danger, a defeat-the-bad-guy plot. Wrong genre.
- **Trauma-mining** — deep wounds excavated at length, therapy-session heaviness, despair. Cozy heals gently; it doesn't dwell in pain.
- **Cruelty between the leads** — real cruelty, humiliation, or contempt; even enemies-to-lovers must stay at bickering.
- **Bait-and-switch tone** — a cozy cover and blurb hiding dark romance, grief-porn, or explicit-by-surprise heat.
- **No HEA/HFN** — a sad or ambiguous ending; an unforgivable break of the romance contract, doubly so here.
- **Dread or grimness** — looming bad news, an oppressive atmosphere, anything that leaves the reader anxious rather than soothed.
- **A passive lead or a controlling partner** — same as broad romance: agency and *power-with* still required; cozy never excuses control.
- **Filler that isn't comforting** — "cozy" is not an excuse for nothing happening; every scene should still warm the bond or the world.
