# Cozy Romance — Reader Expectations

Low-stakes, low-angst, high-charm romance where the conflict is gentle and resolved
through communication and understanding — never manufactured drama. The promise is
*emotional safety*: a warm, comforting read that leaves the reader soothed and happy, the
antidote to high-stress dystopias and angsty third-act breakups. Same HEA/HFN as broad
romance, but the road there is kind.

## Tone & Mood
Warm, fuzzy, gently funny, deeply hopeful. "Low stakes, high charm, and just enough whimsy
to soothe." The reader should finish feeling cared-for, not wrung out. No deep trauma
mining, no cruelty, no dread — escapism with a soft heart.

## Pacing
Unhurried but never dull. Tension comes from *will-they-define-this* and the sweet ache of
new closeness, not from external threat or impending rupture. Relationship beats still land
regularly; the leads simply talk to each other like functional adults. Small worries
(a misread text, a busy season, an old fear) surface and get *resolved on the page* rather
than festering.

## Setting Conventions
Comfort settings that wrap around the reader: a small town, a bakery or bookshop, a cottage,
a knitting circle, a holiday market, an inn. The place is cozy by design — sensory, snug,
and full of supportive community. A hobby or trade often anchors the story and the meet.

## Character Archetypes
Two genuinely kind, emotionally competent leads readers would want as friends. Quirks, not
trauma; relatable people, not broken ones needing extensive backstory. A warm supporting
cast — the nosy-but-loving neighbor, the wise elder, the ride-or-die friend — and pointedly
*no* villain who has to be defeated.

## Length & Format
Often shorter and faster than single-title romance (a comfortable category length ~50k–75k
works well). Sweet to moderate heat is the natural register, occasionally higher but always
playful, never dark. Third person or first; standalone or a connected-community series where
each book gives a different couple their gentle HEA.
