Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- | Do | Don't |
- | :--- | :--- |
- | **DO** use weather to reflect tone and inner state. | **DON'T** use weather as mere backdrop. |
- | **DO** give your protagonist a debilitating personal flaw. | **DON'T** make your protagonist a lone, brooding genius who ignores protocol. |
- | **DO** let social institutions (police, govt, family) be complicit or failing. | **DON'T** frame the crime as the work of a single "evil" individual. |
- | **DO** write dialogue with heavy subtext and restraint. | **DON'T** have characters openly state their emotions or motivations. |
- | **DO** end with a cost to the protagonist's soul or stability. | **DON'T** end with a neat bow and a celebratory drink. |
- Template:** [Character Action/Expression] + [Minimalist Dialogue] + (Subtext Note)
- Example 1: Interrogation**
-    **Don't:** "I'm very angry you won't tell me where you were! I know you're lying!"
-    **Do:** Erik studied the scuff mark on his shoe. He didn't look up. "The E6 was busy, you said. Weather was clear." He let the silence stretch. (Subtext: I have evidence contradicting you. Your move.)
- Example 2: Domestic Strain**
-    **Don't:** "Our marriage has been falling apart ever since our son died, and your constant work is making it worse!"
-    **Do:** "There's leftover *pytt i panna* in the fridge." Anna didn't turn from the window. "It's starting to frost." (Subtext: I am managing the house alone. A coldness has set in between us. The conversation is over.)
-    **Pitfall: The "Action Hero" Detective.** Fix: Ground them. Their greatest weapon is their mind, broken by grief. Their victory in a chase should leave them vomiting from stress, not quipping.
-    **Pitfall: Expository Dialogue.** Fix: Replace "As you know, the victim's DNA was found..." with a tense scene of the team arguing over the *implications* of the DNA evidence.
-    **Pitfall: The Caricature Villain.** Fix: Make the perpetrator's motive stem from recognizable human failings—neglect, greed, shame—exaggerated by systemic pressure.
-    **Pitfall: Setting as Postcard.** Fix: Don't just describe a fjord; describe the bone-chilling damp that seeps into the investigator's jacket, the oppressive silence that hides a scream.
-    **Professional/Formal (Police, Officials):** Terse, protocol-driven. Jargon is used. Emotion is suppressed. Silence is a tool. Respect for hierarchy is surface-level; resentment simmers.
-    **Intimate/Family:** Laden with shared history and unspoken resentment. Often revolves around mundane logistics (food, schedules) to avoid painful truths. Silence is heavy.
-    **Suspect/Interrogation:** A power dance. Evasive, polite, formally correct. The truth is in hesitations, overly detailed alibis, and what they do *not* ask about.
-    **Working-Class/Local:** May be more direct, pragmatic, and rooted in community gossip. Distrust of outsiders (including city police) is common.
