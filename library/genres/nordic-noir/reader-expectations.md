## Setting

Nordic Noir, also known as Scandinavian Noir or Scandi Noir, is a subgenre of crime fiction characterized by its dark, morally complex narratives set against the stark landscapes and ostensibly progressive societies of the Nordic countries. Its non-negotiable elements include a bleak, atmospheric tone that mirrors the region's long winters; a strong sense of place, where the setting acts as a character influencing the plot; and a profound focus on social democracy's hidden flaws. The protagonists are often brooding, melancholic detectives or anti-heroes burdened by personal demons, whose investigations expose corruption, hypocrisy, and the cracks in the egalitarian facade.
