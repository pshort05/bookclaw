## Obligatory Scenes

-  [ ] **Sensory Anchor:** Does the scene start with a strong sensory detail (sound, smell, texture) to ground the reader in the world?
-  [ ] **Protagonist’s Goal:** What does the POV character want in this specific scene? (e.g., find food, avoid patrol, learn a truth).
-  [ ] **Obstacle/Conflict:** What or who stands in their way? (Environmental hazard, another person, their own fear).
-  [ ] **Stakes:** What do they stand to gain or lose? Make it concrete.
-  [ ] **World-Building Grain:** Does the scene include one small, natural detail that reveals the world (e.g., a repaired tool, a faded graffiti symbol, a mutated plant)?
-  [ ] **Dialogue Purpose:** Does any dialogue serve at least one of: revealing character, advancing plot, or building the world?
-  [ ] **Change/Reaction:** Does the scene end with a change in the character’s situation, knowledge, or emotional state?
-  [ ] **Link Forward:** Does the outcome of this scene create a new question or urgency that pushes to the next scene?
