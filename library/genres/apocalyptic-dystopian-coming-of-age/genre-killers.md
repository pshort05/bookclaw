Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

-    **Pitfall: The Generic Wasteland.** Everything is grey, dusty, and full of raiders.
-    **Fix:** Imprint a specific identity on your setting. Is it a flooded city? A genetically-managed biodome gone wrong? A vast silent forest reclaiming ruins? Define its unique aesthetic and rules.
-    **Pitfall: The Passive Protagonist.** Things just keep happening to them.
-    **Fix:** After the initial catalyst, the protagonist must make active, consequential choices, even small ones (e.g., *choose* to help someone, *choose* to steal, *choose* to ask a dangerous question).
-    **Pitfall: Info-Dump World-Building.** Stopping the story to explain the history of the collapse.
-    **Fix:** Drip-feed history through artifacts (a broken sign, a child’s song with changed lyrics), arguments about the past, or the discovery of pre-fall tech/records.
-    **Pitfall: Unearned Cynicism or Hope.** The protagonist becomes a grimdark cliché or transforms the world too easily.
-    **Fix:** Anchor major shifts in their philosophy to traumatic events (for cynicism) or moments of profound, costly human connection (for hope).
-    **Enforcer/Authority:** Terse, command-based. Uses jargon and official titles. Questions are accusations. “Citizen, present your transit chit. Now.”
-    **Scavenger/Outsider:** Pragmatic, deal-focused. Speech is often a negotiation. Uses slang and references to trade, barter, dangers. “That med-kit’s worth two filters and a protein block. Take it or leave it.”
-    **Idealist/Revolutionary:** Passionate, uses ideological language, appeals to a “better past” or “future.” Can be inspiring or dogmatic. “We’re not just surviving scraps. We’re rebuilding what they broke.”
-    **Technocrat/Elite:** Clinical, precise. May use obsolete technical terms or treat people as data points. “Your biomass allocation is insufficient for continued residency. A reclamation team will assist your transition.”
