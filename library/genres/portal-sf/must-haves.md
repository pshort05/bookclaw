Portal SF earns its genre classification through scientific grounding, consequential transit, and rational engagement with what crossing actually means — without these elements, you are writing portal fantasy with a lab coat on.

## Checklist

- [ ] **The portal has a stated mechanism.** The reader should be able to name what the portal is — wormhole, quantum superposition chamber, dimensional stepper, traversal array, biological threshold with a theoretical rationale — and understand at least its basic operating parameters. The mechanism does not need to be fully explained, but it must be named and rule-governed. A portal that "just works" with no framework is a fantasy gateway.

- [ ] **The mechanism has consistent rules.** Whatever the portal can and cannot do in chapter 3, it can and cannot do in chapter 18. If the rules change, that change must be explained by the narrative — a new discovery, a violation of a constraint, a deliberate override. Rules that shift to serve plot without explanation break the SF contract.

- [ ] **Crossing has a cost.** Energy, biology, time, identity coherence, political exposure, the death of a counterpart — something is spent each time the threshold is crossed. The cost should be established before the midpoint and should compound as crossing recurs. A portal that can be used freely with no consequence makes transit trivial and removes the genre's core tension.

- [ ] **The destination world is internally consistent.** The world on the other side operates according to discoverable rules — different from the origin's rules, but coherent. If the destination's physics, biology, or society behaves differently from the origin world, those differences should be explicable in principle, even if the protagonist never fully explains them. Random or arbitrary differences are a fantasy convention, not an SF one.

- [ ] **The protagonist attempts systematic mapping.** At least one sustained sequence in which the protagonist observes, hypothesizes, tests, and revises their understanding of the destination's rules. This is the genre's signature rationalist pleasure. The mapping can fail, can be incomplete, can be dangerous — but the attempt must be present and must be driven by the protagonist's own epistemic effort, not by an exposition-delivering guide character.

- [ ] **The portal is controlled by someone other than the protagonist.** A government program, a corporation, a military installation, an eligibility algorithm — some institutional force controls access to the mechanism. Even if the protagonist eventually operates the portal themselves, the power structure around transit access should be established and should create genuine constraint. Portals freely available to whoever wants them flatten the genre's political dimension.

- [ ] **A rule is tested to its limit and the consequence is paid.** At least one established rule about the portal or the destination world should be pushed against, broken, or discovered to have a harder limit than the protagonist expected. The consequence of that breach should be material and irreversible. Genre readers come for the moment the rule breaks.

- [ ] **Return, if it occurs, is not restoration.** The protagonist who crosses back to the origin world is changed in ways the origin world cannot reverse. They may look the same, occupy the same physical space, resume the same life — but they know something now that cannot be unknowing. The ending should register this transformation, not pretend the crossing left no mark.

- [ ] **The first crossing is rendered in full.** The moment of transit — what it feels like, what the crosser perceives between worlds, what registers first on arrival — must be written as a complete scene. It is the genre's single most obligatory beat and cannot be summarized, elided, or skipped.

- [ ] **Access to the portal is a source of conflict, not a given.** Someone wants to control who crosses, or to prevent crossing entirely, or to cross without permission. The mechanism's existence is itself a political event — knowledge of it changes power relations. This conflict should be established early and should drive plot, not merely provide backstory.
