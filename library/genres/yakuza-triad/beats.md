*Plot structure balances organizational pressure, personal stakes, and the psychological cost of criminal involvement.*

- **Entry/Recruitment** — protagonist drawn or forced into organization (civilian, relative of member, undercover operative); establish initial resistance or acceptance; define relationship to yakuza codes
- **Initiation/Ritual Bonding** — formal or informal induction; sakazuki (sake cup ritual, yakuza), incense ceremony (triad), or demonstration of loyalty; protagonist learns hierarchical position and obligations
- **Early Loyalty Tests** — small assignments that escalate commitment; character crosses moral line, becomes invested in organization success; establish stakes for later conflict
- **Organizational Dynamics** — show internal structure through character interactions; demonstrate hierarchy, demonstrate mentor relationships, reveal power factions within organization
- **Personal Connection / Intimacy** — protagonist forms genuine relationship with higher-status member, family member, or peer; this becomes emotional anchor and source of later conflict
- **External Pressure Escalates** — police surveillance, rival organization threat, legal pressure, or internal betrayal forces organization into crisis mode; personal stakes entangle with organizational survival
- **Code vs. Reality Crisis** — moment where jingi/yi qi demands action that contradicts protagonist's ninjo or moral compass; loyalty and humanity irreconcilably clash
- **Betrayal or Sacrifice** — someone key is threatened, imprisoned, or killed; protagonist must choose between organizational loyalty and personal relationships
- **Escape Attempt / Final Confrontation** — protagonist attempts to leave, flee, or overthrow existing order; organization pursues relentlessly; action climax combines with psychological/emotional culmination
- **Resolution Ambiguity** — avoid clean escape; explore psychological cost of flight, trauma aftermath, impossibility of truly leaving the life; ending reflects genre's melancholic fatalism
