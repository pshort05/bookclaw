*These elements are non-negotiable; their absence weakens reader credibility and breaks genre contract.*

- **Authentic Organizational Structure** — research actual yakuza hierarchy (oyabun, wakagashira, kyodai, shatei) or triad ranks (dragon head, white paper fan, red pole, soldiers); show structure through character interactions and decisions
- **Cultural Codes and Philosophy** — yakuza: jingi, giri-ninjo tension, mono no aware, face/mentsu; triads: yi qi, karma, identity crisis rooted in history; codes must shape character motivations and conflicts
- **Hierarchical Consequences** — demonstrate that actions have organizational consequences; violation of codes triggers response (yubitsume/finger-cutting, expulsion, assassination); show enforcement of rules
- **Atmospheric Setting Specificity** — yakuza: rain-slicked Tokyo streets, neon signs, high-end host clubs, Kabukichō district, tatami rooms, tea gardens; triads: Hong Kong wet markets, cramped apartments, tea houses, cargo docks; atmospheric details anchor narrative authenticity
- **Violence Without Sensationalism** — portray brutality with aesthetic attention to aftermath, physical cost, and psychological impact; avoid glorification; show mess and horror of violence
- **Moral Complexity and Gray Areas** — protagonists make meaningful choices within moral ambiguity; avoid straightforward good-vs-evil framing; show systems that corrupt and individuals who persist despite corruption
- **Initiation/Bonding Ritual** — sakazuki ceremony (yakuza sake-cup oath), blood oath variants (triad incense ceremony), or equivalent ritual that binds protagonist to organization; ritual marks psychological transformation
- **Internal Organization Tension** — show factionalism, generational conflict (old guard vs. new blood), competing interests within organization; allow protagonist to navigate internal politics
- **Relationship Authenticity** — demonstrate genuine connection between protagonist and at least one higher-status member; this relationship becomes emotional center and source of later conflict
- **Cultural Context Respect** — render Japanese and Chinese cultural elements with specificity and respect; avoid exoticization or stereotyping; research criminal justice systems, policing approaches, historical context
