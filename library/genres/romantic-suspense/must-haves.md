*These elements separate romantic suspense from romance-with-danger or thriller-with-romance; balance is essential.*

- **Genuine External Threat** — danger is real and escalating; stalker/criminal/conspiracy genuinely dangerous and difficult to stop
- **Threat Pacing** — suspense elements increase alongside romance; neither plateau while other escalates
- **Protagonists as Partners** — both protagonists contribute to surviving danger; neither is passive victim or sole protector
- **Romance Complicating Survival** — love making protagonists simultaneously stronger and more vulnerable; caring endangering while motivating
- **Trust Development** — protagonists must learn to trust each other despite danger and suspicion; trust earned through adversity
- **Investigation or Action** — protagonists actively working to identify/stop threat rather than passively waiting for resolution
- **Authentic Jeopardy** — threat genuinely could succeed; protagonist safety not guaranteed; readers perceive real danger
- **Relationship Worth Protecting** — romance must matter enough to protagonist that protecting partner becomes central motivation
