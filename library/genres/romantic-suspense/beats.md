*Romantic suspense typically parallels relationship development with threat escalation and danger resolution.*

1. **Threat Introduction and Protagonists Meet** — danger (stalker, conspiracy, crime) endangers one or both; protagonists encounter amid crisis
2. **Initial Attraction and Distrust** — protagonists drawn together but danger makes trust difficult; vulnerability required conflicts with self-protection
3. **Threat Escalates** — stalker/criminal increases activity; danger becomes undeniable; protagonists forced closer for safety or investigation
4. **Relationship Complications Amid Danger** — protagonists must navigate growing attraction while danger intensifies; personal and external conflicts entangle
5. **Crisis Point** — major threat/attack; protagonists must choose between safety and confronting danger; vulnerability deepens as fear for partner evident
6. **Breakthrough/Revelation** — conspiracy exposed, stalker identified, or threat mechanism understood; protagonists use intelligence and partnership to counteract
7. **Climactic Confrontation** — direct confrontation with threat; protagonists face danger together; partner support crucial to survival
8. **Resolution and Secure Future** — threat eliminated; relationship survives danger intact; protagonists commit to future knowing what they've endured together
