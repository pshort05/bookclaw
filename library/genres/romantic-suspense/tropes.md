*Romantic suspense relies on external threats forcing protagonists together and intensifying emotional vulnerability.*

- **The Protective Partner** — one protagonist (often law enforcement) protects other from threat; protection becoming romance through proximity and care
- **The Witness in Danger** — one protagonist witnesses crime; must rely on law enforcement or trusted partner for protection; romance developing through forced proximity
- **The Unstoppable Threat** — stalker, killer, or criminal targeting one protagonist; partner must help evade danger; romance deepening through shared peril
- **The Conspiracy** — protagonists discover larger plot threatening both; must work together to survive and expose conspiracy
- **The Betrayal Suspicion** — protagonists must trust each other while danger escalates; relationship threatened by doubt about loyalty
- **The Marked Couple** — both protagonists targeted by threat; romantic partnership becomes survival necessity and liability
- **The Safe House Proximity** — protagonists forced into isolated setting for protection; confinement intensifying both threat and romance
- **The Double Agent** — one protagonist connected to threat (unwillingly or past involvement); relationship complicated by suspicion
