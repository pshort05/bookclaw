*These titles exemplify romantic suspense balance, grounding romance in authentic threat escalation.*

**Flowers in the Attic by V.C. Andrews:** A gothic suspense featuring family drama and danger, with romantic elements complicated by jeopardy and psychological threat; demonstrates how threat deepens intimate relationships.

**Tell Me No Lies by Carla Neggers:** A contemporary romantic suspense featuring law enforcement and civilian protagonist; danger escalates while romance deepens; protagonists working together toward threat resolution.

**The Witness by Nora Roberts:** Roberts exemplifies romantic suspense excellence; protagonist in danger relies on law enforcement protection; romance developing amid witness protection and threat escalation.

**Twisted by Laurie Halse Anderson:** A YA romantic suspense featuring high school protagonists entangled in dark mystery; danger and relationship complications intertwine throughout narrative.

**The Target by Catherine Coulter:** An FBI romantic suspense featuring law enforcement investigating conspiracy; danger, relationship complication, and investigation procedurally grounded; protagonists' romance strengthened through adversity.
