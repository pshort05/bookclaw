*Avoid these patterns—they undermine the balance romantic suspense demands.*

- ✗ **Damsel in Distress** — one protagonist helpless; other constantly rescuing; undermines partnership and romance authenticity
- ✗ **Convenient Threat Resolution** — villain suddenly caught/dies; threat magically resolves without protagonists' effort or intelligence
- ✗ **Melodramatic Danger** — threat over-the-top ridiculous; readers don't take peril seriously; eliminates suspense impact
- ✗ **Ignored Psychological Impact** — protagonists unaffected by trauma of being hunted; no PTSD, fear, or lasting psychological consequence
- ✗ **Love Solves Danger** — romance somehow enables threat resolution without practical action; magical plot device
- ✗ **Slow Pacing** — threat stagnates while romance develops; pages spent on emotional processing rather than danger escalation
- ✗ **Trust Magically Achieved** — protagonists suddenly trust each other without justification; relationship foundation undercut by unearned connection
- ✗ **One-Sided Attraction** — one protagonist loves while other remains detached; unequal investment undermines satisfying resolution
