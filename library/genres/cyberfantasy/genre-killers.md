## Failure Modes

- Economical without being sparse: Use precise language; avoid flowery description, but maintain atmospheric depth
- Avoid: "The glowing runes filled her with a sense of wonder and possibility."
- Subtext beneath text: What characters don't say matters as much as what they do
- Avoid exposition-heavy dialogue; characters assume shared knowledge
- Avoid Overuse:
- Use em-dashes sparingly for emphasis or interruption; avoid overuse for thought breaks
- Implanted Memories and False Identities—Characters discover their memories have been altered, their identities fabricated, or their entire lives constructed for purposes they don't understand. This trope enables exploration of consciousness and selfhood while creating noir-influenced mysteries.[31][32][34]
- The City—Urban environments symbolize both human achievement and failure, demonstrating our ability to create complex systems while highlighting our inability to ensure those systems serve everyone equitably.[47][5][45][46]
- Anna Mocikat—Behind Blue Eyes features the Blue Eyes, cybernetically augmented killers in a world where technology and mysticism intersect.[55]
