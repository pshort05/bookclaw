*Parallel universe narratives rely on "what if" scenarios and the uncanny experience of encountering alternate selves.*

- **The Multiverse Discovery** — protagonist stumbles into parallel universe accidentally through technology malfunction, magical accident, or scientific experiment
- **The Alternate Self** — protagonist encounters their alternate-universe version; comparing lives, choices, and outcomes creates philosophical and emotional tension
- **The Branching Point** — story explores moment where worlds diverged; small change (different college, failed audition, missed train) created dramatically different reality
- **The Dystopian Mirror** — alternate world shows protagonist's worst-case scenario or best-case fantasy becoming a prison
- **The Parallel Romance** — protagonist encounters love interest who exists in multiple universes with different relationship outcomes across realities
- **The Travel Technology** — device, magic, or accident enables universe-hopping; rules/cost of travel establish stakes for narrative
- **The Identity Collapse** — protagonist questions which version of themselves is "real"; philosophical crisis about what constitutes identity
- **The Convergence Threat** — universes beginning to merge, collapse, or affect each other; large-scale consequence of parallel world contact
