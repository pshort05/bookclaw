## Setting

The parallel universes fiction genre represents one of the most dynamic and rapidly expanding categories in contemporary speculative fiction, offering endless narrative possibilities through the exploration of alternative realities, branching timelines, and coexisting worlds. This genre encompasses diverse subgenres—from alternate history exploring historical divergence points to Isekai narratives of transportation to fantastical realms, from urban fantasy grounding magic in contemporary cities to science fantasy blending technological and mystical elements, and portal fantasy depicting transitions between primary and secondary worlds. The genre has gained remarkable prominence in recent years, particularly following the widespread cultural embrace of multiverse narratives in popular media, with authors producing sophisticated explorations of identity, choice, fate, and the nature of reality itself. The proliferation of parallel universe fiction reflects both scientific interest in quantum mechanics concepts like the many-worlds interpretation and deep philosophical fascination with questions of causality, determinism, and the infinite potential branches of human existence.
