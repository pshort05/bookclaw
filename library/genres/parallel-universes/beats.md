*Parallel universe narratives typically follow discovery and navigation of alternate reality's implications.*

1. **Current Universe Establishment** — introduce protagonist and their world; establish baseline stakes, relationships, dissatisfactions
2. **Parallel Universe Discovery** — protagonist accidentally encounters or learns of parallel reality; crossing threshold into alternate world
3. **Initial Exploration** — protagonist experiences alternate world's strangeness; discovers what changed, how current reality diverged from this one
4. **Encounter with Alternate Self** — protagonist meets their parallel-universe version; confrontation reveals what might-have-been; emotional reckoning
5. **Conflict/Complication Emerges** — plot point forcing choice or engagement with alternate world: loved ones are different, threat emerges, or technology malfunctions
6. **Larger Implications Revealed** — protagonist discovers parallel contact causes problems (universe collapse, identity confusion, unintended consequences)
7. **Climactic Choice** — protagonist must decide between universes, selves, or realities; reconciling identity questions with concrete stakes
8. **Resolution and Integration** — ending shows protagonist's transformed understanding of self, choice, identity; acceptance of unchosen paths or commitment to current reality
