*Without these elements, you have multiverse adventure without the philosophical depth parallel universes demand.*

- **Clear Divergence Point** — readers must understand what changed between worlds; small divergence creating large consequence demonstrates "what if" power
- **Consistent Alternate Logic** — once world rules are established, they apply consistently; culture, technology, relationships follow logically from divergence point
- **Identity Exploration** — parallel self isn't just alternative protagonist; encountering them raises genuine questions about which version is "real"
- **Philosophical Grounding** — story engages seriously with identity/choice questions; not just using multiverse as action backdrop
- **Emotional Stakes** — alternate worlds involve people protagonist cares about; their different versions or fates matter emotionally
- **Travel Mechanics** — readers understand how/why protagonist moves between universes; travel rules apply consistently
- **Moral Weight** — parallel people deserve moral consideration; story treats alternate universes as real with real inhabitants, not simulations
- **Character Change** — protagonist transformed by exposure to alternate realities and selves; understanding of identity, choice, or possibility altered
