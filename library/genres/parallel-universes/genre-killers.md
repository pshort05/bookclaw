*Avoid these patterns—they reduce parallel universes to action gimmick rather than exploring their philosophical potential.*

- ✗ **Inconsistent Alternate Logic** — alternate world's rules change to serve plot; technology or magic works differently when convenient
- ✗ **Alternate Self as Exposition Machine** — parallel version exists only to explain alternate world or recreate key scenes
- ✗ **No Real Divergence** — parallel worlds are identical except surface details; misses "what if" power of small changes creating large consequences
- ✗ **Parallel People as Disposable** — alternate inhabitants treated as less real than protagonist; their deaths/fates matter less than main-self's experiences
- ✗ **Action-Only Narrative** — story reduces to universe-hopping action without engaging identity/choice philosophy
- ✗ **Protagonist Always Uniquely Right** — main-self always superior to alternate selves; story never questions which version made better choices
- ✗ **Ignored Emotional Impact** — encountering alternate-loved-ones treated casually; no genuine reckoning with "who could I have been" weight
- ✗ **Convenient Universe Access** — protagonist can travel between worlds without cost or danger; travel consequence-free undermines stakes
