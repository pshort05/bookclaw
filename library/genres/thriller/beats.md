# Thriller — Beats

The default structure is the **Propulsion Engine**: open on a startling hook, then drive the
plot forward in extremely short chapters, each ending on a cliffhanger, cutting between two or
three POV threads, all under a clock. Stakes escalate every act; the protagonist is always one
step behind the threat until the final reversal. Keep the prose subservient to the plot.

- **Act 1 — The Big Pop & The Pull-In** — open with the startling event (or the clear implication one is coming). The protagonist is dragged in, usually wrongly accused, personally targeted, or the only one who sees it. The clock starts. *Reader: I have to know what this is.*
- **Act 2a — Decode & Escalate** — the protagonist and partner chase the puzzle clue by clue; each answer reveals a larger threat. Cut to the antagonist so the reader sees danger the hero can't. *Reader: it's bigger than they think.*
- **Midpoint — The Reframe** — a twist, betrayal, or revelation flips the understanding of the threat; an ally is a mole, or the real conspiracy is exposed. Stakes go personal and global at once.
- **Act 2b — The Squeeze** — institutions turn hostile, the clock tightens, a trusted resource is lost, the protagonist is most alone here. *Reader: they're cornered.*
- **Act 3 — The Set-Piece & Reversal** — converging POV threads collide at the deadline; the hero turns the antagonist's own plan or the decoded secret against them. *Reader: that's how the clues fit.*
- **Resolution — Order Restored** — the threat is stopped, the secret revealed and explained, loose threads tied; a final sting or unsolved thread can seed the next book.

## Obligatory Scenes (readers would feel cheated without these)
- **The hook** — a startling event or its imminent implication in the first chapter; the touchstone the whole book references.
- **The pull-in** — the moment the protagonist can't walk away (targeted, accused, or uniquely able to act).
- **The clock established** — the deadline or the truth-before-the-kill pressure, made explicit and felt.
- **The decode beat** — expertise applied to crack a clue, document, cipher, or pattern, advancing the plot a concrete step.
- **The villain POV** — at least one cut that hands the reader information the hero lacks (dramatic irony) or plants a red herring.
- **The midpoint reversal** — a twist or betrayal that reframes the threat and raises the stakes.
- **The all-is-lost squeeze** — allies gone, institutions hostile, the protagonist most isolated.
- **The convergent climax** — a real set-piece at the deadline where the threads collide and the protagonist outthinks or outlasts the antagonist.
- **The explanation** — the conspiracy laid bare and made to make sense; order restored.

Alternate structures that also fit: the **24-Hour Treasure Hunt** (compressed real-time clue
chain, *The Da Vinci Code*), the **Manhunt** (haunted-professional investigator closing on a
killer, *Memory Man*), and the **Espionage / Ripped-From-Headlines** present-day threat
(*The Camel Club*, Scot Harvath).
