# Thriller — Tropes

Shared vocabulary, not obligations. Pick three to six that serve your central threat.

- **The Ticking Clock** — literal (a bomb, a deadline, a 24-hour window) or figurative (a truth that must be uncovered before the kill happens); built-in, relentless time pressure.
- **The Big Pop** — a startling event in chapter one — a murder, an attack, a vanishing — that the rest of the book references and pays off.
- **The Concealed World** — a secret society, deep-state cabal, black program, or institutional cover-up the protagonist is forced to penetrate.
- **The Conspiracy** — a hidden network with reach and resources; what looks like one crime is the visible tip of something coordinated.
- **The Expert Out of Their Depth** — a specialist (academic, analyst, lawyer) yanked from the lecture hall into a manhunt.
- **The Haunted Professional** — an investigator with a defining trauma and one extraordinary skill (perfect memory, a sniper's eye, total recall).
- **The Capable Partner** — a competent ally, often the reader's stand-in, who asks the questions that unlock exposition.
- **Dramatic Irony via POV** — cut to the villain so the reader knows the danger the hero doesn't; suspense becomes "when will they figure out what I already know?".
- **The Red Herring** — a deliberately misdirected suspicion that sets up the real twist.
- **The Mole / Inside Betrayal** — the threat is partly *inside* the trusted institution.
- **The Treasure Hunt** — a chain of clues, ciphers, or locations decoded one step at a time, the plot advancing puzzle by puzzle.
- **Expository Dialogue** — complex history, tradecraft, or law delivered through characters talking, never through lecture.
- **Ripped From the Headlines** — a threat that feels plausibly like next week's news.
