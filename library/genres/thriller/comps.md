# Thriller — Comparable Titles

Touchstones and *why* they work — a source for deriving obligatory scenes and calibrating
pace. Fill with live comps during book planning's market-analysis step.

The genre splits into two poles that share an identical structural DNA (short chapters,
cliffhangers, multi-POV, simple prose, a clock) and differ only in content.

## Past-Focused: Historical / Conspiracy
- **Dan Brown, *The Da Vinci Code*** — the template treasure hunt: opens on a murder that *is* the puzzle, runs ~24 hours, decodes a religious conspiracy clue by clue; a scholar-adventurer (Robert Langdon) plus a capable partner; expository dialogue carries the history.
- **Dan Brown, *Angels & Demons* / *Inferno*** — the same engine on cryptography, art, and science; urgent-puzzle hook, cataclysmic countdown, "this part is real" framing.
- **Steve Berry, *Cotton Malone* series** — the most direct inheritor: historical intrigue plus contemporary suspense, an ex-agent turned rare-book dealer blending action with scholarship.
- **James Rollins, *Sigma Force* series** — the science variation ("scientists with guns"); a historical mystery fused with military thriller and cutting-edge science.

## Present-Focused: Political / Legal / Espionage
- **David Baldacci, *Memory Man* (Amos Decker)** — the haunted-professional manhunt: a flawed-genius hero (perfect memory) defined by trauma and one extraordinary skill; the "big pop" hook.
- **David Baldacci, *Absolute Power* / *The Camel Club*** — institutional conspiracy and government corruption; multiple POVs for tension and misdirection; the ticking clock as core device.
- **David Baldacci, Will Robie / John Puller series** — franchise heroes built around a high-concept hook (the CIA hitman with a conscience; the military investigator).
- **Brad Thor, *Scot Harvath* series** — espionage and counterterrorism "ripped from tomorrow's headlines"; fast-paced and cinematic, focused on external threats.
- **Vince Flynn (Mitch Rapp); Lee Child (Jack Reacher)** — serialized, high-concept heroes driving present-day political/crime thrillers at relentless pace.
