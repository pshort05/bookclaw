# Thriller — Themes

The ideas a thriller turns into stakes. Choose two or three to anchor the book.

- **Order from chaos** — a large, frightening problem (a global conspiracy, deep-state corruption) is made solvable; there is always an explanation and the hero finds it. This resolution *is* the genre's comfort.
- **Hidden knowledge as power** — what's concealed (a secret, a cipher, a classified truth) controls who lives and who wins; uncovering it is the whole fight.
- **Justice, power, and corruption** — institutions meant to protect are compromised from within; the protagonist's loyalty to the truth outruns their loyalty to the system.
- **The individual against the machine** — one competent person versus a conspiracy with vastly more reach, money, and force.
- **Trust and betrayal** — the ally who is a mole, the institution that sells you out; who can be trusted is itself a moving target.
- **Competence under pressure** — expertise and nerve, not luck or superpowers, are what keep the protagonist alive.
- **The cost of the truth** — exposing the secret has a price; someone pays for the resolution.
