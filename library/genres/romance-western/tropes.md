*Romance western relies on frontier settings and protagonists testing each other through survival challenges.*

- **The Stranger Comes to Town** — drifter or outsider arrives in frontier community; established locals eye stranger with suspicion; romance develops despite/through initial opposition
- **The Mail-Order Bride** — woman travels frontier to marry arranged match; relationship must develop despite strangers beginning; frontier isolation forces intimacy
- **The Rancher and the Outsider** — established frontier resident (rancher, prospector) meets person from civilization; romance navigates culture clash
- **The Protective Frontier Partner** — one protagonist protects other from frontier dangers (harsh weather, violence, predators); protection becoming romance
- **The Rivals to Partners** — competing ranchers, prospectors, or community members turn enemies into allies; romance developing through cooperation necessity
- **The Wilderness Survival** — protagonists stranded or traveling frontier wilderness together; isolation and interdependence force relationship development
- **The Outlaw and the Respectable** — romance crossing social lines; outlaw and law-abiding or criminal and innocent creating forbidden attraction
- **The Community-Building Partnership** — protagonists work together building homestead, ranch, or town; domestic partnership becoming romance
