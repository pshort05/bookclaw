*Romance western typically parallels relationship development with frontier challenges and community establishment.*

1. **Frontier Setting and Protagonist Introduction** — establish western setting authentically; introduce protagonists in their frontier contexts
2. **Protagonists Meet** — stranger arrives or circumstances force first meeting; initial attraction or antagonism established
3. **Frontier Challenge** — immediate threat or survival challenge forces cooperation; protagonists must rely on each other
4. **Developing Trust** — working together through hardship, protagonists learn each other's competence and character; attraction deepens
5. **Community or Personal Obstacle** — romance threatened by community opposition, personal secrets, or frontier challenges
6. **Deepening Commitment** — protagonists explicitly choose each other; willingness to build future together established
7. **Climactic Frontier Crisis** — major threat (weather, violence, danger) tests commitment and forces ultimate choice
8. **Resolution and Home-Building** — romance established; protagonists building life together on frontier; future in wilderness established
