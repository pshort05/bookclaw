*These elements define romance western; without them, you have contemporary romance with western setting.*

- **Authentic Frontier Setting** — research actual frontier life; hardships, resources, dangers accurately portrayed; avoid romanticizing deprivation
- **Protagonists Frontier-Competent** — at least one protagonist possesses genuine frontier skills (ranching, wilderness survival, frontier knowledge)
- **Physical Environment as Character** — landscape itself creates challenges and beauty; frontier setting isn't backdrop but active element
- **Isolation Affecting Romance** — frontier isolation forces protagonists together; limited social outlet making couple central to each other
- **Resource Scarcity Reality** — romance exists within scarcity context; protagonists don't maintain pre-frontier comfort
- **Diverse Frontier Representation** — authentic inclusion of people actually in frontier history (Indigenous peoples, Black settlers, Chinese immigrants, etc.)
- **Historical Authenticity or Clear Departure** — either research historical accuracy or establish alternate-history framework; avoid vague "western-ish" setting
- **Romance Grounded in Respect** — protagonists valuing each other's frontier competence; partnership based on mutual capability
