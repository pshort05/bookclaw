*Police procedurals typically follow an investigation arc from crime scene through resolution, with investigation complexity increasing.*

1. **Crime Discovery/Reporting** — crime committed and reported; establish scene, victim, basic circumstances; detective assignment
2. **Initial Investigation** — crime scene processing, preliminary interviews, evidence collection; establish baseline facts and initial suspects
3. **Investigation Deepens** — follow evidence trails, interview additional witnesses, discover victim background; initial theories form and test
4. **Complications Emerge** — evidence contradicts initial theory; false leads pursued; jurisdictional or bureaucratic obstacles slow progress
5. **New Evidence/Breakthrough** — forensic results, witness recanting, or new information redirects investigation; new suspect emerges
6. **Interrogation and Confrontation** — detectives interview primary suspect; psychological cat-and-mouse or straightforward confession depending on narrative
7. **Legal Process** — arrest made; evidence presented to prosecutor; legal standards must be met for prosecution; potential procedural complications
8. **Closure (Arrest/Trial Outcome)** — resolution through arrest/conviction or closure acceptance; investigation concludes with justice or acknowledgment of limitations
