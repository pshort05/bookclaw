*Avoid these patterns—they undermine procedural authenticity and investigation satisfaction.*

- ✗ **Magical Evidence** — exactly-needed clue appears without investigation work; convenient DNA match when technology shouldn't enable it; lucky breakthrough solving case
- ✗ **Ignored Procedure** — detective ignores Miranda rights, searches without warrant, or violates chain of custody without consequence; readers familiar with law notice
- ✗ **Solo Detective** — lone investigator solves crime without team, collaboration, or institutional support; actual police work is profoundly collaborative
- ✗ **Unrealistic Timeline** — case solved in days what realistically takes months/years; investigation feels rushed and unearned
- ✗ **Confession Solves Everything** — suspect spontaneously confesses explaining crime; eliminates need for evidence trail; feels cheap narratively
- ✗ **Perfect Police** — no institutional problems, no bureaucratic obstacles, no resource constraints; police work is complicated by real-world obstacles
- ✗ **Victim Insignificance** — crime is plot device rather than tragedy; victim remains faceless statistic; procedurals honor victims through thorough investigation
- ✗ **Perpetrator Incomprehensibility** — killer's motivation is inexplicable; good procedurals make perpetrator understandable even if unsympathetic
