*These elements separate police procedurals from mysteries; without them, you lose investigative satisfaction readers expect.*

- **Authentic Police Procedure** — research actual investigation methodology, evidence standards, and legal requirements; procedural shortcuts alienate informed readers
- **Team Dynamics and Collaboration** — investigations involve multiple people with different roles; show genuine teamwork, conflicts, and mutual respect
- **Clear Evidence Trail** — readers must understand what evidence points toward resolution; avoid magical leaps or unexplained investigative breakthroughs
- **Jurisdictional Reality** — understand police department structure, chain of command, resource allocation, political pressures; these affect investigation pace
- **Legal Constraints** — evidence must meet legal standards; illegally obtained evidence creates procedural problems; investigation bound by real-world law
- **Interview and Interrogation Realism** — suspect interviews follow procedural rules (Miranda rights, solicitor presence); psychological manipulation has limits
- **Diverse Representation** — modern police departments reflect demographic diversity; portrayal should match actual professional law enforcement
- **Investigation Timeline** — crimes take time to solve; multiple false leads, delays, and setbacks realistic; avoid instant solutions
