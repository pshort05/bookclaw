*Non-negotiable elements for commercial romantasy: dual-element centrality, magic systems, protagonist power, and explicit content.*

Romance and fantasy must be equally essential. The plot should collapse if you remove either element—neither is subplot. Worldbuilding should facilitate romance (courts enabling political intrigue, academies creating forced proximity, magic systems supporting emotional bonding), and romance should matter to world-resolution (love forging unlikely alliances, sacrificial bonds reshaping magic, couple's combined power saving kingdoms).

Develop original magic systems with internal logic. Rules matter—magic shouldn't appear conveniently when plot requires or violate established parameters. Tie magic to emotion or sensation (desire triggering power, bonds felt physically, magic responding to feeling) to integrate it into romantic tension. Make magic sensory: how it feels, tastes, sounds, looks.

Create powerful, capable protagonists with genuine agency. Heroines should be physically strong (warriors, riders, fighters), magically gifted, and mentally resilient. They make meaningful choices affecting outcomes—not merely reacting or awaiting rescue. Male leads should have personality, goals, and complexity beyond "dark and brooding." Both partners should have reasons to love each other beyond fate.

Include explicit sexual content appropriate for New Adult or Adult readership. Multiple intimate scenes throughout—beginning early in the narrative and continuing as relationship deepens—demonstrate physical and emotional connection. Scenes should reflect character voice and relationship dynamics; intimacy deepens trust and vulnerability.

Establish fast-paced narrative momentum with propulsive pacing. Short chapters, frequent POV switches, cliffhanger endings, and constant developments maintain forward movement. Readers should feel driven to continue; pacing should support "unputdownable" quality. Avoid meandering passages or slow sections that test patience.

Develop found family dynamics beyond central couple. Secondary relationships with authentic care, loyalty, and interdependence provide support infrastructure. These chosen-family bonds often parallel romantic intimacy, validating multiple forms of love and connection.

Deliver satisfying worldbuilding with sensory immersion. Beautiful, atmospheric settings matter—glittering courts, dangerous forests, magical academies. Describe settings sensuously; readers should experience them viscerally. Ensure settings could function as societies (political structures, cultural practices, economic systems) rather than existing purely as backdrops.

Structure for series potential while providing individual book satisfaction. Each book resolves central romantic arc (HEA or HFN) while launching continuing threads for sequels. This structure appeals to readers wanting relationship development across multiple books while providing closure within single volumes.
