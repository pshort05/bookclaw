*Follow this eight-beat progression for original romantasy with magic-centered worldbuilding and romance as central plot.*

1. **World Establishment and Status Quo** — Immerse readers in your magical world immediately. Establish magic system, key factions or courts, societal hierarchy, and immediate conflict. Introduce protagonist with clear position within this world and hint at dissatisfaction or desire for change. Plant seeds of larger conflict brewing.

2. **Meeting and Immediate Tension** — Love interest enters, disrupting status quo. Establish instant tension through opposition (enemy faction, rival magic, forbidden dynamic) or attraction complicated by circumstance. Make their connection feel destabilizing to both. Hint at larger stakes than personal romance.

3. **Forced Proximity and Escalating Interaction** — Circumstances mandate repeated interaction—academy enrollment, shared mission, magical binding, court intrigue. Tension shifts from antagonistic to charged with attraction. Include first emotionally vulnerable moment where one character reveals something genuine, fracturing pretense.

4. **Power Discovery or Identity Revelation** — Protagonist discovers hidden ability, true heritage, or magical significance. This discovery complicates her relationship (perhaps love interest was hiding knowledge, or revelation creates obligation), forces reliance on partner, or shifts power dynamics. Tie worldbuilding stakes to personal discovery.

5. **Intensifying External Conflict** — Larger fantasy plot escalates: war looms, ancient enemy awakens, political faction moves against them, magical imbalance threatens reality. Romantic relationship is tested by these external pressures. Characters must choose between personal safety and public duty; romance becomes context for difficult decisions.

6. **Physical and Emotional Intimacy** — Romantic relationship deepens through multiple intimate scenes reflecting growing trust and vulnerability. Characters share painful pasts, reveal fears, and demonstrate mutual sacrifice. Physical intimacy mirrors emotional deepening—sex becomes expression of emotional progression rather than gratuitous scene.

7. **Crisis and Betrayal** — Relationship faces genuine threat through misunderstanding, revelation of hidden truth, or external force pulling them apart. At least one character questions whether love is enough; stakes rise to catastrophic level. External conflict intersects with romantic crisis.

8. **Choice and Resolution** — Characters actively choose each other and their path forward despite consequences. Together they resolve external conflict through combined agency, magic, or sacrifice. Romantic resolution (commitment, bonding, marriage) validates their choice. Final beat establishes new reality they've built as partners, suggesting expansive future within transformed world.
