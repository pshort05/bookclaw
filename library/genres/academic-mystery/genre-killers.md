- The physical architecture itself carries narrative weight. Gothic and Victorian architecture—libraries with rare book collections, dormitories with hidden passages, gardens with dark histories—provides atmospheric depth and becomes symbolically intertwined with themes of hidden knowledge and buried secrets. The isolation of these settings (whether literal island locations like in Wilder Girls, geographic remoteness like New England colleges, or psychological isolation within dense urban institutions) amplifies the claustrophobia and forces characters into unavoidable proximity with potential murderers.[7][10][11][12][22]

- Avoid characters stating feelings directly; show emotional subtext through word choice and what's left unsaid
