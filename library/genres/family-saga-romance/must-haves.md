## Essential Checklist

- Narrative Anchors: Identifies essential elements like "The Ancestral Anchor" (estate/business) and "The Legacy Burden."
- Must shift subtly between generations to reflect changing social norms.
- The Ancestral Anchor: A house, estate, or business that acts as a central stage. It must change/decay/grow alongside the family.

- The Conditional Will: The patriarch/matriarch dies, but the inheritance is tied to a stipulation (e.g., "Must marry within a year" or "Must live under the same roof for 6 months").
- Saving the Legacy: The family business (ranch, vineyard, hotel) is failing. The hero/heroine must team up with an expert (the Love Interest) to save it.
- Sins of the Father: Generational trauma. The current generation must resolve the mistakes, debts, or feuds started by their parents.
- Forgiveness and Redemption: Old wounds (between siblings or parents) must be healed for the romantic relationship to thrive.
- The Weight of History: Characters should rarely make decisions in a vacuum. They must consider: "What would Dad think?" or "How will this affect the family reputation?"
- The Feud/Conflict: If there is a rival family or a black sheep, the tension must be palpable. Love interests are often framed as "Outsiders" who must earn their place at the table or "Enemies" who threaten the legacy.
