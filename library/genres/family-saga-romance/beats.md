- I. Tropes (Narrative Devices & Character Archetypes)
- The Matchmaking Matriarch: The grandmother or mother actively manipulates events to get her children married off.
- Character Archetypes
- The Iron Matriarch: The grandmother who holds the purse strings and the secrets. She is terrifying but ultimately acts for "the good of the family."
- III. Motifs (Recurring Imagery & Narrative Beats)
- The Name: A character's last name is a brand, a shield, and a burden.
- Theme: Trust. Is the Love Interest actually in love, or are they a corporate spy?
- Motif: Gossip. The "Ton" or society papers serve as the chorus judging the family actions.
- 4. The Magical Family Saga (e.g., Practical Magic)
- Setting as Legacy: The primary setting (The Ranch, The Vineyard, The Coastal Estate) is a character. Describe it with reverence: the age of the wood, the portraits on the walls, the land passed down for generations.
- Subtext: Conversations at the dinner table are rarely about the food. "Pass the potatoes" might actually mean "I know you're sleeping with the enemy."
- The Public vs. Private Face: Characters speak politely in front of "outsiders" but switch to fierce arguing once the doors are closed.
- Pacing & Beats
- The Weight of History: Characters should rarely make decisions in a vacuum. They must consider: "What would Dad think?" or "How will this affect the family reputation?"
