## Central Themes

- Core Identity: A sweeping narrative blending the emotional intimacy of romance with the epic scope of historical fiction across multiple generations of a single family (or interconnected families).
- The Ancestral Anchor: A house, estate, or business that acts as a central stage. It must change/decay/grow alongside the family.
- DON'T: Resolve the central mystery too early; let it ripple until the end.

- II. Themes (Central Ideas)
- Blood is Thicker: The idea that no matter how much they fight, the family unit is the ultimate loyalty.
- The Dinner Table: The central arena for conflict. Arguments, announcements, and silent wars happen over food.
- Theme: Trust. Is the Love Interest actually in love, or are they a corporate spy?
- Theme: Duty. Marrying for title vs. marrying for love.
- Theme: Otherness. The family is united because the town fears them.
- Theme: Belonging. The town knows the family history better than the family does.
