# Alternative Sports Romance — Reader Expectations

The reader's promise centers on authentic athletic storytelling in non-mainstream sports: soccer, volleyball, rugby, track and field, skiing, climbing, and other disciplines beyond the saturated hockey and football niches. The genre delivers the same high-pressure environments, physical excellence, and intense rivalries as mainstream sports romance—with fresh narrative territory.

## Tone & Mood
Athletic, compelling, and full of energy. The narrative captures the specific culture, intensity, and demands of each sport. Whether it's the relentless endurance of track and field, the team-oriented chaos of rugby, the technical precision of climbing, or the strategic beauty of soccer, the tone celebrates the sport itself. The mood is aspirational and empowering.

## Pacing
Dual-track progression: alternate competition beats with romance development. External stakes (qualifying events, championship tournaments, team dynamics, career-defining moments) mirror and amplify internal romantic stakes. The pacing propels through athletic milestones and romantic escalation.

## Setting Conventions
Authentic, specific sports environments: soccer clubs and fields, volleyball courts, rugby pitches, track facilities, ski resorts, climbing gyms. Avoid generic "sports" backdrops; ground the narrative in the particular demands and culture of that athletic discipline. Readers appreciate the authentic details that make each sport distinctive.

## Character Archetypes
- **The dedicated athlete** — highly trained, driven, physically exceptional; defined by their commitment to their sport.
- **The rival / teammate / coach** — within the sports ecosystem; someone who sees the heroine's excellence and challenges her.
- **The passionate supporter** — family, friends, or mentors who celebrate the athlete's identity.

## Heat Levels
Flexible across sweet to spicy. The romance is grounded in mutual respect and desire for an athlete's competence and drive.

## Length & Format
**70,000–100,000 words** (85,000–90,000 sweet spot). **Dual POV with alternating chapters** is preferred. Propulsive pacing. Often part of **interconnected standalones** within the same sport or athletic community, allowing marketing spend to cascade through backlist.
