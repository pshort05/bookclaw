# Workplace Romance — Comparable Titles

Touchstones for calibrating tone, pacing, and trope execution — the commercial vanguard of the
2025–2026 market.

- **The Hating Game by Sally Thorne** — the modern gold standard for enemies-to-lovers and forced proximity; flawless rivalry-to-obsession across a shared office, anchoring the competence-vs-vulnerability theme.
- **The Love Hypothesis by Ali Hazelwood** — defined the modern women-in-STEM niche; a specific academic setting (a biology department) grounds fake-dating and grumpy/sunshine while exploring real gender dynamics and funding struggles.
- **The Fine Print by Lauren Asher** — a masterclass in the modernized billionaire/boss dynamic; a theme-park empire setting and a creative sunshine subordinate whose genius is made indispensable, leveling the power imbalance.
- **Yours Truly by Abby Jimenez** — exemplary interconnected-standalone architecture in a high-stress ER; weaves real-world anxieties (chronic illness, social anxiety, class disparity) with fake-dating, using epistolary notes to build intimacy.
- **By a Thread by Lucy Score** — the grumpy-boss / plucky-subordinate dynamic with excellent pacing; a luxury lifestyle brand fuels combative banter, and the heroine's refusal to be intimidated establishes equal footing.
- **Blueprints & Iara Dreams by Mick Caswell** — a specialized-industry romance in architectural engineering; authentic male-dominated-field barriers enhance the romantic payoff and deliver professional empowerment.
- **The Ex Talk by Rachel Lynn Solomon** — fake-relationship plus professional rivals in public radio; rival producers forced to co-host as exes, hinging on hyper-specific setting detail and an escalating professional lie.
- **Beautiful Bastard by Christina Lauren** — calibration for high-heat (Level 4/5) workplace romance; leans into a boss/intern dynamic but keeps the heroine ferociously ambitious and brilliant, preventing absolute power over her.
