# Workplace Romance — Tropes

Recurring situational devices that force emotional escalation — the architectural promises made
to the reader. Pick three to six to serve your central question; using all of them yields a
bloated, unfocused manuscript.

- **The meet-cute (workplace collision)** — the leads meet in an amusing or charged way before realizing they work together (stealing a cab from the new CEO; a disastrous blind date who turns out to be the lead on a shared project).
- **Enemies to lovers (professional rivals)** — mutual disdain while competing for the same promotion, grant, or approval; the hinge is realizing the rivalry springs from shared competence and respect.
- **Grumpy × sunshine** — a clash of temperaments where the cynical, rigid lead softens only for the relentlessly warm one, while keeping their walls up to the world.
- **Forced proximity** — a shared high-stakes project, a joint business trip ("only one bed"), or a cramped office/lab strips away any ability to avoid each other.
- **Fake dating / marriage of convenience** — a fraudulent arrangement for professional or financial gain (a will clause securing a CEO seat, a date for a corporate retreat, warding off a toxic ex) where proximity turns fake feelings real.
- **The boss / employee dynamic** — a modernized hierarchy where the power imbalance is heavily mitigated by the subordinate's indispensable competence; grounded in mutual respect and explicit consent, never coercion.
- **Women in STEM / competence porn** — the FMC fights for validation and intellectual respect in a male-dominated field; the love interest champions her intellect rather than diminishing it.
- **Secret office romance** — the leads hide an escalating relationship from HR, the board, or gossiping colleagues, turning every glance and breakroom brush into concealed desire.
- **Found family** — the office as a community hub; coworkers act as surrogate siblings and emotional barriers, enriching the ensemble that fuels future standalones.
- **Second-chance romance** — a shared romantic history (exes, childhood sweethearts, a past one-night stand) reunited in the workplace, navigating baggage while keeping professional decorum.
- **The billionaire / executive savior** — a powerful executive falls for an entry-level employee; the fantasy of security paired with resources used to protect and elevate (without stripping her agency).
- **Sports / athlete and staff** — the setting expands to the athletic world: a star athlete falls for the team's PR manager, physical therapist, or analyst, combining high-adrenaline stakes with a forbidden professional line.
