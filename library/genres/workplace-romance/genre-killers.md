# Workplace Romance — Genre Killers (avoid; these make readers DNF or one-star)

The broken promises, toxic dynamics, and tonal betrayals that trigger DNF reactions and one-star
reviews.

- **Weaponizing institutional power** — the MMC using rank, wealth, or corporate authority to coerce, threaten, or trap the FMC crosses from romance into non-consensual thriller territory.
- **Stripping professional agency** — resolving her career obstacles by having the powerful MMC buy her company, fire her enemies, or secretly fund her project. She must win her own professional battles.
- **A "green-screen" setting** — a hollow workplace backdrop. If they're bio-engineers, architects, or publishers, include accurate, grounded details of that specific daily grind.
- **The "simple misunderstanding" breakup** — an Act III rupture a five-minute conversation would fix. The breakup must stem from an actual clash of career goals or deep-seated fear.
- **Unequal career sacrifice** — forcing one lead (traditionally the FMC) to permanently give up her ambitions for the other's success, unless framed as her joyful, autonomous transition into a new, equally fulfilling venture.
- **Infidelity between the leads** — cheating between the two main leads at any point severs the reader's trust in the central pairing.
- **Confusing "grumpy" with "abusive"** — a grumpy character who crosses from curmudgeonly and demanding into emotionally abusive, verbally cruel, or professionally vindictive. The grumpiness is a shield, not a weapon.
