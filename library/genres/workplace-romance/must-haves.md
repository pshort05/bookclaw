# Workplace Romance — Must-Haves (non-negotiable checklist)

Skip these and the manuscript fails the fundamental requirements of the genre. Treat as a
revision pass.

- [ ] **Establish immediate competence** — both leads demonstrate high-level competence and passion for their careers within the first three chapters; the reader must respect their work ethic.
- [ ] **Anchor the external conflict in the workplace** — a concrete job-rooted conflict (competing for one promotion, saving a failing division, finalizing a merger) that the romance specifically complicates.
- [ ] **Maintain mutual respect** — intellectual and professional respect exists at all times, even during the "enemies" phase. They can hate each other's personalities but must respect each other's skills.
- [ ] **Deliver industry-specific banter** — sharp, rapid-fire dialogue built on industry terminology, corporate absurdities, or academic rivalries.
- [ ] **Guarantee the HEA/HFN without career destruction** — a happy ending where neither lead is forced to permanently abandon their core career ambitions.
- [ ] **Force the adhesion** — an explicit event that locks the characters into unavoidable, prolonged proximity they cannot professionally escape.
- [ ] **Mitigate power imbalances with consent** — in boss/employee or billionaire tropes, require active, continuous consent; the subordinate keeps agency, boundaries, and viable exit options.
- [ ] **Earn the black moment** — a breakup logically derived from the Act I professional stakes, not a simple miscommunication a single text could solve.
