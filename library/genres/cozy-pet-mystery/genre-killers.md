Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- Do:**
-  Develop pets as characters with distinct personalities and skills.
-  Incorporate sensory details to enhance coziness (e.g., scents, sounds).
-  Build a supportive community with recurring characters.
-  Use light humor and pet antics to balance mystery tension.
-  Plant clues early and ensure they are accessible to readers.
- Don't:**
-  Make pets overly anthropomorphic or capable of unrealistic feats.
-  Include gore, excessive violence, or dark themes.
-  Rush the mystery; allow for gradual clue discovery.
-  Neglect the pet's role in the investigation.
-  Use complex forensics or technical jargon that breaks the cozy tone.
- Template for Human-Pet Interaction:**
-  Human speaks to pet, revealing thoughts or plans.
-  Pet responds through action, sound, or internal thought (if POV allowed).
-  Example: "'What do you think, Whiskers?' Sarah murmured, stroking the cat's back. Whiskers purred and nudged the old locket under the couch, his eyes gleaming with intent."
- Template for Community Dialogue:**
-  Characters engage in casual conversation, weaving in clues.
-  Example: "'I saw Mr. Benson by the bakery yesterday,' Mrs. Chen said, pouring tea. 'He seemed distracted, and his dog was sniffing around the rose bushes.' Sarah nodded, filing away the detail for later."
-  **Pitfall:** Overcomplicating the mystery with too many suspects or subplots.
- Fix:** Limit suspects to 4-5 key characters and keep subplots relevant to the main mystery or community.
-  **Pitfall:** Pets becoming passive or irrelevant.
- Fix:** Give pets active roles—e.g., finding clues, alerting to danger—and show their impact on the sleuth's decisions.
-  **Pitfall:** Inconsistent tone shifting to dark or suspenseful.
- Fix:** Maintain a light-hearted voice; use humor or pet moments to diffuse tension.
-  **Pitfall:** Clues feeling contrived or hidden from readers.
- Fix:** Integrate clues naturally through dialogue or description, and ensure they are noticeable upon reflection.
