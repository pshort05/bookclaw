- Common Tropes
- Trope: The Golden Handcuffs. The protagonist works for a corrupt firm that pays them too much to leave, effectively owning them.
- Trope: The Paper Trail. The crime is not blood; it is financial (embezzlement, toxic dumping cover-ups). The "weapon" is a memo.
- Trope: The Client is Guilty. The lawyer knows the client did it but must defend them anyway to uphold "The System."

## Rising Tropes

- Trope: Jailhouse Snitch. A witness who invents a confession to get a reduced sentence.
- Trope: Political Pressure. The DA is up for re-election and needs a conviction in a high-profile case, regardless of the truth.
- Trope: Fruit of the Poisonous Tree. The protagonist finds the perfect evidence, but it was obtained illegally and cannot be used.
- Trope: The Settlement Offer. The antagonist tries to buy the protagonist's silence. The climax is often rejecting the money to go to trial.
- Trope: Bankruptcy. The protagonist lawyer spends their own money to fund the case, risking personal financial ruin.
