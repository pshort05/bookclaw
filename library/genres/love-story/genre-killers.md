Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- Common mistakes to avoid across multiple categories
- Avoid authorial intrusion; let thoughts reflect character voice
- Use "said" and "asked" for most tags; avoid excessive adverbs (never "protested hotly," etc.)
- What characters don't discuss is equally important: avoided topics reveal secrets, trauma, fears
- Common Dialogue Mistakes to Avoid
- Sensory details: All five senses engaged; avoid purple prose clichés
- Maintain consistent heat level throughout manuscript (avoid jarring genre shifts)
- COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
- Avoid information-dumping; weave historical details through character experience
- Avoid trauma narratives as sole queer representation

- Chemistry is Non-Negotiable: Show, don't tell their connection. Use:
- The Grand Gesture / Climax: One or both protagonists realize their mistake. They must perform a decisive, emotionally honest action to win the other back. This isn't just an apology; it's a demonstration that they have grown and overcome their internal flaw.
- Show, Don't Tell Feelings: Instead of "she was sad," write "a familiar ache settled behind her ribs." Instead of "he loved her," show him doing something selfless that proves it.
- Second Chance: Built-in history, unresolved tension, overcoming past mistakes.
- Wedding date situation (ex's wedding, family event, avoiding questions)
- Parental/family expectations (proving they're dating, avoiding fix-up)
- Delayed Recognition: Fated mates don't immediately recognize connection
- Usually result of contraceptive failure or brief sexual encounter
