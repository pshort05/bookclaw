## Rising Tropes

- Enemies to Lovers remains among the most popular and enduring romance tropes, depicting protagonists who initially regard each other with antagonism, dislike, or competitive opposition but whose emotional proximity gradually transforms animosity into romantic connection. This trope works by leveraging the psychological reality that intense emotion—whether negative or positive—can channel into passionate attraction.[32][33][34]
- Friends to Lovers presents the inverse scenario in which protagonists begin with established platonic connection, often with one party secretly harboring romantic feelings while the other remains oblivious. The tension in this trope derives from the risk that acknowledging romantic feelings will destroy the valued friendship.[33][32]
- Second-Chance Romance features protagonists who have previously been in a relationship, often a failed or separated romantic partnership, who have opportunity to reunite and give their love another attempt. This trope typically involves resolution of past hurts, changed circumstances, or personal growth that makes the renewed relationship possible.[34][32]
- Workplace Romance features protagonists navigating romantic connection within professional contexts, often exploiting power differentials (boss/employee), competitive dynamics (colleagues competing for promotion), or forbidden element due to workplace policies. This trope frequently incorporates grumpy/sunshine dynamics in which one protagonist presents a stern, dismissive exterior while the other embodies warmth and optimism.[32]
- Forced Proximity creates structural situations requiring protagonists to spend extended time in intimate proximity, such as being stranded in a snowstorm, sharing a small apartment, working on a project together, or being locked in a confined space. This trope exploits the psychological reality that proximity and vulnerability facilitate emotional bonding.[15]
