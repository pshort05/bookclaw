*Follow this eight-beat structure for fairy tale retellings that honor source material while forging new paths.*

1. **Recognition of Constraint** — Open with protagonist recognizing the limitations of her situation or the curse binding her. She's trapped by the original tale's logic: imprisoned, enchanted, bound by prophecy, or defined by family role. Establish what path the fairy tale prescribes and hint at her resistance.

2. **Introduction of Disruptor** — The love interest enters and upsets the narrative equilibrium. He's often an enemy (rival faction, forbidden being, curse source), representing the inverse of what the tale predicted. Establish immediate tension—attraction complicated by conflict.

3. **Discovery of Hidden Power** — The protagonist discovers a suppressed ability, true identity, or magical heritage. This revelation doesn't complete her; instead, it complicates her path. The power frightens her, or she's unsure how to wield it responsibly. Tie this discovery to the love interest, forcing reliance or collaboration.

4. **Forced Proximity and Escalating Intimacy** — Circumstance forces continuous proximity: shared quest, magical binding, court intrigue. Romantic and emotional tension build as walls lower. Include the first intimate moment (physical or emotional vulnerability), creating no-return threshold.

5. **Revelation and Crisis** — Secrets surface—hidden identities, moral complications, betrayals. One or both protagonists must confront who they believed the other to be versus reality. The romance faces genuine threat. External conflict also intensifies (political/magical escalation).

6. **Sacrifice or Crossroads** — Each protagonist chooses to accept the other's full truth and act accordingly. One sacrifices power, position, or safety; both make active choices to remain together despite consequences. This is the emotional climax, regardless of external plot resolution.

7. **Breaking the Curse/Reclamation** — The literal or metaphorical curse breaks through combined agency, not heroine's passive suffering or rescue. She acts to reshape her destiny; the love interest assists but doesn't save. The original tale's predetermined ending fractures.

8. **New Beginning and Hard-Won HEA** — Establish the new reality they've built. Show them taking power (thrones, homes, community), making choices together, and navigating world-changes their love enabled. End with intimacy and commitment validated, suggesting expansive future.
