*Comparable titles exemplifying successful fairy tale retellings across dark, cozy, political, and mythological subgenres.*

**A Court of Thorns and Roses by Sarah J. Maas:** Beauty and the Beast retelling transplanted into fae courts with political intrigue. Established the template for modern romantasy retellings—New Adult pacing, explicit spice, heroine journey from victim to queen, morally gray love interest, fae court politics dominating the landscape.

**Plated Prisoner series by Raven Kennedy:** King Midas retelling told from the caged mistress perspective. Exemplifies dark romantasy, trauma recovery, enemies-to-lovers with genuine antagonism, and hero's journey from helplessness to power. Demonstrates trauma's realistic psychological impact across multiple books.

**Scarlett by A.C. Gaughan:** Retelling of the Scarlet Pimpernel with emphasis on the woman's agency. Shows how classic romance narratives can be reclaimed for feminist interpretations while maintaining adventure, political intrigue, and genuine romance.

**A Touch of Darkness by Scarlett St. Clair:** Hades and Persephone myth reimagined as contemporary romantasy. Demonstrates successful mythological retelling, first-person POV immediacy, explicit content, and godlike protagonists navigating immortality and power imbalances.

**Sorcery of Thorns by Margaret Rogerson:** Beauty and the Beast-adjacent with sorcery, libraries, and dangerous magic. Shows how retellings can reference familiar frameworks while building original world elements—proves you can honor source material while creating distinctiveness.

**The Cruel Prince by Holly Black:** Cinderella-inspired fae court intrigue with political cunning and enemies-to-lovers romance. Exemplifies sophisticated prose, fae court dynamics, intelligent protagonists, and moral complexity without losing narrative momentum.

**Heartless by Marissa Meyer:** The Evil Queen's origin story from Alice in Wonderland universe. Demonstrates villain-protagonist retellings, exploring how circumstance and love complicate clear moral categories. Shows sympathetic antagonist development.

**Radiance by Grace Draven:** Beauty and the Beast retelling with cultural misunderstanding and slow-burn intimacy. Exemplifies how retellings can explore themes of acceptance, physical difference as non-issue, and genuine partnership built through understanding rather than physical attraction alone.
