- The Patch-Up: The most essential trope. One character tends to the other’s wounds (removing a bullet, stitching a cut, applying antiseptic). It allows for physical intimacy, vulnerability ("I've got you"), and the inspection of scars.

## Rising Tropes

- Key Trope Variation: The shadow. The Bodyguard must watch the Client living their life, creating a sense of longing and voyeurism before they connect.
- Key Trope Variation: The Wrong Place at the Wrong Time. The protagonist witnesses a crime.
- Key Trope Variation: Rivals to Lovers. They are on opposite sides or competing agencies.
- Key Trope Variation: The Homecoming. The soldier returning from war, or the romance happening during a deployment.
- The narrative voice is typically contemporary (even in historical settings, modern voice patterns predominate) and emphasizes character interiority, showing protagonists' internal emotional conflicts alongside their external actions. This creates the distinctive tension of action romance: watching capable, competent people capable of killing also become emotionally vulnerable before the person they love.[4][7][12]
