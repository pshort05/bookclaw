# Mundane Science Fiction — Must-Haves (non-negotiable checklist)

Skip these and it isn't really mundane SF. Treat as a revision checklist.

- [ ] Plausible physics: realistic transit times (months between planets), real orbital mechanics, vacuum that kills instantly and silently, ambient ongoing radiation, felt acceleration, directional spin gravity.
- [ ] A mass-balance universe: air, water, food, propellant, and heat all come from somewhere and go somewhere — the author has done the math even if the reader never sees a spreadsheet.
- [ ] Technology with failure modes: every piece of hardware breaks plausibly (seals leak, code has bugs, panels accumulate dust, organic filters clog, bearings wear).
- [ ] Incremental extrapolation only: technologies are clear extensions of 2026 (CRISPR, mRNA, reusable rockets, language models, vertical farming, fusion research) — extrapolate aggressively but never jump disciplines.
- [ ] Plausible geopolitics: states, state-corporate partnerships, and recognizable corporations ("metanats") with contracts, liability, employment conditions, and currency flows.
- [ ] Class and labor on the page: who mines the ice, who scrubs the air filters, who raises children in low gravity.
- [ ] Demographic realism: international, multigenerational casts — children, elders, the disabled, queer people, religious minorities, every ethnic background.
- [ ] Climate as background physics: a materially altered Earth (sea-level rise, heatwaves, refugee movement, altered agriculture) present without lecturing.
- [ ] Respect for expertise: scientists, engineers, farmers, doctors, and skilled workers are genuinely knowledgeable; their competence is the engine of plot.
- [ ] Emotional realism under constraint, and a reckoning with Earth as the enduring legal, economic, and biological reference point.
- [ ] An honest ending — hopeful is allowed ("good futures that are likely"), but earned, never hand-waved.
- [ ] Landscape taken seriously: the dust, the light, the temperature gradients, and the silence of a real place (Mars, the Moon, low Earth orbit).
