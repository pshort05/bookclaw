# Mundane Science Fiction — Tropes

Shared vocabulary, not clichés. Pick three to six that serve your central question.

- **The Colonized Solar System** — settlements weeks or months apart, fragile and supply-dependent; the plot turns on the brittleness of the supply line.
- **The Fragile Habitat** — dome, cylinder, or buried hab whose life-support tolerances erode under pressure; the reader learns its systems alongside the protagonist.
- **The Generation Ship** — a closed, decaying ecology; political entropy and the moral weight of decisions made by ancestors for descendants who never consented.
- **Man vs. Vacuum** — the environment as instant, impersonal antagonist (a torn suit, a failed airlock, a micrometeorite, a flare).
- **The Resource Crunch** — a single dwindling tank (air, water, reaction mass, food, a critical spare) structures the whole novel.
- **The Competence Cascade** — narrow expertise stretched, problem by problem, into a jury-rigged solution.
- **Labor & Class Conflict in Space** — Belters vs. Inners, colonists vs. Earth corporations, a physically distinct space-born generation against the metropole.
- **Post-Climate Earth** — drowned coasts, mass migration, space framed as a pressure valve or as elites abandoning a ruined planet.
- **Telepresence at Light-Delay** — a 4–24 minute comms lag makes real-time conversation impossible; the lag itself becomes a plot device.
- **The Slow Disaster** — nothing explodes; atmospheres leak, crops fail, hulls accumulate damage — and the tension is whether anyone notices in time.
- **The Corporate Frontier** — transnational "metanats" whose contracts supersede sovereignty; your employer owns your air.
- **Realistic technology** — ion / nuclear-thermal / fusion drives, closed-loop life support, spin gravity with Coriolis, CRISPR-descendant edits, AI-as-tool, lab-grown protein.
