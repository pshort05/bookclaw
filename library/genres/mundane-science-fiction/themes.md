# Mundane Science Fiction — Themes

The ideas MSF exists to explore. Choose two or three to anchor the book.

- **Earth as the only home** — closing the FTL escape hatch forces a reckoning with the one planet we have; the stakes are environmental and humanist.
- **The ethical weight of scale** — decisions that affect thousands across decades; the moral gravity of logistics, treaties, and supply.
- **Expertise and its limits** — competence is the engine of plot, but data alone never changes minds; expertise without political allies is powerless.
- **Labor, class, and who builds the frontier** — the people who mine the ice and scrub the filters, not heroes alone.
- **Inherited commitments** — the weight of plans made on your behalf before you were born (the generation-ship question).
- **Corporate power vs. human cost** — markets that grind people to dust while every individual interaction stays polite.
- **Humanity under constraint** — grief, boredom, lust, ambition, and faith in recognizable proportion; space sanctifies no one.
