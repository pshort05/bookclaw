# Mundane Science Fiction — Reader Expectations

Grounded, plausible near-future SF set within the solar system — no FTL, aliens, time
travel, or magic. Hard-science rigor, real orbital mechanics, and the moral weight of
decisions that ripple across thousands of lives and decades. "Mundane" means *of this
world*, not boring.

## Tone & Mood
Serious, humane, and clear-eyed. Awe comes from logistics, landscape, and competence
rather than spectacle. Hope is allowed — the Manifesto asks for "good futures that are
likely" — but it is earned, never hand-waved. Catastrophism is as cheap an escape as FTL.

## Pacing
A problem-solution rhythm: a wall, a real fix that buys time, and the next problem already
germinating inside it. Tension comes from "will the fix hold, and what will it cost," not
from fresh threats arriving. Cumulative damage — resources and trust spent stay spent.

## Setting Conventions
One primary near-future setting (a Mars hab, a Lunar colony, a generation ship, a
post-climate Earth village, a Belt mining station). The habitat is a character: oxygen
budgets, CO₂ scrubbers, water loops, radiation, spin gravity. Earth remains the legal,
economic, and cultural reference point even off-world.

## Character Archetypes
Competent specialists enmeshed in institutions — not lone geniuses or chosen ones: the
Working Specialist, the Charismatic Founder, the Pragmatist Administrator, the
Second-Generation Colonist, the Earthbound Witness, the Corporate Operator, and the
AI / Ship treated as a genuinely alien form of attention.

## Length & Format
40k–60k words at this craft level (one major arc plus two or three subplots). Fits
single-POV survival, small-cast political thriller, or a compressed mosaic; documentary /
log, parallel-perspective (across comms lag), and AI-narrated forms all work. Casts are
demographically real: international, multigenerational, every background.
