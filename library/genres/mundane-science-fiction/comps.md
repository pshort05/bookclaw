# Mundane Science Fiction — Comparable Titles

Touchstones and *why* they work — a source for deriving obligatory scenes. Fill with live
comps during book planning's market-analysis step.

- **Andy Weir, *The Martian*** — the pure problem-solution survival spiral; every fix is real engineering against a ticking clock.
- **Kim Stanley Robinson, *Aurora*** — the generation ship as a decaying closed ecology; the inherited-commitments question; AI-narrated. Often called MSF's most significant novel.
- **Kim Stanley Robinson, *Red / Green / Blue Mars*** — the colonization mosaic; terraforming ethics (the Red/Green debate); metanat corporate power; the Founder/Pragmatist archetypes.
- **Geoff Ryman, *Air*** — a single village meeting a disruptive network; small in geographic scope, planetary in implication (the Earthbound-Witness form).
- **Maureen McHugh, *China Mountain Zhang*** — post-revolutionary geopolitics and near-future tech at human scale.
- **Paolo Bacigalupi, *The Windup Girl*** — climate-and-biotech dystopia on the corporate frontier.
- **Elizabeth Moon, *The Speed of Dark*** — neurodivergence and genetic modification.
- **Film / TV** — *Gattaca*, *Moon* (2009), *Children of Men*, *Her*, *Star Cops* — grounded near-future texture without spectacle.
