# Mundane Science Fiction — Genre Killers (avoid; these make readers DNF or one-star)

- **Breaking the Manifesto's contract** — FTL, warp drives or wormholes, aliens with starships, Martians/Venusians or other solar-system natives, alternate/parallel worlds, magic or the supernatural, telepathy/telekinesis/ghosts, time travel, teleportation, or interstellar radio. Use any and you've left the genre (fine — just don't market it as MSF).
- **Technology that just works** — hardware without failure modes; a world readers will not believe.
- **Magic dressed as science** — discipline-jumping hand-waves, infinite energy, free reaction mass, a cure-all nanotech.
- **Ignoring mass balance** — air, water, food, or fuel that appear from nowhere; a habitat with no oxygen budget.
- **1950s SF with better propellant** — a homogenous, all-competent, all-American crew; no class, labor, or demographic reality.
- **A fully Earth-disowning future** — off-world stories that forget Earth's legal, economic, and biological gravity (a sign the author skipped the demographics).
- **Reset stakes** — quarters or acts that forget the previous one's losses; danger with no cumulative cost.
- **Fortune-flipping twists** — pulp reversals (a betrayal, a hidden identity, a new enemy at the quarter break) that read as cheating where readers signed a plausibility contract.
- **Cheap catastrophism** — grimness as a substitute for thought; despair is as lazy an escape as FTL.
- **The psychologically invincible astronaut** — no cabin fever, grief, boredom, or breakdown across a years-long enclosure.
