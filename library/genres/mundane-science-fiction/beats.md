# Mundane Science Fiction — Beats

The default structure is the **Four-Part Hinge** (problem-solution pacing): four roughly
equal quarters, each ending on a *hinge* — a low point followed by a real, scientifically
grounded solution that buys time without saving the protagonist outright. Vary the *kind*
of wall (technical → political → technical → ethical). Each quarter inherits the previous
one's losses.

- **Q1 — Establish & Commit** — open in competence; the inciting trouble arrives and reveals its full scope; the protagonist takes inventory and commits to a first plan. *Reader: okay, we have a shot.*
- **Q2 — The First Plan Works, Imperfectly** — execute it; show the work and the narrow margins; a quiet realization reveals what the plan didn't account for. *Reader: not dead, not saved.*
- **Q3 — The Low Point** — something built fails or something unplanned arrives; the hinge costs something irrecoverable and forces the option ruled out earlier as too dangerous. *Reader: the only option left is the one we swore we wouldn't take.*
- **Q4 — Resolution with Cost** — the final, riskiest solution works, but imperfectly; something is lost; if a series, the next problem germinates in the resolution. *Reader: this was real, and the price was real.*

## Obligatory Scenes (readers would feel cheated without these)
- **Competence on display** — the protagonist doing their specialized job under normal-for-this-setting conditions.
- **The inciting failure** — a plausible system or mission failure that strips away support and names the problem.
- **Taking inventory** — an honest accounting of what they have, know, and can reach (the mass-balance moment).
- **The fix that holds (barely)** — a real application of expertise that buys time and seeds the next problem.
- **The cost that can't be undone** — depleted resources, an eroded relationship, a lost illusion.
- **The reckoning with Earth** — a beat tying the off-world story back to the planet's legal, economic, and human reality.
- **The earned ending** — survival or resolution at a real, quantifiable price; honest rather than triumphal.

Alternate structures that also fit: the Survival Spiral (three-act, *The Martian*), the
Colonization Saga mosaic (*Red Mars*), the Slow Decay (generational / AI-narrated,
*Aurora*), and the Near-Future Mosaic (*Air*).
