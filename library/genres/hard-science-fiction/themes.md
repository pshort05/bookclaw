# Hard Science Fiction — Themes

The ideas hard SF exists to explore. Choose two or three to anchor the book.

- **Hubris and the limits of control** — humanity believes it has mastered a system (fusion, aging, intelligence) and is undone by the variable it didn't model.
- **Fragility of hyper-complexity** — the more interdependent and optimized a civilization, the more catastrophically it cascades when one node fails.
- **Entropy and maintenance** — survival is not conquest but the unglamorous, unending work of keeping degrading systems running; the real heroism is upkeep.
- **The rule of consequence** — every technology reshapes society in traceable ways; the book's job is to *show* the second- and third-order effects, not just the gadget.
- **Competence as moral weight** — when expertise dictates who lives, technical decisions become the most consequential ethical and political ones in the story.
- **Knowledge as a resource** — who holds it, who preserves it, how it fragments and gets mis-applied; the loss of context is its own catastrophe.
- **Truth versus convenient belief** — fragmented data breeds competing theories; the drama is the fight to act on correct science against a seductive, unsound one.
