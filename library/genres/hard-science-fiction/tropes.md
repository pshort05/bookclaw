# Hard Science Fiction — Tropes

Shared vocabulary, not obligations. Pick three to six that serve your central scientific
question.

- **Man vs. Physics** — the antagonist is the universe's indifference; the conflict is a technical problem with a real, calculable solution.
- **The Competence Cascade** — narrow expertise, stretched problem by problem, improvising a jury-rigged solution from what's actually available.
- **Entropy as Antagonist** — the relentless decay of high-tech systems that can't be maintained with degraded resources, lost knowledge, and missing parts.
- **Knowledge Friction** — fragmented, out-of-context manuals describe tech that needed industrial infrastructure now gone; the specialist must reverse-engineer and recalculate, not just follow steps.
- **The Cascading Failure** — one small variable collapses a hyper-complex system: coolant fails → power loss → AGI offline → manufacturing halts → panic. The author traces every link.
- **The Ticking Clock** — a meltdown countdown, failing life support, or a closing window of opportunity forces decisions under pressure.
- **The Tech Roadmap with a Date** — compact fusion, genetic life extension, mature AGI, fault-tolerant QC, BCIs — each with confinement methods, fuel cycles, delivery vectors, and failure modes specified, not hand-waved.
- **Pre-Collapse Hubris** — humanity believed it had solved scarcity, then was blindsided by fixed limits and sociological constraints it ignored.
- **Scientific Debate as Drama** — characters argue the right engineering or biological solution; the technical disagreement *is* the high-stakes conflict.
- **The Misguided Technocrat** — an antagonist pursuing a scientifically unsound "fix" that will trigger a secondary catastrophe; the climax is an intellectual duel.
- **Gamified Problem-Solving** — the protagonist solves the crisis using clues and constraints planted chapters earlier; the reader decodes the puzzle alongside them.
- **The Conspiracy / Hidden Program** — a secret research effort or institutional cover-up that interlocks with the technical content, so the science and the secrecy are both load-bearing.
- **Rigorously-Extrapolated Exotica** — FTL, aliens, or AGI permitted *if* their rules are consistent and their consequences traced (the line that separates hard SF from mundane SF).
