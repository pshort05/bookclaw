- ❌ Damsel-in-Distress - Actively avoided by readers
- 🔴 AVOID THESE OPPORTUNITIES
- Recommendation: Avoid unless significantly differentiated
- Recommendation: Avoid unless subverting expectations
- Recommendation: Strongly avoid; readers demand active protagonists
- What Doesn't Work (Avoided by Successful Authors)
- ❌ Shallow worldbuilding - Settings that don't feel lived-in
- Red (Upper Left): Avoid - Generic sweet retellings, formulaic narratives, passive heroines
- Setting development guidance (avoid generic "Fairy-Tale-Landia")
- 9. Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Creates dramatic irony when characters don't understand each other's motivations
- Avoid: "Her eyes were like sapphires" or "His smile was breathtaking"
- Avoids exposition dumps; characters don't explain things one already knows
- Avoid "magic solves everything"—create drama through magic's constraints
- Avoid: Generic "Fairy-Tale-Landia" with vague medieval pastiche
- Avoid being mere props for protagonist's journey
- Avoid mechanical description (mechanical motions feel disconnected)
- Avoid didactic messaging or heavy-handed morals
- COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID
- Moral complexity present; easy answers avoided
- Spindle/Needle: Fate, industry, unavoidable destiny, pain.
- Language: Timeless, slightly archaic, but accessible. Avoid modern idioms or slang.
