Every book in this genre needs these essential elements.

## Essential Checklist

-  [ ] **Act I:** The protagonist's world is established with clear historical and social constraints.
-  [ ] **Inciting Incident:** A historical or personal event breaks the routine, linked to the wider world.
-  [ ] **First Choice:** Protagonist makes an active (if reluctant) decision to engage with the new challenge.
-  [ ] **Act II:** Protagonist enters a 'new world' (physical or social) and is tested.
-  [ ] **Mentor/Allies:** Characters provide period-specific knowledge or tools.
-  [ ] **Midpoint:** A significant success or discovery that clarifies the central conflict (personal vs. historical).
-  [ ] **The Crisis:** Protagonist must choose between their old world values and a new, harder truth. They fail or lose something dear.
-  [ ] **All is Lost:** Historical forces or enemies seem to crush their progress.
-  [ ] **Act III:** Protagonist, through introspection, synthesizes lessons and finds a new source of strength.
-  [ ] **Climax:** Takes decisive, proactive action using their unique, hard-won skills and perspective.
-  [ ] **Resolution:** Shows their new place in the world. The outcome is bittersweet, mature, and historically plausible.
