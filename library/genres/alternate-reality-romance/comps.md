## Comparable Titles

- The Anchor: The character who retains memory of the "original" reality. They carry the burden of comparison.
- Color Grading: Use distinct color palettes to signal different realities to the reader (e.g., Reality A is desaturated and cold; Reality B is golden and saturated).
- The Comparison Phase: The protagonist navigates the new world, highlighting what they miss about their old life and what is better in the new one.
- Grounding is Key: because the world is unstable, the emotions must be hyper-realistic. Focus on tactile sensations (touch, smell) to anchor the reader.
- Avoid Over-Explaining: Do not get bogged down in quantum physics or complex magic systems. The mechanism of travel matters less than the emotional consequence of the travel.
