## Rising Tropes

- The Weathered Object: A recurring man-made artifact (a sign, a toy, a coin) being slowly reclaimed by nature (rust, vines, sea-growth). Symbolizes the transience of human endeavor.
- Sub-genre & Trope Preferences
- Solarpunk: The most sought-after and growing micro-genre. Focuses on optimistic, community-driven, eco-technological futures. Not just a trope, but an aesthetic and ideological movement.

- The roots of Cli-Fi can be traced to mid-20th-century works that grappled with human-impacted environmental catastrophe, though the term 'Cli-Fi' wasn't coined until much later. J.G. Ballard's 'The Drowned World' (1962) is a seminal text, depicting a London transformed by rising temperatures and floods due to solar radiation. John Brunner's 'Stand on Zanzibar' (1968) included overpopulation and ecological stress. These early works established core tropes: transformed landscapes, societal collapse under environmental pressure, and a focus on the psychological impact of a changed world. They were often framed as dystopian or disaster fiction, but their specific anchor in climate (or anthropogenic environmental change) set the foundation.
