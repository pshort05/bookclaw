Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- | Do | Don't |
- | :--- | :--- |
- | **DO** root disasters in real climate science (e.g., wet-bulb temperatures, ocean acidification). | **DON'T** use climate change as a vague, magical apocalypse with no logical cause-effect. |
- | **DO** focus on adaptation, resilience, and moral choice. | **DON'T** rely on a single "silver bullet" tech or hero to fix everything. |
- | **DO** create conflict between different survival strategies (e.g., fortress vs. nomadic). | **DON'T** make all characters uniformly noble or villainous; everyone is compromised. |
- | **DO** let the environment interrupt and shape scenes (e.g., a negotiation halted by a heat curfew). | **DON'T** let the setting become a static painted backdrop. |
- | **DO** research the lived experience of current climate-affected communities. | **DON'T** treat climate impacts as a purely futuristic, hypothetical problem. |
- Template:** [CHARACTER'S SOCIAL/CONTEXTUAL POSITION] + [IMMEDIATE GOAL] + [UNDERLYING FEAR/BELIEF] = AUTHENTIC LINE
- Examples:**
-    **Frontline Survivor (Goal: Trade for medicine. Belief: The system is rigged.)**
- > "I've got three liters of filtered. The clinic wants five for the antibiotics. You know that's robbery. But my kid's burning up. What's your real price?"
-    **Corporate Enclave Manager (Goal: Maintain order. Belief: Efficiency is survival.)**
- > "The atmospheric scrubbers in Sector 7 are at 62% efficiency. Reallocation of breathing-mask privileges from the low-productivity cohort begins at 0800. It's not personal. It's arithmetic."
-    **Wandering Scientist (Goal: Warn. Fear: Being too late.)**
- > "Your aquifer isn't receding. It's being displaced. The data shows a massive saline front moving inland. You have months, not years. And no, your council didn't 'lose' my last report."
-    **Pitfall: Info-Dump Opening.** Starting with a textbook lecture on future history.
-    **Fix:** Start *in media res* with a character performing a mundane task in the altered world (e.g., checking air quality, calibrating a moisture trap). Let context build organically.
-    **Pitfall: Generic Dystopia.** A grim, monotonous world where everything is just broken.
-    **Fix:** Define specific, contrasting cultures that have arisen in response. Create beauty and strangeness in the adaptation (e.g., vertical gardens on ruins, new art forms using salvaged tech).
-    **Pitfall: Passive Protagonist.** A character who only observes and suffers.
-    **Fix:** Give them agency—a skill (hydrologist, engineer, storyteller), a goal (find a place, protect someone, deliver a message), and a flaw (denial, greed, hopelessness) that the crisis forces them to confront.
-    **Pitfall: Preachiness.** The story becomes a polemic, shutting down narrative nuance.
-    **Fix:** Embody ideological debates in character conflict. Give valid points (or understandable motivations) to all sides. Let the reader wrestle with the questions.
-    **Elite/Corporate:** Euphemistic, detached, systems-language. (*"We're initiating a phased population adjustment."*)
-    **Military/Security:** Tactical, resource-focused, threat-assessment. (*"That storm front changes the exfil timeline. We go now or we lose the asset."*)
-    **Scientific:** Precise, alarmed, often frustrated by political inaction. (*"The models were conservative. The permafrost melt is accelerating non-linearly."*)
-    **Frontline Worker/Survivor:** Direct, physical, experience-based. Uses local slang for phenomena. (*"The red wind's coming. Seal the vents or you'll be coughing glass for a week."*)
-    **Religious/Spiritual:** Framed through ritual, myth, karma, or punishment. (*"The sky is bleeding. We forgot the songs to soothe it."*)
