*Avoid these mistakes; they break immersion, alienate informed readers, and undermine the genre's distinctive strengths.*

- **Generic Location Masquerading as Zoo** — vague animal descriptions, inaccurate operational details, or interchangeable setting; readers familiar with zoos recognize hollow research immediately
- **Animals as Props or Deus Ex Machina** — animals used for cheap emotional beats, talking/reasoning to solve crime, or conveniently appearing to rescue protagonist; destroys credibility and feels manipulative
- **Protagonist Without Legitimate Access** — detective from outside institution investigating without credible reason or institutional knowledge; loses insider puzzle and expertise advantage
- **Procedural Inaccuracy That Breaks Immersion** — vet performing police investigation alone, keeper entering dangerous enclosure solo, or skipping established safety protocols without immediate consequence; research actual procedures
- **Oversimplified Zoo Hierarchy** — flat "zookeeper" characters without departmental differentiation; no distinction between primate keeper expertise, aquarium knowledge, or administrative perspective
- **Motive Unrelated to Zoo Peculiarity** — crime motivation (generic revenge, robbery, affair) could happen anywhere; loses the genre's essential tie between motive and institutional stakes
- **Animal Death for Emotional Shortcut** — killing animals as narrative tension-builder or protagonist motivation rather than integral plot necessity; feels exploitative and violates reader expectations
- **Ignoring Conservation/Ethics Entirely** — setting becomes pure spectacle without engaging real questions of animal welfare, captivity ethics, or institutional responsibility that give the genre substance
- **Head-Hopping POV Diluting Investigation** — frequent perspective shifts between many characters; undermines protagonist's insider observation advantage and intellectual satisfaction of following their reasoning
- **Supernatural or Magical Elements** — ghosts, telepathy, mystical animal powers; zoo mystery requires grounded, rational explanation derived from specialized knowledge
- **Cartoonish Villain Stereotype** — evil zookeeper trope or obviously bad character; credible suspects must have believable motivations and departmental complexity
