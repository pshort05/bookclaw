*Study these published works to understand zoo mystery tone, structure, and execution.*

- **"A Zeal for Zebras" by Erin E. George** — cozy mystery grounded in authentic zoo operations; protagonist with real expertise; animal behavior provides genuine clues
- **"Endangered" by Eliot Schrefer** — mystery elements intertwined with primate behavior study and conservation ethics; research-driven narrative voice
- **"The Case of the Missing Tiger" (fictional model)** — investigation hinges on specialized veterinary knowledge and keeper access; closed community suspect pool with inter-departmental conflict
- **"Gorilla My Dreams" type narratives** — protagonist's deep knowledge of specific animals central to investigation; emotional stakes tied to animal welfare rather than human melodrama
- **Jacqueline Winspey's Maisie Dobbs series (select titles)** — protagonist-driven investigation blending specialized expertise with procedural rigor; institutional setting provides constraints and access
- **Louise Penny's Inspector Gamache series (select titles)** — community-centered mystery with hierarchical investigation structure; small-town dynamics mirror zoo's closed employee ecosystem
- **Donna Andrews' "Turing Hopper" mysteries** — protagonist with genuine specialized expertise; setting (museum, tech lab, etc.) integral to plot; family/institutional dynamics provide motive and suspect pool
- **"The Poisonwood Bible" by Barbara Kingsolver** — though not a mystery, demonstrates how institutional setting (mission compound) can function as active character; research depth and ethical complexity model ambitious worldbuilding
- **Jessica Day George's "Tuesdays at the Castle"** — setting (castle) as integral character; protagonist with insider access; knowledge-driven investigation using specialized insight
- **British cozy mysteries with institutional settings** — study how Agatha Christie and contemporary British authors use closed locations (hotel, estate, ship) as investigative puzzle and suspect pool; apply these structures to zoo environment
