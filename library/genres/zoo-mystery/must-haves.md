*These elements separate zoo mysteries from general mysteries; without them, you lose the distinctive puzzle-solving satisfaction and immersive authenticity readers expect.*

- **Authentic Zoological and Operational Knowledge** — research real zoo layouts, animal care protocols, veterinary procedures, keeper responsibilities, facility hierarchy, and jargon; operational shortcuts alienate knowledgeable readers
- **Setting as Integral Plot Device** — zoo environment must enable the crime, provide clues, create hazards, or drive investigation forward; mystery cannot function in a generic location
- **Specialized Expertise as Solution** — resolution hinges on zoological, veterinary, or keeper knowledge accessible only to insiders; protagonist's credibility derives from legitimate professional expertise
- **Closed Community Dynamics** — suspect pool limited to plausibly recurring zoo personnel (staff, regular volunteers, contractors, frequent visitors); hierarchy and inter-departmental tensions provide motivation
- **Animal-Derived Evidence** — clues must come from animal behavior patterns, toxin analysis, tracks, enclosure forensics, health records, feeding logs, or animal reactions captured on security footage
- **Realistic Zoo Hierarchy and Protocols** — respect for chain of command, safety rules (two-person rule, lock-out/tag-out), incident reporting, departmental structure; violations require consequence
- **Ethical Dimension Engaged** — story engages substantively with conservation, animal welfare, captive ethics, or corporate responsibility; not mere backdrop, but genuine thematic weight
- **Animal Agency Without Anthropomorphism** — animals provide crucial observations (unusual agitation, behavioral refusal, timing markers) without talking, thinking, or solving the crime; treat them as real creatures with autonomy
- **Procedural Detail Over Spectacle** — investigation driven by mundane reality—keeper notes, maintenance schedules, feeding procedures, veterinary records—not dramatic revelation or external rescue
- **Investigation Bounded by Institutional Reality** — protagonist's investigation constrained by management pressure, police authority, public relations concerns, animal welfare priorities, and legitimate bureaucratic procedures
