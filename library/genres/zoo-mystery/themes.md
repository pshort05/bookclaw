*These underlying currents give zoo mysteries thematic depth beyond the investigative puzzle.*

- **Conservation vs. Commerce** — institutional tension between genuine animal welfare mission and profit motive, donor visibility, ticket sales, or breeding-program credit
- **Captivity Ethics** — moral complexity of confining wild animals for human benefit; research, education, and entertainment in tension with freedom and autonomy
- **Expertise and Authority** — specialized knowledge (zoology, animal behavior, veterinary science) as power; respect for professional expertise vs. bureaucratic dismissal of keeper observations
- **Institutional Vulnerability** — single scandal threatening accreditation, funding, reputation, animal welfare certifications; individual actions have organizational consequences
- **Stewardship and Responsibility** — caretaker burden; keepers bound to animals' well-being even amid human crime and institutional pressure
- **Hidden Worlds** — public spectacle masking backstage reality; visitor enchantment dependent on operational complexity and risk management most never witness
- **Community and Hierarchy** — workplace intimacy; colleagues become suspects; hierarchy (Director → Curator → Head Keeper → Junior Keeper) shapes communication and conflict
- **Nature Untamed** — underlying tension that wild animals are unpredictable; even controlled environments harbor risk; animal autonomy complicates human plans
- **Privilege and Access** — insider status granted to select few; investigations expose who controls information, who has legitimate facility access, whose observations are believed
