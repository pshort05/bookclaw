- Competence Porn: A theme borrowed from hard sci-fi but softened. The satisfaction of watching characters who are very good at their jobs (mechanics, xenobiologists, chefs) solve problems through skill and cooperation.
- Science fiction that prioritizes "slice-of-life" narratives, interpersonal relationships, and optimism over high-stakes warfare, existential dread, or hard physics. It is intimate, domestic, and focused on community rather than galactic conquest. Think Stardew Valley in space, or the works of Becky Chambers.
- Metaphors: Domestic and biological. Compare starships to homes, nebulae to blankets, and robots to pets or helpers.
- Cadence: Conversational and relaxed. Arguments are usually resolved through communication and compromise, not blasters.
- Do use: Safe, warm, cozy, companion, brew, patch, cultivate, neighbor.
- Avoid: Aggressive military jargon (unless being subverted), overly grim dystopic slang, or excessive techno-babble that alienates the reader.
- Aesthetic: Technology should feel "lived-in." Scuffed bulkheads, stickers on computer terminals, plants growing in the airlocks.
- Contrast: The juxtaposition of high-tech settings (space stations, starships) with low-tech comforts (knitting, cooking from scratch, reading paper books).
