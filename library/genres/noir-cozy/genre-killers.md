Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

-    **Excessive Whimsy:** Do not make the town *too* perfect. The quaintness should feel like a façade hiding secrets.
-    **Graphic Viscera:** Describe the *feeling* of death (cold, silent, wrong), not the biology of it.
-    **Hopelessness:** Unlike true Noir, the protagonist must not be doomed. They can be damaged, but they must be resilient.
- Dialogue in Noir Cozy is stylized. It moves fast and utilizes subtext.
-    **Banter is Armor:** Characters use wit to deflect emotion.
-    **The "Clean" Hardboiled:** Use the rhythm of 1940s detective speak but adapt the vocabulary to modern, polite society.
-    *Standard Cozy:* "Oh dear, I think he’s lying about the cookies."
-    *Noir Cozy:* "He’s selling a story about cookies, but the expiration date on his alibi passed a week ago."
-    **Interrogation Style:** Conversations with suspects should feel like verbal chess matches. The sleuth presses; the suspect deflects.
- 1.  **The Moody Opening:** Establish the atmospheric setting and the protagonist’s "ghost" (their internal struggle/past failure).
- 2.  **The Inciting Incident:** A body is found in a place of comfort (e.g., the library, the garden show). The death exposes a rift in the community.
- 3.  **The Reluctant Acceptance:** The sleuth doesn't want to get involved but is pulled in due to personal stakes or a moral code.
- 4.  **The Descent:** The investigation reveals the town's "underbelly" (e.g., the knitting circle is a front for blackmail, not drugs).
- 5.  **The False Dame/Gentleman:** A character who appears helpful/romantic is revealed to be manipulative (a Red Herring).
- 6.  **The Confrontation:** A high-tension scene (often during a storm or event) where the killer is confronted. The weapon is usually improvised and non-ballistic.
- 7.  **The Bittersweet Wrap:** The crime is solved, but the sleuth acknowledges that human nature is flawed. They return to their cozy norm, slightly wiser.
-    **Pitfall:** The story gets too depressing.
-    **Fix:** Introduce a "Cozy Anchor." A loyal pet, a comfort food scene, or a moment of genuine friendship to lighten the load.
-    **Pitfall:** The story feels like a standard cozy.
-    **Fix:** darken the internal monologue. Make the sleuth question human nature. Remove a happy coincidence and replace it with hard work.
