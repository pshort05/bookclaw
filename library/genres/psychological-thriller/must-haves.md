*These elements separate a competent thriller from a psychological one; missing any risks reader disconnect.*

- **Unreliable Narration** — protagonist's perspective must be compromised; readers should question what's real
- **Internal Tension Over External Action** — psychological conflict drives plot more than high-speed chases or shootouts
- **Slow-Burn Pacing** — claustrophobic dread that accumulates page by page, maintaining suspense through uncertainty rather than velocity
- **Psychologically Motivated Behavior** — character actions must stem from authentic trauma, mental illness, or distorted perception (not plot contrivance)
- **Intimate Settings** — homes, relationships, and close environments where danger feels personal and inescapable
- **Strategic Information Withholding** — reader discovery of truth timed for maximum impact; narrative structure controls revelation
- **Morally Ambiguous Characters** — protagonists and antagonists operate in gray areas; no simple heroes or villains
- **Psychological Authenticity** — accurate portrayal of trauma effects, mental illness, dissociation, or manipulation (consult sources; avoid stereotype)
