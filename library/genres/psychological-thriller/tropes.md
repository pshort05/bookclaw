*The genre's power comes from narrative tricks that disable trust: unreliable minds, hidden identities, and manipulative antagonists.*

- **The Unreliable Narrator** — the cornerstone device; protagonist's version distorted by alcoholism, head trauma, mental illness, amnesia, or deliberate deception
- **Domestic Betrayal** — marriages become psychological battlegrounds where partners manipulate, deceive, or plot against each other
- **The Gaslighter** — a charming, manipulative antagonist who systematically dismantles the protagonist's sanity and grip on reality
- **Multiple Perspectives/POV Shifting** — conflicting narrators, each offering different versions of events, forcing readers to synthesize contradictions
- **The Isolated Setting/Locked Room** — confined spaces (houses, islands, resorts) that heighten claustrophobia and interpersonal tension
- **Nonlinear Timeline** — intercutting "Before" and "After" a traumatic event to withhold context and generate suspense
- **Con Artist/Manipulator Protagonist** — intelligent, charming criminals or predators serving as point-of-view characters
- **The Twist or Shocking Reveal** — strategic reversals of reader assumption about character identity, motivation, or truth
