*Psychological thrillers often follow this structure, building pressure through unreliability and control rather than external action.*

1. **Establish Normal/Baseline** — introduce protagonist and their psychological state; hint at unreliability through small inconsistencies
2. **First Crack in Reality** — something doesn't add up; protagonist notices contradictions or finds evidence contradicting their understanding
3. **Gaslighting/Manipulation Intensifies** — antagonist (or protagonist's own mind) systematically dismantles protagonist's certainty about events and self
4. **Investigation/Confusion Deepens** — protagonist pursues truth but each new piece of evidence contradicts previous assumptions; reality becomes murkier
5. **Psychological Pressure Mounts** — claustrophobia and dread accumulate; protagonist increasingly isolated, doubting their own sanity
6. **Revelation of Deception** — hidden truth emerges that reframes earlier events; protagonist was wrong about core assumptions
7. **Confrontation and Reckoning** — protagonist faces antagonist (external or internal); must overcome psychological manipulation or self-delusion
8. **Resolution in Psychological Complexity** — ending may show protagonist victorious but changed, or reveals the manipulation was deeper than anticipated
