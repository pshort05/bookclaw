## Obligatory Scenes

-  [ ] **Mood:** Does the scene's sensory detail (light, sound, smell, texture) reinforce the intended emotional tone?
-  [ ] **Stakes:** Is there something concrete to be gained or lost in this scene, physically or emotionally?
-  [ ] **Subtext:** Are characters speaking around the real issue? What is left unsaid?
-  [ ] **World-Building:** Does the scene reveal one new, organic piece of lore, magic, or social custom?
-  [ ] **Character Reveal:** Does the scene force a choice or reaction that reveals a new facet of a character?
-  [ ] **Consequence:** Does the scene end in a way that changes the situation or relationship, creating a narrative hook for the next scene?
-  [ ] **Pacing:** Is the sentence length and description level appropriate for the scene's action (short for tension, longer for reflection)?
-  [ ] **The Dark Edge:** Is there a moment of slight unease, moral ambiguity, or hinted danger that aligns with the genre?
