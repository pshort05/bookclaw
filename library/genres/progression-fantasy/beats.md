*Progression fantasy typically follows protagonist's advancement journey from weakness to growing competence.*

1. **Current Status Established** — introduce protagonist's current magical level, abilities, and limitations; establish baseline weakness or disadvantage
2. **Motivation for Growth** — inciting incident demonstrating protagonist's need for advancement (threat, goal requiring power, or awakening to possibility)
3. **Training Begins** — protagonist finds mentor, enters academy, or deliberately pursues advancement through structured learning
4. **Initial Advancement** — first stage of power acquisition; protagonist learns foundational skills and experiences early success
5. **Challenge Escalates** — training intensifies; opponents become stronger; protagonist must develop new techniques to progress
6. **Plateau and Struggle** — advancement slows; protagonist hits ceiling requiring breakthrough realization or innovation
7. **Breakthrough/New Tier** — protagonist achieves qualitative advancement; previous ceiling becomes new baseline
8. **Continued Ascension** — ending shows protagonist further advanced, prepared for next phase of growth; trajectory toward mastery established
