*Progression fantasy explores mastery, discipline, and what dedication to improvement means; it's fundamentally about growth and self-transcendence.*

- **Mastery Through Dedication** — advancement requires discipline, study, practice, and perseverance; no shortcuts to genuine skill
- **Growth as Self-Definition** — protagonists define themselves through advancement trajectory; becoming stronger is identity-forming
- **Competition and Collaboration** — exploring healthy peer rivalry, mentorship, and communities built around shared advancement pursuit
- **Systemic Limitations** — understanding magical system's rules; finding loopholes, pushing boundaries, transcending perceived limitations through creativity
- **The Cost of Power** — advancement requires sacrifice (time, relationships, innocence) or carries risks (magical injury, mental strain, corruption)
- **Natural Talent vs. Hard Work** — addressing whether gift or effort matters more; exploring how dedicated practitioners surpass naturally gifted but lazy people
- **Reaching Potential** — exploring whether protagonists achieve their full potential or accept limitations; accepting that extraordinary isn't infinite
