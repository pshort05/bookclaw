*Avoid these patterns—they eliminate the progression satisfaction the genre demands.*

- ✗ **Power Gifting** — protagonist suddenly gains enormous power without training or earning advancement; removes satisfaction of earned growth
- ✗ **Trivial Opponents** — protagonist fights opponents far weaker; no genuine challenge, no growth necessity, no advancement satisfaction
- ✗ **Inconsistent Leveling** — advancement rate changes arbitrarily; sometimes protagonist advances instantly, sometimes training takes forever
- ✗ **Magic as Action Device** — protagonist uses powerful magic without understanding how/why; magic treated as plot convenience rather than system
- ✗ **Skipped Training Sequences** — "six months of training passed" dismisses narrative focus; progression fantasy must show advancement process
- ✗ **Unexplained Advancement** — protagonist gains new abilities without explanation; readers don't understand what enables this tier jump
- ✗ **Mentor as Exposition** — trainer character only delivers information; no genuine relationship, no cost, no difficulty in learning
- ✗ **Abandoned Growth Framework** — advancement system established then ignored; progression stops mattering to narrative
