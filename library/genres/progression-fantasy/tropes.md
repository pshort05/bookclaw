*Progression fantasy relies on systematic advancement and the satisfaction of measurable growth.*

- **The Weak Start** — protagonist begins with minimal magical ability, low level status, or significant disadvantage requiring diligent training
- **The Mentor/Trainer** — guide figure teaches magic system, provides training framework, or structures advancement progression
- **The Training Arc** — extended narrative devoted to skill-building, magical theory study, or deliberate advancement through challenge and practice
- **The Leveling System** — explicit framework (levels, experience points, skill trees) quantifying and tracking protagonist advancement
- **The Skill Acquisition** — protagonist deliberately learns specific magical abilities, fighting techniques, or specialized knowledge enabling advancement
- **The Competition Framework** — tournaments, trials, or ranked systems providing measurable advancement and peer competition
- **The Plateau and Breakthrough** — protagonist reaches advancement ceiling, struggles with stagnation, then discovers technique enabling next-tier growth
- **The Power Scaling** — challenges escalate proportionate to protagonist's advancing strength; opponents never trivially easy nor impossibly overwhelming
