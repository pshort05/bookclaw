*These titles exemplify progression fantasy done well, balancing systematic advancement with narrative engagement.*

**Cradle series by Will Wight:** A comprehensive progression fantasy following multiple characters through deliberate magical advancement, tournament competitions, and systematic power escalation. Wight's series exemplifies how mechanical progression can drive compelling narrative.

**Warbreaker by Brandon Sanderson:** While not purely progression-focused, Sanderson's complex magical system and protagonist's gradual mastery of Awakening demonstrates how magical learning can integrate with plot progression.

**The Poppy War by R.F. Kuang:** A military academy fantasy where protagonist discovers shamanic powers, undergoes training, and progressively masters increasingly destructive magical abilities; shows both growth satisfaction and power's moral cost.

**Slave to Sensation by Nalini Singh:** While paranormal romance, Singh's Psy characters' gradual psychological evolution and developing psychic abilities demonstrate progression-fantasy elements; systematic power development across series.

**Super Powereds by Drew Hayes:** A university series following students developing superhuman abilities through structured training; exemplifies progression fantasy's appeal of watching competent development.
