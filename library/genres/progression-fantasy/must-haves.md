*These elements define progression fantasy; without them, you have fantasy adventure rather than true progression narrative.*

- **Clear Advancement Framework** — readers must understand how power-ups/leveling works; consistent rules apply throughout
- **Measurable Protagonist Growth** — clear before-and-after comparison showing tangible advancement; readers see protagonist becoming demonstrably stronger
- **Proportional Challenges** — opponents and obstacles scale with protagonist's advancing abilities; never trivially easy nor unbeatable
- **Training Shown Not Skipped** — progression fantasy shows work of advancement, not just results; time spent learning matters narratively
- **Skill Specificity** — protagonist learns identifiable techniques, spells, or abilities; clear understanding of what each advancement enables
- **Limitation Acknowledgment** — protagonist's current ceiling established; readers understand what protagonist can't do yet and what must be learned
- **Peer Comparison** — other advancing characters provide context for protagonist's growth rate; some peers advance faster, others slower
- **Earned Power** — progression through practice, challenge, discovery; power-ups aren't gifts but results of protagonist's effort
