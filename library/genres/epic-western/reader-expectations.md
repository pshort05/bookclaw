## Setting

The golden age of western fiction extended through much of the twentieth century, with Louis L'Amour becoming perhaps the most commercially successful western author in history, with sales exceeding 300 million copies worldwide by the time of his death in 1988. The genre peaked around the early 1960s, largely due to the popularity of televised westerns such as Bonanza. However, readership began declining in the mid-to-late 1970s and reached its nadir in the 2000s, when most bookstores outside a few western American states carried only minimal western fiction selections. The contemporary period has seen a modest resurgence of interest, particularly in younger demographics, driven by television adaptations, BookTok influence, and genre blending strategies.[4][5][6][1]
