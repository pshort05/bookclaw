# genre-killers

#### Tropes Declining

1.  **The Magical Negro/Sacrificial Black Character:** The wise Black character who exists solely to aid the white protagonist's journey with supernatural wisdom or sacrifice. Now rightfully seen as racist and lazy.

2.  **The "Simple" Mentally Disabled Character as Prophet or Monster:** Using cognitive disability as a shortcut for either mystical insight or inherent savagery is offensive and outdated.

3.  **The Genteel Southern Belle Who Is Secretly a Monster:** While a classic twist, it has become predictable without significant subversion or deeper exploration of the forces that created her.

4.  **Confederate Ghosts as Noble Tragic Figures:** Portraying Confederate soldier ghosts as merely sad, lost souls, divorced from the cause they fought for, is increasingly untenable and criticized.

### Symbolic Elements

1.  **Spanish Moss:** Represents both eerie beauty and suffocation. It drapes over live oaks like a shroud, obscuring clarity, trapping moisture and memories. It's the visible, tangible weight of the past.

2.  **The Rusted Weathervane:** Symbolizes stagnation and a broken connection to nature or God. It points falsely, stuck in time, on a roof that shelters decay. It signifies a family or community that has lost its true direction.

3.  **The Bone-China Teacup:** The epitome of surface gentility. The horror occurs when this symbol of refinement is cracked, stained with poison, or used in a blasphemous ritual. It represents the fragility of civilized veneers.

4.  **The Swamp/Marsh:** A liminal space between land and water, life and death. It hides bodies, secrets, and primordial creatures. It symbolizes the subconscious, moral ambiguity, and the slow, consuming nature of the region's secrets.
