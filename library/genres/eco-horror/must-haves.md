Every book in this genre needs these essential elements.

## Essential Checklist

-  [ ] **Act I: The Violation**
-  [ ] Established the human environment and its problematic relationship with nature.
-  [ ] Introduced the "Inciting Anomaly"—first clear sign of nature's reaction.
-  [ ] Showed institutional denial or dismissal of the threat.
-  [ ] Protagonist is committed to investigating.
-  [ ] **Act II: The Unraveling**
-  [ ] Threat escalates from disturbing to directly dangerous.
-  [ ] Protagonist discovers the human cause (e.g., logs, data, confession).
-  [ ] **Midpoint:** Major set-piece where nature asserts power.
-  [ ] Attempts to fight/escape fail, highlighting the threat's nature.
-  [ ] Alliances fracture; hope diminishes.
-  [ ] **Act III: The Reckoning**
-  [ ] **Climax:** Final confrontation is about survival/understanding, not victory.
-  [ ] **Resolution:** Natural world establishes a new equilibrium. Bleak, bittersweet, or ambiguous ending.
-  [ ] **Denouement:** Final, haunting image of the changed world.
