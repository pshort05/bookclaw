*These elements separate romantic apocalypse from survival-only narratives; without them, you lose the emotional depth post-apocalyptic romance requires.*

- **Genuine Survival Stakes** — romance is secondary to authentic survival challenges; scarce resources, real threats, infrastructure failure affect relationship dynamics
- **Emotional Intimacy Grounded in Reality** — love must exist within apocalypse context, not as escape from it; survivors affected by trauma, loss, hardship
- **Reciprocal Commitment** — both protagonists choose each other actively; relationships aren't one-sided rescue narratives
- **Trust Earned Through Crisis** — romantic trust deepens through surviving shared hardship; intimacy grounded in mutual protection and sacrifice
- **Resource Economics** — love is tested by scarcity choices; protagonists' willingness to share limited resources demonstrates devotion
- **Authentic Vulnerability** — trauma, grief, and survival stress visible in protagonists; romance includes acknowledging damage catastrophe causes
- **Diverse Survival Experience** — recognition that apocalypse affects different communities differently; avoid homogeneous survival experience
- **Future-Building** — romance isn't just current passion but foundation for post-collapse future; couple imagines building life together despite hardship
