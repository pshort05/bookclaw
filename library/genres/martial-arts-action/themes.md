Core themes and ideas central to this genre.

## Central Themes

- 1.  **Kinetic Clarity:** The reader must always visualize the action. Prioritize clear spatial relationships and cause-and-effect over florid, confusing description.
- 2.  **Rhythmic Pacing:** Prose should mirror the fight's tempo. Use short, staccato sentences for rapid exchanges. Use longer, flowing sentences for setup, analysis (*jianbie*), and the culmination of techniques.
- 3.  **Sensory Immersion:** Go beyond sight. Describe the *crack* of bone, the *scent* of sweat and iron, the *taste* of blood, the *feel* of vibrating parries and shifting floorboards.
- 4.  **Internal-External Balance:** Intercut physical action with the fighter's internal state—their focus, strategy, fatigue, and emotional control (or lack thereof).
-    **Technique Naming:** Use technique names sparingly and strategically (e.g., "He unleashed the *Whirlwind Kick*."). Overuse becomes jargon. Let the description convey the move's nature.
-    **Anatomy of a Blow:** Don't just state "he punched." Describe the *setup* (weight shift, hip rotation), the *execution* (fist path, knuckle alignment), and the *impact* (sound, sensation, effect on target).
-    **Power Systems:** Define your system's rules early and be consistent. Whether it's *qi*, *ki*, internal/external styles, or pure physics, the reader needs to understand the source and limits of power.
-    **Avoid:** Purple prose during combat, vague terms like "a blur of motion," and disorienting perspective shifts mid-fight.
