Every book in this genre needs these essential elements.

## Essential Checklist

-  [ ] **Catalyzing Loss/Injustice:** Does the inciting incident create a deep, personal need (for vengeance, strength, justice)?
-  [ ] **Mentor or Manual:** Does the protagonist gain guidance, even if it's cryptic or from a discovered text?
-  [ ] **The First Threshold:** Is there a clear entry into the world of martial arts (e.g., entering a school, a first fight)?
-  [ ] **Signature Training Sequence:** Have you shown a unique, grueling, or innovative method of training that defines the hero's growth?
-  [ ] **Thematic Midpoint Defeat:** Does the hero lose a fight that challenges their core approach or morality?
-  [ ] **Innovation/Revelation:** Does the hero adapt, learn a new principle, or forge a new technique after the defeat?
-  [ ] **Final Confrontation Logic:** Does the climactic duel exploit the hero's new insight and the villain's established flaw?
-  [ ] **Cost of Victory:** Is the victory physically, emotionally, or spiritually costly?
-  [ ] **Changed Worldview:** Does the hero's understanding of "strength" or "peace" fundamentally change by the end?
