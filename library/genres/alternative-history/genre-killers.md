Avoid these mistakes that can undermine your story.

## Failure Modes

- Common pitfalls to avoid[1]
- Avoid heavy exposition dumps—reveal changes gradually
- Show, Don't Lecture: Reveal historical changes through character interaction and setting details, not encyclopedic info-dumps.
- Constraint: strictly avoid anachronistic idioms (e.g., don't use "gaslighting" in an 1800s setting).
- Pitfalls to Avoid (Negative Constraints)
- Avoid "Time Travel" Tropes: Unless this is specifically a time-travel story, characters should have no awareness of the "Real" timeline.
- Maintain a grounded, authoritative tone. Treat the alternate timeline as absolute fact. strictly avoid anachronisms in dialogue and narration. Reveal history through environmental details and context ("The Butterfly Effect"), not exposition dumps. Historical figures must act consistent with their real-world personalities, adjusted only by their new circumstances. Characters are unaware of the "real" timeline.
