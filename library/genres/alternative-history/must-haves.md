- Plausibility is King: The narrative must treat the alternate timeline as absolute reality. Logic dictates the flow of events from the Point of Divergence (POD).
- Period-Accurate Language: Dialogue and narration must match the era's vocabulary and syntax.
- Rule: Events before the POD are identical to real history. Events after must extrapolate logically.
- Social mores (gender roles, religion, class) must reflect the altered political landscape.
- Must retain their core personalities and distinct mannerisms from real history.
- Maintain a grounded, authoritative tone. Treat the alternate timeline as absolute fact. strictly avoid anachronisms in dialogue and narration. Reveal history through environmental details and context ("The Butterfly Effect"), not exposition dumps. Historical figures must act consistent with their real-world personalities, adjusted only by their new circumstances. Characters are unaware of the "real" timeline.
