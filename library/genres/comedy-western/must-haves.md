## Essential Checklist

- Strategy 2: The Incompetent Hero. The protagonist lacks traditional Western competencies (fast-draw, horsemanship, frontier wisdom) yet must navigate situations requiring precisely these skills. Fish-out-of-water narratives—an educated Easterner arriving in the West—activate this dynamic. The humor arises from the gap between expectations (set by Western conventions) and reality (a bungling, verbose protagonist).[14]
- The Paranormal Bureaucrat: A creature from folklore (vampire, witch, demon) must navigate frontier institutions—law enforcement, property ownership, social hierarchy—leading to absurd collisions between supernatural capability and institutional constraint. This trope appears frequently in paranormal Western romance and represents a sophisticated evolution of the "fish-out-of-water" concept.[10][12][18]
