# Dark Romance — Comparable Titles

Touchstones for calibrating tone, deriving obligatory scenes, and executing popular tropes.
Fill live comps during book planning's market-analysis step; these foundational texts dictate
the current commercial landscape.

- **The Maddest Obsession by Danielle Lori** — enemies-to-lovers, mafia syndicate, morally grey hero. A masterclass in antagonistic banter and grueling psychological slow-burn, with a hero who leverages syndicate power to obsessively protect a traumatized heroine while refusing to sanitize his own violence.
- **Haunting Adeline by H.D. Carlton** — stalker, "Touch Her and Die," captive/forced proximity. The definitive modern stalker text: extreme dub-con framed as a hyper-protective shield against a far darker external evil, with flawlessly executed retribution.
- **The Predator by RuNyx** — rival mafia families, enemies-to-lovers, trauma bond. Essential for equal-footing enemies: a heroine as lethal and capable as the hero, two apex predators forced to collaborate and realizing they're each other's only safe harbor.
- **Sinners Anonymous by Somme Sketcher** — age gap, forbidden romance, arranged marriage. A blueprint for pacing a slow-burn taboo relationship inside a strict mafia hierarchy, culminating in extreme "Who did this to you?" violence as catharsis.
- **God of Wrath by Rina Kent** — bully romance, good girl / bad boy, corruption arc. Exemplifies the aggressive corruption arc: the hero torments the heroine until an external threat forces him to weaponize that same obsessive energy to save her — the "villain to everyone but her" promise.
