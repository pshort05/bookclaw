# Dark Romance — Tropes

Pick three to six that serve your central psychological question. Tropes are flavor profiles,
not obligations; using all of them yields a bloated, incoherent narrative.

- **Touch Her and Die** — feral protectiveness; any threat to the heroine is met with disproportionate, lethal wrath. Thrives on restraint held under threat until the explosive payoff, proving he values her safety above his own morality.
- **Who Did This To You?** — trauma validation. The hero discovers physical or emotional evidence of her abuse and shifts instantly from cold calculation to enraged, protective vengeance. His retribution is the reader's catharsis.
- **Arranged Marriage / Contractual Obligation** — a union forged for syndicate alliances, blood-debt repayment, or peace treaties. "Untouched until the wedding day" clauses layer tension; bitter compliance turns to obsessive bond against a backdrop of mafia politics.
- **Enemies to Lovers** — genuine, lethal opposition (rival bloodlines, captor/captive), not mild irritation. Earned slowly: competence → reluctance → respect → dangerous, unavoidable attraction.
- **Forced Proximity (Captive / Safehouse)** — a locked compound, cell, or remote cabin traps the leads, stripping their masks and accelerating vulnerability and undeniable chemistry.
- **Age Gap / Extreme Power Imbalance** — disparity in age, wealth, or underworld status (a seasoned Bratva don and a younger, debt-ridden civilian). Interrogates dominance and control; the appeal is his terrifying power turned solely to elevate, protect, and worship her.
- **Stalker / Secret Admirer** — he monitors her from the shadows before introducing himself. Executed as hyper-vigilant protection against the outside world's dangers, not predatory malice — a twisted, intoxicating sense of absolute safety.
