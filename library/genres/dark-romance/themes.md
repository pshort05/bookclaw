# Dark Romance — Themes

Choose two or three to give the book a cohesive philosophical spine rather than a grab-bag of
dark events. Every scene should test these ideas.

- **Love as absolute power and complete surrender** — the dichotomy of autonomy and submission. In a violently unpredictable world, surrendering control to a dominant partner is an act of ultimate, terrifying trust; the dominant reads his possessiveness as the highest expression of love and safety.
- **The validation of trauma and the architecture of healing** — the genre's deep retentive core: scars do not render a person unlovable or broken. True intimacy means baring those scars to someone who accepts them and violently avenges the wounded.
- **The ethical weight of survival** — characters are forced into morally bankrupt choices to protect those they love. The thematic question: what atrocities are justified for survival, and when does self-preservation permanently corrupt the soul?
- **Found family vs. inherited bloodlines** — the toxic, obligatory loyalty of a corrupt crime family set against the chosen, fiercely loyal bonds of a created family or partnership. Can you truly walk away from your blood?
