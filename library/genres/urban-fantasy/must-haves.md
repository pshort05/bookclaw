# Must-Haves: Urban Fantasy

Non-negotiable elements that distinguish Urban Fantasy from other genres. Successful titles incorporate the majority of these checklist items; omitting multiple elements risks reader rejection or genre miscategorization.

## Magic System Fundamentals

- [ ] Magic operates on clear, consistent rules. Define what magic can and cannot do; limitations create conflict and prevent deus ex machina.
- [ ] Magic has defined costs (physical, emotional, temporal, spiritual, knowledge, resources). Magic without consequence eliminates tension.
- [ ] Magic is demonstrated in action with sensory detail, not explained through exposition or character dialogue.
- [ ] Magic system complexity is balanced against other worldbuilding complexity; don't overwhelm readers with simultaneous layers.

## Urban Setting Requirements

- [ ] Contemporary urban location (actual city or fictional urban center) serves as active character shaping narrative and atmosphere.
- [ ] Setting includes concrete architectural, cultural, geographical details specific to location (neighborhoods, local institutions, regional character).
- [ ] Urban infrastructure is acknowledged (smartphones, surveillance, social media, digital networks) with credible integration into paranormal worldbuilding.
- [ ] Setting description integrates into action and movement rather than halting narrative for exposition blocks.
- [ ] Physical liminal spaces exist where magical and mundane worlds intersect or supernatural community operates.

## Worldbuilding Mechanics

- [ ] The Masquerade is clearly established—whether magic is hidden, known, or operating in negotiated coexistence with human society.
- [ ] Supernatural hierarchy or power structure is defined (councils, law enforcement, family packs, species dominance patterns).
- [ ] Rules governing supernatural beings are consistent and logical (vampire invitations, fae inability to lie, werewolf pack dynamics).
- [ ] Technology and magic interaction is addressed—whether they clash, coexist, or blend.

## Character Development

- [ ] Protagonist has clear motivation for engaging with supernatural conflict; passive characters drifting into danger lack agency.
- [ ] Protagonist demonstrates distinctive voice, whether first-person or third-person limited POV.
- [ ] Protagonist displays both competence/capability and meaningful vulnerability; overpowered heroes eliminate tension.
- [ ] Secondary characters possess three-dimensional development with recognizable speech patterns, motivations, and independent agency.
- [ ] Romantic interest (if present) is complex character with secrets, agency, and legitimate perspective even when antagonistic.

## Narrative Fundamentals

- [ ] POV remains consistent within scenes (avoid head-hopping between characters' internal thoughts).
- [ ] Opening hook captures attention within first page with action, character voice, or compelling magical moment.
- [ ] Pacing balances action sequences (chases, magical confrontations, revelations) with character-driven, lower-tension scenes.
- [ ] Dialogue feels contemporary and natural, avoiding dated slang and archaic fantasy speech in modern settings.
- [ ] Prose maintains active voice during action sequences; passive voice dilutes urgency.
- [ ] Sensory details engage all five senses (sight, sound, smell, touch, taste), grounding descriptions in character perception.

## Genre Expectations

- [ ] Protagonist navigates between dual identities (mundane/magical worlds, human/paranormal, insider/outsider).
- [ ] Supernatural beings are complex characters with agency and valid motivations, not purely villainous.
- [ ] Contemporary setting is central to narrative identity; magic couldn't exist in medieval fantasy setting and maintain same resonance.
- [ ] Tone spans tonal register within contemporary setting allowing rapid shifts (casual conversation to supernatural danger in same scene).

## Series Architecture (for series installments)

- [ ] Character development spans multiple books with meaningful arc progression.
- [ ] Standalone plot resolution completes within individual books while series mysteries continue across installments.
- [ ] Secondary character arcs develop alongside protagonist's journey.
- [ ] Series structure sustains reader engagement without feeling repetitive or stagnant.
