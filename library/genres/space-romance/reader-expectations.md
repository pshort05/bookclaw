# Space Romance — Reader Expectations

The reader's promise centers on an intimate love story set against a space backdrop. The romance is the primary narrative driver; the space setting provides high-stakes external conflict (isolation, danger, survival, mission-critical objectives) that mirrors and amplifies emotional stakes.

## Tone & Mood
Intimate and emotionally grounded despite the futuristic setting. The narrative balances the vastness and isolation of space with the closeness and vulnerability of romantic connection. The mood is both expansive (endless frontier) and intimate (two people alone in the cosmos). Tension emerges from physical danger, isolation, and the urgency of survival, which forces rapid emotional escalation.

## Pacing
The dual-track progression of romance and external plot drives the narrative forward. Survival needs, mission objectives, or the constraints of isolation force characters into proximity and emotional honesty. Pacing is propulsive; every scene should balance romantic escalation with the external stakes of their situation.

## Setting Conventions
Space-based environments: starships, space stations, planetary colonies, mining outposts, research vessels, salvage operations. The setting must feel grounded and tactile; readers appreciate authentic details about life support systems, communication delays, isolation effects, and the particular hazards of the specific space scenario.

## Character Archetypes
- **The capable space professional** — an astronaut, pilot, engineer, scientist, or crew member; competent and experienced.
- **The outsider / newcomer** — arriving in the space setting, creating dynamic tension and perspective shifts.
- **The forced-proximity partner** — another crew member; isolation forces them into unavoidable intimacy.

## Heat Levels
Flexible from sweet to spicy. The romance can range from fade-to-black to explicit, depending on the emotional core.

## Length & Format
**70,000–100,000 words** (85,000–90,000 sweet spot). **Dual POV with alternating chapters** is preferred for dramatic irony and intimacy. Fast, propulsive pacing reflects high external stakes.
