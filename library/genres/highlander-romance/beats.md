## Obligatory Scenes

- Purpose**: The climactic emotional confession.
- Structure**:
-  Character breaks through their final wall.
-  Language is direct, personal, and stripped of wit. No banter here—raw truth.
-  May include Gaelic endearment for emotional emphasis.
- Example**:
- > He caught her hand, held it against his chest where his heart hammered. "I would burn every alliance I have and fight every army in Christendom for ye. Ye are my heart, *mo chridhe*. Without ye, I am nothing but a man with a sword and an empty keep."
- 
- Purpose**: Advance external conflict through group dialogue.
- Structure**:
-  Elder or advisor raises concern.
-  Hero weighs duty against personal desire.
-  Heroine observes or contributes a perspective that shifts the dynamic.
- Example**:
- > Old Dougal slammed his fist on the table. "An English bride will bring the Sassenach army to our door, Laird."
- >
- > "She is my wife," Torin said, his voice low and dangerous. "And I protect what is mine."
- >
- > From the doorway, Eleanor spoke. "Or perhaps I protect you, my lord. You forget—I know how my father thinks."
- 
- Use this checklist for every scene:
-  [ ] **Purpose**: Can I state in one sentence what this scene accomplishes for plot, character, or romance?
-  [ ] **POV**: Am I in the correct viewpoint character for maximum emotional impact?
-  [ ] **Sensory Detail**: Have I engaged at least two senses beyond sight?
-  [ ] **Setting**: Does the physical environment appear? Am I grounding the reader in place?
-  [ ] **Conflict**: Is there tension, opposition, or unresolved desire in this scene?
-  [ ] **Dialogue**: Does every line serve character, plot, or romantic tension?
-  [ ] **Emotion**: Can the reader feel what the POV character feels? Is internal emotion present?
-  [ ] **Pacing**: Does the scene length match its importance? Am I lingering where I should and moving quickly through transitions?
-  [ ] **Hook**: Does the scene end with a reason to turn the page—question, revelation, shift, or escalation?
-  [ ] **Romance Forward**: Does this scene advance the love story in some measurable way, even if subtly?
