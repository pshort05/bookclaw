Every book in this genre needs these essential elements.

## Essential Checklist

-  [ ] Heroine's ordinary world established within first 3 pages
-  [ ] Inciting incident places heroine in the Highland setting by chapter 2–3
-  [ ] Hero introduced with a strong, characteristic action (not just description)
-  [ ] Cultural clash scene within first 5 chapters
-  [ ] First charged romantic encounter (physical awareness, verbal spark)
-  [ ] Binding agreement or forced proximity established
-  [ ] Stakes clearly defined: what does each character stand to lose?
-  [ ] At least 3 intimacy-building scenes (emotional or physical)
-  [ ] Midpoint shift: relationship changes status (first love scene or emotional revelation)
-  [ ] External conflict escalates (threat, betrayal, battle)
-  [ ] Hero's vulnerability revealed to heroine (and only heroine)
-  [ ] Heroine demonstrates agency and competence in a Highland context
-  [ ] Black moment setup: misunderstanding, secret revealed, or loyalty tested
-  [ ] Black moment: couple separated or trust shattered
-  [ ] Hero makes a sacrificial choice driven by love
-  [ ] Climactic confrontation resolves external threat
-  [ ] Reunion scene with emotional declarations
-  [ ] Epilogue or coda confirms HEA
-  [ ] Loose ends tied (subplot resolution, secondary character arcs)
