*Post-apocalyptic narratives typically establish collapse, then chart survival and adaptation across escalating challenges.*

1. **The Collapse Event** — catastrophe occurs (or world already post-collapse); introduce protagonist(s) and immediate survival context
2. **Initial Survival** — protagonist learns the new rules; scavenging, navigation, threat-avoidance become primary occupation; small victories matter enormously
3. **Discovery of Threat/Challenge** — larger problem emerges (faction conflict, environmental disaster, resource scarcity, loss) requiring response beyond immediate survival
4. **Alliance/Community Formation** — protagonist encounters others; negotiates trust, establishes roles, builds functional unit despite differences
5. **Push Toward Goal** — protagonist(s) pursue objective (reaching safe haven, acquiring critical resources, defending territory, solving emerging crisis)
6. **Major Setback** — goal seems impossible; loss occurs; confidence shakes; must adapt strategy and psychologically accept new limitations
7. **Regrouping and Resolution** — protagonist(s) draw on learned skills and community bonds to overcome final obstacle; hard-won survival or transformation
8. **New Normal Established** — ending shows adjusted reality; rebuilding underway; characters' place in post-collapse society clarified
