*Without these elements, you have survival adventure but not authentic post-apocalyptic worldbuilding.*

- **Detailed Collapse Mechanics** — understand how your specific world fell; inconsistencies in scarcity, technology, or survival requirements undermine credibility
- **Authentic Survival Challenges** — research actual resource scarcity, medical complications, infrastructure failure; avoid action-movie-ified survival
- **Resource Economics** — clear understanding of what's valuable, scarce, abundant; currency systems (barter, tokens, services); value shifts inform plot
- **Worldbuilding Consistency** — if technology fails, stay consistent; if only electronics failed, show functioning mechanics; avoid convenient access to pre-collapse goods
- **Diverse Group Dynamics** — realistic depiction of how different people collaborate and conflict; avoid homogeneous communities or stereotypical role assignment
- **Psychological Adaptation** — show how characters mentally adjust to permanent loss, new dangers, changed self-concepts, and altered futures
- **Clear Survival Stakes** — readers must understand what actual danger exists (not just implied peril); specific scarcities, threats, and survival costs
- **Hope Grounded in Reality** — avoid naive optimism; balance hardship with human resilience; show both costs of survival and genuine reasons for perseverance
