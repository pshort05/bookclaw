*Post-apocalyptic fiction relies on scenarios testing human nature and forcing prioritization of authentic values over previous status.*

- **The Lone Survivor** — protagonist alone (or nearly so) navigates hostile post-collapse environment; gradually finds or creates community
- **Scavenging for Resources** — characters search ruins, abandoned sites, or hostile territories for food, medicine, weapons, or technology needed to survive
- **The Found Family** — diverse group thrown together by circumstance creates functional unit bound by survival interdependence and genuine care
- **Competing Factions/Tribes** — different groups with conflicting ideologies, resource access, or survival strategies create political/military conflict
- **The Safe Haven** — rumored or discovered community offering shelter, structure, hope; characters journey toward or defend this sanctuary
- **Knowledge as Power** — survivors with pre-collapse expertise (medical, technological, agricultural) become invaluable to communities
- **Loss and Adaptation** — protagonists lose people, capabilities, or worldviews; must psychologically adapt to permanently transformed reality
- **Infrastructure as Character** — abandoned cities, dangerous terrain, or environmental hazards become active antagonistic forces
