*These mistakes undermine post-apocalyptic immersion and reader investment in survival stakes.*

- ✗ **Inconsistent Worldbuilding** — technology/resources available when convenient; survival rules that don't apply consistently; reader notices author forgetting the collapse
- ✗ **Cheap Survival Solutions** — problems resolve through convenient access to exactly-needed resources; no actual scarcity tension
- ✗ **Unrealistic Resource Abundance** — too-easy access to food, weapons, medicine, fuel; readers who understand scarcity economics notice undermined stakes
- ✗ **Action-Movie Survival** — Hollywood-physics survival (falling from heights, extended hand-to-hand combat with no stamina cost); ignores actual human limitations
- ✗ **Villain Groups with Incoherent Motivation** — antagonists evil for evil's sake; lack clear resource conflicts or ideological differences driving opposition
- ✗ **Passive Protagonists Rescued by Luck** — constant lucky escapes; protagonist never demonstrates learned survival skill; reader feels manipulated rather than earned
- ✗ **Ignored Population Questions** — vague hand-waving about population; doesn't address what happened to 99% of humanity; worldbuilding feels half-baked
- ✗ **Pre-Collapse Comfort** — protagonists maintain pre-collapse lifestyle (hot showers, prepared foods, intact shelter) without narrative justification for access
