# Clean Romantasy — Genre Killers (avoid; these make readers DNF or one-star)

The broken promises, tonal betrayals, and structural failures that trigger DNF reactions and one-star reviews from clean romantasy readers specifically.

- **The surprise explicit scene** — an open-door sex scene appearing in a book marketed as clean or closed-door is the cardinal sin of this genre. It does not matter how well written it is. Readers in this market are actively avoiding that content; presenting it is a breach of contract that generates one-star reviews and permanent distrust.
- **Sanitizing the darkness** — treating "clean" as "morally safe" by removing genuine threat, morally ambiguous choices, meaningful violence, or complex antagonists. A world where evil is cartoonish and consequences are minimal fails the fantasy half of the contract. Clean means no explicit sex, not no edge.
- **The inert slow burn** — a story where nothing changes between the romantic leads across long stretches of narrative: no escalating intimacy milestone, no genuine revelation, no deepening of knowledge or trust. A slow burn that does not burn reads as the absence of a romance, not the restraint of one.
- **The interrupted almost-moment as a crutch** — using external interruption (a door opening, an alarm, a battle starting) to prevent the almost-moment from landing, repeatedly, without ever letting the characters make an active choice. Interruption is not restraint; it is the author avoiding the scene. The choice to stop must be made by the characters from the inside.
- **Conflating clean with chaste narration** — a first- or third-person narrator who does not register the love interest's physical presence, who has no interior desire, who experiences attraction as merely intellectual or spiritual. The reader must feel what the protagonist feels even when the depiction stops short of the explicit. Flat, affectless narration of attraction reads as failure of craft, not virtue.
- **The "clean" label applied to a completed romance arc** — a book where the couple arrives at what is clearly their full emotional and physical resolution (every barrier cleared, every declaration made, clearly domestic and established) but the physical consummation is simply not depicted. Readers recognize structural completeness and do not experience the fade-to-black as tension. The constraint must be built into the arc's structure, not bolted on at the end.
- **Unequal sacrifice on behalf of the romance** — one lead (typically the FMC) permanently abandons their mission, identity, or agency to be with the love interest. Clean romantasy readers skew toward readers who want the full romantasy emotional arc, including a protagonist who retains her own story and does not dissolve into the love interest's.
- **A world without consequence** — political alliances formed and broken without fallout, betrayals forgiven in a single scene, deaths that carry no weight in subsequent chapters. The romantic cost of the slow burn requires a world that makes those costs feel real. A consequence-free world deflates the stakes that make the romance meaningful.
- **Heat-level bait and switch in a series** — escalating the heat level from clean to spicy between book one and book two or three without signaling this shift to the reader. Series readers in the clean market commit to an author's heat-level posture; changing it mid-series reads as a betrayal of the readership that found the series specifically because it was clean.
