# Clean Romantasy — Tropes

Recurring situational devices that generate romantic tension and force emotional escalation in a fantasy setting — the architectural promises made to the reader. Pick three to six to serve your central question; using all of them yields a bloated, unfocused manuscript.

- **Fated mates / soul bond** — a magical, divine, or prophetic connection marks the leads as destined for each other before either chooses the other; the arc is about choosing freely what fate already decreed, or refusing a bond that threatens to erase individual will.
- **Enemies to lovers (across political lines)** — the leads begin on opposite sides of a war, court, or ideological divide; mutual disdain is undercut by reluctant admiration for competence, and the political stakes ensure that love is genuinely dangerous.
- **Forbidden bond** — the relationship is prohibited by law, class, magical contract, or divine decree; every moment of tenderness is an act of defiance with lethal consequences.
- **Forced proximity (quest or captivity)** — a shared quest, a treaty requiring a political prisoner, or an alliance of necessity confines the leads in close quarters across weeks of danger; intimacy builds through observation and shared survival rather than manufactured social events.
- **The reluctant protector** — a dangerous, powerful character is assigned or compelled to guard the protagonist; the dynamic shifts from obligation to genuine care when the protector finds themselves acting beyond the mission.
- **Grumpy x sunshine (across power divides)** — a severe, closed-off figure of political or magical power is disarmed by the irreverent warmth of someone who refuses to be intimidated; the humor and the tenderness coexist without softening the external stakes.
- **Hidden identity / disguise** — one lead hides their true identity (a prince among common soldiers, a spy at a foreign court, a goddess in mortal form); the romance builds on who they are beneath the mask, and the reveal tests whether love was real or constructed.
- **Political marriage of convenience** — a treaty, an alliance, or a dynastic necessity forces a betrothal; the story is the journey from obligation to genuine love, with the political stakes ensuring the arrangement cannot simply be dissolved.
- **Morally grey love interest (dark romantasy sub-segment)** — a love interest who operates outside conventional morality — a crime lord, an assassin, a tyrant's enforcer — whose specific tenderness toward the protagonist reveals the person beneath the reputation; all dark behavior stays on-page but sexuality does not.
- **Second chance / past lives** — the leads have a shared history (a severed bond, a past life memory, a betrayal in a former war) that must be relitigated before trust can be rebuilt; the reader holds the dramatic irony of knowing what the characters have yet to remember.
- **Slow-burn confession under duress** — the leads suppress their feelings across hundreds of pages of near-death danger and emotional distance; the confession arrives when circumstances make denial no longer tenable (a battle's aftermath, an expected execution, a world-ending ritual).
- **Found family as the emotional stakes** — the leads' romantic bond is embedded within a larger found-family group whose safety and survival are equally at risk; the romance is not a distraction from the world but the emotional core of the world they are fighting to preserve.
