# Clean Romantasy — Must-Haves (non-negotiable checklist)

Skip these and the manuscript fails the fundamental requirements of the genre. Treat as a revision pass.

- [ ] **Honor the heat-level contract** — every intimate scene must close the door before explicit description begins. A single anatomically explicit passage disqualifies the book for the clean romantasy market and betrays the reader's trust. Emotional heat is not bounded by this constraint; it must be maximized.
- [ ] **Build a fully realized world** — the fantasy setting must have internal logic, political stakes, and enough specific texture to feel inhabited. The romance cannot be the only thing holding the story together; the world must be worth saving.
- [ ] **Ground the romantic obstacle in the world's rules** — the reason the leads cannot simply be together must be rooted in the world's political, magical, or social systems. A manufactured personal misunderstanding is not a sufficient obstacle in a genre that can offer war, prophecy, class prohibition, and magical consequence.
- [ ] **Earn the slow burn with escalating intimacy milestones** — the almost-touch, the almost-kiss, the first acknowledgment of feeling, the first kiss, the first declaration: these must be spaced deliberately across the narrative and each must feel earned by what preceded it. Arbitrary acceleration collapses the reader's investment.
- [ ] **Make the almost-moment as charged as the spicy alternative** — the scene where consummation is possible and does not occur must be written with the same emotional precision and physical awareness as any explicit scene in a hotter genre. The reader must feel the restraint as a force, not an absence.
- [ ] **Deliver a morally and emotionally complex cast** — clean does not mean safe. The antagonist must have coherent motivation. The love interest must have a genuine darkness or flaw. The protagonist must make morally ambiguous choices. The story cannot be sanitized of human complexity.
- [ ] **Show the love interest as genuinely dangerous** — particularly in dark romantasy sub-segments, the love interest's moral history and present capability for violence or harm must be established as real and not merely decorative. The reader must feel the risk in loving this person.
- [ ] **Guarantee the HEA or clear progress toward it per book** — a series reader accepts deferred resolution, but each book must deliver a meaningful emotional milestone on the romantic arc. Ending a book at the same emotional place it began is a betrayal of the reader's investment.
- [ ] **Distinguish clean from chaste in the narrative voice** — the narrating consciousness must register physical attraction, desire, and the specific quality of the love interest's presence with full emotional honesty. The restraint is in depiction, not in the character's interior experience.
- [ ] **Signal the heat level clearly in metadata and cover copy** — readers in this market are actively seeking low-spice or closed-door content; burying that information or omitting it leads to misdirected readership, poor reviews, and reader distrust.
