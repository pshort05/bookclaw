The internet and self-publishing have democratized Sandalpunk, fueling its current growth. Social media platforms like TikTok (#BookTok), Instagram, and dedicated subreddits (r/fantasy, r/sandalpunk) allow niche audiences to connect, share fan art, and propel indie titles to visibility. The success of self-published authors in adjacent genres (e.g., 'Progression Fantasy' with Roman motifs) has created a spillover effect. Furthermore, the demand for #OwnVoices narratives and non-European settings has pushed Sandalpunk beyond Greco-Roman confines, inspiring works rooted in Mauryan India, Kushite Africa, or Mesoamerican civilizations, often published first through digital platforms like Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing.

### Current Landscape

Sandalpunk occupies a stable and growing niche within the broader speculative fiction market. It is not a mainstream bestseller powerhouse like contemporary romantasy or space opera, but it boasts a highly engaged, discerning reader base. Market dominance is seen in specific pockets: successful indie series, critically acclaimed mid-list titles from smaller presses (like Tor.com or Angry Robot), and a strong presence in tabletop gaming (e.g., the *Godsforge* card game) and video games (like *Assassin's Creed Odyssey* with its Da Vinci-esque inventions). Its popularity is rising in parallel with cultural trends seeking decolonized narratives and alternatives to traditional Tolkien-esque fantasy. The market is currently underserved by big publishing houses, creating a prime opportunity for targeted acquisitions and savvy marketing to this loyal community.
