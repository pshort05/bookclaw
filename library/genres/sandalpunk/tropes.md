# tropes

### Core Tropes

#### Tropes on the Rise

##### The Indigenous Engineer

A protagonist from a colonized or marginalized culture who masters and subverts the imperial technology, using it to defend their homeland or forge a unique, culturally-synthed path. This moves beyond the 'noble savage' trope to one of sophisticated, contextual innovation.

##### Found-Family Mechanai Crew

A crew of specialists (a smith, a philosopher, a sailor, a thief) come together to operate a complex vehicle (a submersible trireme, a walking fortress) or complete an impossible project. Focus is on diverse skills, camaraderie, and overcoming differences---a classic ensemble adventure trope refreshed for the genre.

##### The Scholarly Soldier

A warrior who is also an engineer, cartographer, or historian. They apply logic and study to warfare, preferring cunning traps and psychological strategy over brute force. This trope blends martial and intellectual prowess, appealing to the genre's core theme of Logos.

#### Stable Classics

##### The Exiled Genius

A brilliant inventor or philosopher cast out from their city-state for political or religious reasons, who finds a new patron (often a rival state or a wealthy merchant) and seeks to prove their worth---or get revenge---through their creations.

##### The Clockwork Oracle

A seemingly divine prophetic mechanism, often powered by steam, hydraulics, or intricate gears, that delivers ambiguous pronouncements. Is it a true window into fate, an elaborate fraud, or a complex calculation engine? Its mystery is a central plot device.

##### The Automaton with a Soul

A mechanical servant, soldier, or artwork (a bronze bull, a silver songbird) that displays signs of consciousness, emotion, or free will. This trope directly explores themes of personhood, slavery, and the nature of life.
