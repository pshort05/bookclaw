Readers expect a fantasy world rendered in a warm, intimate register where the central pleasures are community, comfort, small magic, and everyday life rather than world-ending stakes or violent conflict — the antithesis of epic fantasy's scale.

## Tone & Mood

The tone is gentle, warm, and reassuring. Tension exists — a problem must be solved, a relationship must be mended, a community must be supported — but it never tips into genuine dread or despair. Unlike standard fantasy, which uses the fantastic to raise stakes and threaten the world, cozy fantasy uses it to make the world more beautiful, habitable, and strange in pleasing ways. Magic should feel domestic and sensory: the smell of a potion, the warmth of an enchanted hearth, the softness of a familiar. The reader should finish each chapter feeling settled, not wound up.

## Pacing

Pacing is deliberate and unhurried. The narrative pauses for meals, for crafting, for conversations by the fire, for the small rituals of daily life — and these pauses are not interruptions but the point. Plot advances, but the journey matters more than the destination. Conflict is real but resolves without catastrophe; deaths, if they occur, are handled gently and off-page or in the past. What distinguishes cozy fantasy from standard low-stakes fantasy is that the slow pace is a feature, not a flaw — readers are here for the atmosphere.

## Setting Conventions

Settings favor the intimate and the contained: a bakery in a magical city, a small herbalist's shop, a village at the edge of an enchanted wood, a cozy inn on a well-traveled road. The world beyond the immediate community may be large and interesting, but the story does not travel to it; the scope is local. Sensory comfort details are essential — warmth, light, food, texture — and should be specific rather than generic. The fantasy world should feel fully realized but not encyclopedic; worldbuilding serves atmosphere, not lore accumulation.

## Character Archetypes

The Found Family: a group of diverse, complementary characters who have chosen each other and whose bonds are the emotional core of the narrative. The Retired Adventurer: someone with a complicated or dangerous past who has deliberately stepped back into quiet life — their expertise is present but their sword is hung on the wall. The Gentle Magical Creature: a familiar, a small being, or a magical neighbor whose presence signals the world's warmth. The Gruff Protector With a Soft Interior: a character who appears prickly but is revealed to be deeply caring. Character relationships drive plot; external antagonists are uncommon.

## Length & Format

60,000–90,000 words is the typical range. Shorter novels and novellas (30,000–50,000 words) are common and well-received in the genre. Series are popular, with each installment focused on a new problem within the same community. First-person or close third-person point-of-view sustains the intimate register. Chapter breaks often coincide with meals or the end of a task, reinforcing the domestic rhythm.
