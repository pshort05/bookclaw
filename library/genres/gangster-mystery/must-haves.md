Every book in this genre needs these essential elements.

## Essential Checklist

-  [ ] **Inciting Incident**: A crime/disruption *within* the mob world is discovered.
-  [ ] **Investigator Hook**: Why must *this* character solve it? (Duty, debt, survival).
-  [ ] **First Foray**: Investigator's first tense contact with the organization.
-  [ ] **Clue #1**: A tangible lead tied to mob business (money, a ritual, a location).
-  [ ] **Red Herring**: A false lead born from the organization's lies or internal strife.
-  [ ] **Rising Action**: Investigation prompts retaliation (warning, vandalism, minor violence).
-  [ ] **Midpoint Reversal**: A major setback (death of contact, loss of evidence, frame job).
-  [ ] **Isolation**: Investigator's allies/support systems are stripped away.
-  [ ] **Dark Night**: Investigator confronts failure and the apparent power of the organization.
-  [ ] **Breakthrough**: A hidden detail, overlooked due to arrogance or protocol, reveals the path.
-  [ ] **Confrontation**: A high-stakes verbal showdown, often with multiple power players present.
-  [ ] **Revelation**: The truth is laid bare, exposing motive rooted in the mob world (greed, revenge for a slight, power grab).
-  [ ] **Aftermath**: Show the cost. Who lived? Who died? What's left of the organization? What's left of the investigator?
