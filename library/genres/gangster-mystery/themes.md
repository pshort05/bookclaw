Core themes and ideas central to this genre.

## Central Themes

- 1. **Subtext is King**: Characters rarely say exactly what they mean. Listen to what's *not* said. A compliment can be a threat. A question can be an accusation.
- 2. **Economy**: Gangster dialogue is rarely verbose. It's terse, loaded, and purposeful.
- 3. **Code & Ritual**: Dialogue follows the organization's unwritten rules: respect for hierarchy, use of titles ("Don," "Boss," "Mr."), and coded language for illegal activities.
- 4. **Credibility**: Dialogue must sound authentic to the character's background, era, and role. A seasoned capo speaks differently than a street-level enforcer or a federal agent.
-  **Veiled Threats**: "It'd be a real shame if something happened to that nice bakery of yours."
-  **Interrogation as Conversation**: Questions are indirect. Instead of "Did you kill him?" try "The Boss was disappointed Johnny wasn't at the meet. Real disappointed."
-  **Negotiation**: Every conversation is a negotiation of power, loyalty, or price.
-  **Betrayal Signals**: Sudden formality, avoidance of eye contact, overly elaborate explanations.
-  **Jargon**: Use period- and role-appropriate slang sparingly for flavor, not overwhelm. (e.g., "whack," "sit-down," "vig," "made man," "canary").
