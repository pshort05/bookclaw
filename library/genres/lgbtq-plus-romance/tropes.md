# LGBTQ+ Romance — Tropes

- **Coming out together** — two people navigating their identities and attraction simultaneously; the relationship and self-discovery are intertwined.
- **First love / self-discovery** — a character's first queer relationship coinciding with their identity awakening.
- **Grumpy × sunshine (with queer specificity)** — one partner closeted and cynical, the other out and joyful; attraction and the journey toward authenticity.
- **Forced proximity (same-sex workplace, LGBTQ+ community event, etc.)** — environment-specific tropes that naturally arise in queer settings.
- **Secret romance** — partners hiding their relationship from a hostile environment (family, workplace, cultural context, historical period); high-stakes emotional tension.
- **Found family** — LGBTQ+ characters bonded through chosen family; romantic bond deepens within a community of chosen siblings.
- **Reunited exes (navigating changed identities)** — former lovers reconnecting after one or both have come out or transitioned; history + new selves.
- **Across-the-divide romance** — characters from different LGBTQ+ communities, political alignments, or identity spectrums; connection despite difference.
- **Trans & non-binary love stories** — romance where gender identity (transition, non-binary identity, gender-nonconformity) is central to the characters and plot.
- **Asexual / ace spectrum romance** — intimacy and love expressed outside or alongside sexual attraction.
