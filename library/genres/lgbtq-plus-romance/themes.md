# LGBTQ+ Romance — Themes

- **Authenticity & the courage to be seen** — the profound joy and terror of being fully known by a lover and a community.
- **Chosen family & belonging** — love as a form of chosen kinship and community within or outside biological family.
- **Systemic barriers & resilience** — navigating homophobia, transphobia, discrimination, and marginalization without minimizing or exploiting trauma.
- **Intersectionality** — identities informed by multiple factors (race, class, disability, immigration status) that shape both queerness and romance.
- **Reclaiming joy & pleasure** — celebrating queer joy, sexuality, and love as inherently valuable and not contingent on suffering.
- **Self-acceptance & self-love** — a character's journey toward accepting their identity; the romantic partner witnessing and celebrating that journey.
- **Community & solidarity** — queer characters supporting each other; the broader LGBTQ+ community as backdrop and emotional anchor.
- **Transgression & freedom** — love as an act of freedom, rebellion, or reclamation in contexts where queerness is constrained.
