# LGBTQ+ Romance — Reader Expectations

The reader's promise centers on an authentic love story between LGBTQ+ protagonists. This genre encompasses any romance subgenre (contemporary, fantasy, paranormal, historical) with the explicit requirement that the central characters are queer-identified and that the narrative treats their identity with respect, nuance, and celebration rather than as a plot complication.

## Tone & Mood
Authentic, joyful, and emotionally grounded. The narrative can range from humorous to intensely emotional, depending on the specific subgenre, but the tone must honor the lived experience of LGBTQ+ characters. The mood may include the particular joy of finding love as a marginalized person, the relief of authenticity, and the depth of connection between characters navigating similar or intersecting experiences.

## Pacing
Variable depending on subgenre (contemporary rom-com may be fast and witty; historical may be slower and more atmospheric). What remains consistent is that the queer identity and the romance are equally central; neither should be treated as secondary or apologetic.

## Setting Conventions
Any setting authentic to the subgenre; the setting should reflect the specific historical moment, cultural context, or fantasy world as it relates to LGBTQ+ experiences. Contemporary settings may include LGBTQ+ communities, workplaces, social groups. Historical settings may navigate the constraints and dangers faced by queer people in past eras. Fantasy/paranormal settings may explore how queerness exists within magical or supernatural worldbuilding.

## Character Archetypes
- **The out & proud character** — comfortable with their identity; may serve as an anchor or guide for a less-out partner.
- **The closeted or questioning character** — wrestling with identity, internalized shame, fear of rejection, or uncertainty.
- **The community member** — defined partly by their role in LGBTQ+ social structures, activism, or cultural groups.
- **The intersectional protagonist** — identity informed by race, disability, class, immigration status, or other factors beyond sexual/gender identity.

## Heat Levels
Flexible from clean to explicit. LGBTQ+ readers have diverse preferences; the heat level should be clearly signaled in marketing but never apologized for or treated as less legitimate than heterosexual romance heat.

## Length & Format
**70,000–100,000 words** (85,000–90,000 sweet spot for contemporary; historical/fantasy may run longer). **Dual POV with alternating chapters** is strongly preferred so both characters' inner lives and identities are fully realized. Present tense or past tense both work depending on subgenre.
