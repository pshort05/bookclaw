# LGBTQ+ Romance — Must-Haves

- [ ] **Center LGBTQ+ identity respectfully.** The characters' queer identity is integral to the story, not incidental. Treat identity with nuance; avoid stereotypes or caricature.
- [ ] **Deliver authentic representation.** Research lived experience of the specific identities in your story; sensitivity readers from those communities are invaluable.
- [ ] **Honor both characters' inner lives equally.** If dual POV, both characters should have similar narrative weight and interiority; avoid centering one character's journey over the other's.
- [ ] **Avoid redemption narratives for the love interest.** Don't frame one character as "saving" or "converting" the other; celebrate mutual choice and authentic connection.
- [ ] **Guarantee the HEA/HFN without erasing identity.** The happy ending includes authentic lives as LGBTQ+ people, not assimilation or hiding.
- [ ] **Respect the spectrum.** LGBTQ+ encompasses many identities and expressions; avoid collapsing all queerness into a single experience.
- [ ] **Navigate obstacles authentically.** If facing discrimination or family rejection, treat these as real obstacles that are earned through the narrative, not melodrama.
