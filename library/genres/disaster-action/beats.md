Obligatory scenes and key beats drive this genre forward.

## Obligatory Scenes

- 1. **The Warning (Ignored):** An expert or sign hints at the coming catastrophe.
- 2. **The First Blow:** The disaster hits with full, shocking force.
- 3. **The Narrow Escape:** Protagonist barely survives the initial impact.
- 4. **The False Safe Haven:** A location seems secure but is soon threatened.
- 5. **The Loss:** A significant character death or failure that raises the stakes.
- 6. **The Choice:** Protagonist must choose between personal safety and a greater good.
- 7. **The Last Stand:** Facing the disaster's peak with ingenuity and courage.
- 8. **The Cost:** Assessing the damage, both physical and emotional.
-  [ ] **Goal:** Does the POV character have a clear, immediate objective (e.g., find water, bandage wound, escape room)?
-  [ ] **Disaster Pressure:** Is the disaster actively threatening (aftershocks, rising water, spreading fire, looters)?
-  [ ] **Sensory Detail:** Have I used at least 3 of the 5 senses?
-  [ ] **Stakes:** What happens if the character fails this immediate goal? Is it clear?
-  [ ] **Change:** Does the scene end in a different state than it began (goal achieved/failed, new problem discovered, relationship altered)?
-  [ ] **Pacing:** Are sentences and paragraphs shorter during action? Is there a mix of action beats and brief reaction moments?
-  [ ] **Dialogue Utility:** Is every line of dialogue necessary (conveying info, revealing character, advancing action)?
