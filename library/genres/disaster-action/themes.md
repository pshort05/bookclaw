## Central Themes

-  **Utility Over Wit:** Dialogue is functional. It conveys vital information, coordinates action, reveals character under stress, and expresses primal fear, determination, or despair.
-  **Interrupted & Overlapping:** Conversations are fractured by crashing sounds, tremors, and urgent events. Use em-dashes and ellipses to denote interruptions and trailing off.
-  **Jargon as Characterization:** Experts use precise, clipped jargon ("Aftershock cluster, magnitude 5.8, bearing 2-4-0."). Laypeople misunderstand or simplify it ("More quakes coming!")
-  **Pre-Disaster:** Normal, establishing relationships. Can be mundane, warm, or tense, setting up what will be lost or tested.
-  **During Impact:** Terse shouts, screams, pure imperative commands ("Down!" "Hold on!" "Move!").
-  **Immediate Aftermath:** Gasped questions, reassurances, expressions of shock and pain. Dialect and education levels may temporarily break down.
-  **Sustained Survival:** Dialogue becomes strategic, planning-focused. Conflicts flare over resources and leadership. Moments of vulnerability surface in quiet moments.
- Act I: The Fault Line (Setup ~25%)**
-  Introduce protagonist(s) in their normal world, showcasing their skills, flaws, and key relationships.
-  Foreshadow the disaster through subtle signs (news reports of strange weather, minor tremors, a scientist's ignored warning).
-  Establish the physical and social setting that will be destroyed.
-  **The Inciting Incident:** The first, unmistakable strike of the disaster. This is the point of no return.
- Act II: The Collapse (Confrontation ~50%)**
-  **Part 1: Immediate Survival:** Chaos reigns. The goal is simple: don't die. Protagonists react instinctively, often separated from loved ones.
-  **Midpoint:** A major setback or revelation deepens the crisis (a dam fails, the virus mutates, the "safe zone" is compromised). The goal shifts from passive survival to active objective (rescue someone, reach safety, find a cure).
-  **Part 2: The Long Haul:** The initial adrenaline fades, replaced by exhaustion and grim resolve. Antagonists may emerge (desperate looters, corrupt officials, the disaster itself). Protagonists are tested morally and physically.
- Act III: The Reckoning (Resolution ~25%)**
-  **Climax:** A final, desperate push to achieve the story goal against the disaster's last, worst wave (the eye of the hurricane, the final aftershock, the core meltdown). This often involves a sacrificial choice or a brilliant, risky application of the protagonist's skill.
-  **Aftermath:** The immediate danger passes. Focus on the cost: who and what was lost. A brief moment of relief or mourning.
-  **New Normal:** A glimpse of the future, however bleak or hopeful. The survivors are forever changed. The resolution is about enduring, not restoring.
