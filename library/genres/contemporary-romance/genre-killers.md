# Contemporary Romance — Genre Killers (avoid; these make readers DNF or one-star)

- **No HEA/HFN.** A tragic, bittersweet, or ambiguous romantic ending breaks the core promise. This is the one unforgivable sin.
- **The romance as an afterthought** — leads kept apart for most of the book, the LI introduced late (after ~20%), or chemistry that never lands.
- **A deus-ex-machina ending** — the conflict resolved by police, fate, an inheritance, or the villain's convenient death instead of the leads' own growth and choices.
- **The dark moment built on a dumb misunderstanding** — a third-act breakup that one honest sentence would have prevented reads as manufactured and infuriates readers.
- **Cheating by a lead** without a fully earned redemption — most readers will not forgive it, and the "cheated-on partner takes them back" beat is a known turn-off.
- **A controlling "alpha" sold as romantic** — tracking, isolating, deciding "for her own good," using money/position as leverage. Modern readers read this as the antagonist, not the hero.
- **Insta-love with no earned development**, or attraction that is purely physical with no personality connection.
- **A passive lead** with no agency over their own arc, choices, or partner — or a damsel waiting to be rescued.
- **Bait-and-switch heat** — promising spice and delivering none, or sweet-marketed and then explicit. Honor the cover.
- **Reader-fatigued tropes played straight** — surprise/secret baby out of nowhere, an unresolved love triangle, the big misunderstanding as the entire engine.
- **A rushed resolution** crammed into the final 5% — the emotional payoff readers waited the whole book for, denied.
