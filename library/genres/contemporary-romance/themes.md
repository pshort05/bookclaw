# Contemporary Romance — Themes

The ideas a romance explores beneath the love story. Choose two or three to give the book
a spine; the relationship dramatizes the theme rather than pausing to discuss it.

- **Love as transformation** — both partners must grow and sacrifice for the relationship; mutuality is the engine, not one person fixing the other.
- **Vulnerability and trust** — intimacy is the willingness to be seen; the romance is the slow lowering of a guard.
- **Self-worth and being chosen** — a lead learns they deserve love as they are; the partner sees what they couldn't see in themselves.
- **Partnership over power** — healthy love is *power-with*, not *power-over*; respect for autonomy is what makes a lead swoon-worthy instead of controlling.
- **Belonging and found family** — the friend group, the small town, the crew that becomes home; love widens a life, not narrows it.
- **The past vs. the present** — old wounds, exes, and history must be reckoned with before the new bond can hold (especially second-chance and friends-to-lovers).
- **Independence and interdependence** — keeping a self while building a "we"; love that supports goals instead of swallowing them.
- **Risk and the leap** — choosing love means choosing the possibility of loss; the courage to be the one who says it first.
