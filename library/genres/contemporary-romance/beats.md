# Contemporary Romance — Beats

The default structure is the **romance beat sheet**: the relationship is the plot, so the
beats track the bond's escalation and rupture, not an external mission. Percentages are of
total length; treat them as targets, not handcuffs.

- **Love-interest introduction (0–10%)** — the LI is *on the page* by here, or commercial failure. Readers came for the couple.
- **Meet-cute or catalyst (0–15%)** — for a new couple, the charming/spiky first encounter that establishes chemistry *and* conflict; for an existing-history pairing (friends-to-lovers, second chance), the catalyst that forces the shift from platonic.
- **Inciting incident (15–25%)** — an event that forces continued interaction (the engineered proximity locks in).
- **First plot point (~25%)** — the leads commit to the journey together, often reluctantly.
- **First pinch (~37%)** — an external force threatens the developing bond.
- **Midpoint (~50%)** — a major shift: first kiss / first intimacy, immediately followed by a pullback. The stakes of losing each other become real.
- **Second pinch (~62%)** — the relationship deepens but a larger obstacle looms.
- **Dark moment / all-is-lost (75–80%)** — the breakup or rupture; love and fear in direct conflict; it must rest on a *real* wound, never a dumb misunderstanding.
- **Climax (85–90%)** — the grand gesture, the declaration, choosing love over fear. The leads' own growth resolves it — not coincidence, fate, or a convenient outside force.
- **Resolution (90–100%)** — HEA/HFN locked in: a clear commitment and a glimpse of the shared future.

## Obligatory Scenes (readers would feel cheated without these)
- **The meeting / catalyst** — the leads collide; friction or fascination established.
- **Forced proximity / first alliance** — circumstance binds them; escape isn't an option.
- **The first turn** — a kiss, confession, or act of trust that changes the relationship.
- **The midpoint deepening** — intimacy or shared danger raises the cost of losing each other.
- **The dark moment** — a real rupture (~75%) that forces them apart and earns the reunion.
- **The grovel / grand gesture** — the at-fault lead changes and proves it; love chosen over fear.
- **The earned HEA/HFN** — together and hopeful, with a forward glimpse.

Alternate structures: **linear/rapid** for external-conflict tropes (no prologues, all
momentum); **dual-timeline or prologue framing** for internal-history tropes that must
compress years of backstory.
