# Contemporary Romance — Must-Haves (non-negotiable checklist)

Skip these and it isn't really contemporary romance. Treat as a revision checklist.

- [ ] A central love story that is the spine of the book, not a subplot.
- [ ] An emotionally satisfying, optimistic ending — HEA (happily ever after) or HFN (happy for now). The couple ends together and hopeful.
- [ ] The love interest on the page by ~10%, and the two leads together often, with rising attraction and a real obstacle between them.
- [ ] Mutuality: both characters grow and sacrifice for the relationship; neither is merely fixed by the other.
- [ ] Genuine relationship stakes — what each lead risks, internally, by loving the other.
- [ ] Engineered proximity: a concrete reason the leads can't simply walk away from each other.
- [ ] A midpoint shift in intimacy or trust, and a dark moment (~75%) built on a real wound that forces them apart.
- [ ] A resolution the leads earn through their own choices and growth — the grovel/grand gesture, not a coincidence or outside rescue.
- [ ] Two leads readers actively like and want together: relatable flaws plus appealing traits, with real agency.
- [ ] A *power-with* dynamic: the love interest respects the other's autonomy and "no"; protectiveness never becomes control.
- [ ] A heat level set early and held consistent end-to-end, matching the cover/blurb promise.
- [ ] A sensory, specific setting that shapes the romance rather than sitting behind it.
