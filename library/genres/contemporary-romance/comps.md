# Contemporary Romance — Comparable Titles

Touchstones and *why* they work — a source for deriving obligatory scenes and calibrating
heat and tone. Position against current bestsellers; fill live comps during book
planning's market-analysis step.

- **Ali Hazelwood, *The Love Hypothesis*** — STEM rivals-to-lovers, fake dating, linear rapid pacing, minimal backstory; the external-conflict template done clean.
- **Emily Henry, *Book Lovers* / *Beach Read*** — dual timelines, emotionally layered, meta-aware; the model for internal-history and second-chance structure.
- **Abby Jimenez** — high-concept opposites-attract, quirky meet-cutes, dramatic contrast with real emotional depth under the banter.
- **Sarah Adams** — small-town friends-to-lovers, deep history, slow burn; the catalyst-moment approach to platonic-to-romantic.
- **Christina Lauren, *The Unhoneymooners*** — forced proximity and enemies-to-lovers with a romcom voice.
- **Julia Quinn, *The Duke and I* (Bridgertons)** — Regency historical; social-convention conflict, marriage-market stakes, slow-burn courtship.
- **Tessa Dare, *The Duchess Deal*** — witty Regency, marriage-of-convenience to love.

Use these to calibrate, not to copy: note where each places its meet-cute, midpoint, and
dark moment, and what heat level its cover and category promise.
