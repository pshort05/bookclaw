Tropes are the recurring patterns that readers expect and enjoy in this genre.

## Dominant Tropes

- Diane Mott Davidson’s 'Catering to Nobody' (1990) established core tropes: caterer Goldy Bear solves crimes while sharing recipes. Virginia Rich’s 'The Cooking School Murders' (1982) pioneered the food-as-investigative-tool concept. These early works focused on domestic settings and small-town communities, embedding recipes naturally into plot structures.
- Series prevail (75% of trad-pub releases), enabling recipe collections and community arcs. Standalones are rare, often in suspense hybrids. Plots follow linear 'crime-recipe-solution' patterns. Flashbacks primarily reveal culinary backstories.
