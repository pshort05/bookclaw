# Culinary Mystery Must-Haves

Non-negotiable elements for this genre.

## Essential Checklist

- [ ] Food details must be accurate: readers in this genre cook. A wrong technique, a bad pairing, or a misrepresented cooking time will be caught and will break trust.
- [ ] At least three to five recipes or food descriptions are woven into scenes where preparation or consumption does story work — not appended as afterthoughts.
- [ ] The culinary world creates both the crime and the clues: motive, method, and evidence arise from the food business — recipe theft, sabotaged ingredients, allergy exploitation, professional rivalry.
- [ ] The protagonist's culinary skills actively aid the investigation: they gain access or extract information through knowledge a non-professional would not have.
- [ ] Crime disrupts the food establishment in ways that ripple through its community: the business is at risk, staff are suspects, regulars are shaken.
- [ ] The sleuth's investigative access is credibly constrained: it comes through their professional role and must be earned, not assumed.
- [ ] The mystery is solvable from clues available to the reader: culinary knowledge required is either common or explained within the text.
- [ ] Food descriptions engage multiple senses: smell and texture matter as much as appearance. Clinical prose breaks the genre's sensory contract.
- [ ] The killer's motive connects to the culinary world: expressed through food-industry specifics — a stolen recipe, sabotaged review, contested provenance claim.
