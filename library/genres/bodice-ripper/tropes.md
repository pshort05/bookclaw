- The Kidnapping and Captivity Trope
- Among the most distinctive and controversial elements of bodice-ripper narratives is the abduction or kidnapping trope, in which the hero forcibly takes the heroine against her will. This narrative device is not unique to bodice-rippers—it has deep historical and literary precedents. The mythology of Hades abducting Persephone, the legendary Rape of the Sabine Women (in which early Roman men abducted women from neighboring Sabine territories to secure brides), and Samuel Richardson's 18th-century novel Pamela (in which a young woman is eventually seduced by her aristocratic captor) all establish literary and cultural templates for romantic narratives that begin with coercive captivity. Within the bodice-ripper framework, the kidnapping functions as both plot device and thematic exploration of power, agency, and the possibility of genuine love emerging from fundamentally unequal circumstances.[11]
