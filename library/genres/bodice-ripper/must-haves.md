- The Traitorous Body: The most essential trope. Even if the Heroine hates the Hero intellectually or fears him, her body betrays her by responding physically to his touch (e.g., "Her mind screamed 'No,' but her blood sang 'Yes'").
- The Nursing Scene: The Hero gets wounded (usually defending her or in a duel), and the Heroine must tend to his fevered, half-naked body. This shifts the power dynamic from fear to intimacy.
- Key Trope Variation: The Compromise. If they are caught alone together, they must marry. The Hero often engineers this.
- No "Beta" Heroes: The Hero must never be gentle initially. He forces the Heroine to confront her passion.
