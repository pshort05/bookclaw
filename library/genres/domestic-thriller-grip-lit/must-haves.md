- This setting choice is thematically essential. The power of the domestic thriller derives from the juxtaposition between the familiar, safe quality of these spaces and the darkness that emerges within them. A reader feels personally implicated because the settings are recognizable; the crimes and crises unfolding could theoretically occur in the reader's own living room. The home transforms from shelter into prison or hunting ground. The specific presentation of setting in successful domestic thrillers often emphasizes the gap between exterior appearance and interior reality.[13][12][14][6]
- Domestic thrillers consistently feature ordinary, relatable protagonists rather than trained detectives or action heroes. A reader encounters a character who resembles themselves—a suburban mother, a professional woman, a stay-at-home parent—and must follow that character into extraordinary psychological territory. The protagonist's ordinariness creates identification.[15][12]
