# Sports Romance — Reader Expectations

The structural backbone of the genre: high-stakes emotion mirroring physical exertion and
elite competition. The promise is an emotional cocktail of *heat, heart, and high-pressure
vulnerability*, where the grueling realities of elite sport actively dictate the romance.

## Tone & Mood
Heightened, immersive, and adrenaline-fueled. The mood balances intense physical
competition with sharp, witty banter, delivering heat, heart, and high-pressure
vulnerability in equal measure.

## Pacing
Pacing must mirror the rhythm of the sport's season — athletic stakes actively drive the
scene stakes. Alternate between high-adrenaline plot beats (games, media scrums, drafts) and
slower, intimate emotional hinges (locker rooms, hotel beds, physical recovery) where the
walls come down.

## Setting Conventions
Environments of elite performance: locker rooms, private team flights, ice rinks,
fieldhouses, and high-profile media events. The world is constrained by public-identity
pressure — fame, media scrutiny, and team contracts are *structural constraints* that
complicate the romance, not just backdrop.

## Character Archetypes
The elite athlete whose body is their career, masking immense pressure with cockiness or
grumpiness. The chaotic but grounded love interest (sunshine to their grumpy). Found family
is essential: the protective enforcer, the meddling teammate, the demanding coach, and the
ruthless agent.

## Length & Format
Aim for 80,000–100,000 words. The BookTok bestseller format is first person, present tense,
using dual POV (alternating chapters). Heat levels are typically high/explicit. Standalones
within interconnected series — a different teammate per book — are the industry standard.
