# Sports Romance — Genre Killers (avoid; these make readers DNF or one-star)

The anti-checklist. This is what makes readers one-star or DNF a sports romance.

- **Sport as wallpaper** — starting with strong athletic stakes in Act I, then abandoning the sport entirely so the climax happens in a kitchen instead of an arena.
- **Identical POVs** — dual-POV chapters where the brooding athlete and the quirky love interest sound exactly the same, down to the same niche vocabulary.
- **Told, not shown, chemistry** — insisting characters have an "undeniable connection" without putting them in high-tension, banter-filled situations to prove it.
- **Surprise cheating / unwarned love triangles** — cheating between the main couple or unresolved love triangles without a massive, explicit warning in the blurb.
- **The emotional doormat** — a main character who exists solely to micromanage a supposedly strong athlete's emotional outbursts while sacrificing their own agency.
