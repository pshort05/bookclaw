These are verified published sports romance titles representing the genre's commercial center, its foundational slow-burn tradition, its leading authors, and its recent bestselling directions.

## The Wall of Winnipeg and Me — Mariana Zapata (2016)

**Sport:** American football (NFL). **Heat level:** Low to moderate; closed-door by modern standards. **Structure:** Marriage of convenience / forced proximity. The love interest is the athlete's personal assistant — proximity is built into the job. Zapata's signature is extreme slow burn: the emotional intimacy develops over a very long timeline (the book runs 150,000+ words) before any romantic acknowledgment occurs. This book trained a generation of sports romance readers to expect that length and that pacing. Use as a comp when writing: marriage of convenience, extended slow burn, low-heat but emotionally intense, football setting.

## Heated Rivalry — Rachel Reid (2019)

**Sport:** Ice hockey (NHL). **Heat level:** Explicit. **Structure:** Rivals-to-lovers; M/M romance between players on competing teams. Shane Hollander and Ilya Rozanov play the same position for rival teams and cannot escape their collision in either professional or personal life. The rivals dynamic is structurally inseparable from the athletic world — it would not function in any other setting. The sex scenes are character-developing rather than decorative. This title was adapted into a series for a major streaming platform (2025), signaling mainstream crossover success. Use as a comp when writing: rivals to lovers, hockey, M/M, explicit heat, dual POV.

## The Deal — Elle Kennedy (2015)

**Sport:** College ice hockey. **Heat level:** Explicit. **Structure:** Fake dating. A college athlete agrees to tutor the love interest in exchange for their help with a fake-dating arrangement. The Off-Campus series is the foundational text of college sports romance — it established the template that dozens of subsequent series have iterated on. Kennedy's books are known for witty banter, ensemble casts, and a balance of emotional depth and explicit heat. Use as a comp when writing: college sports romance, hockey, fake dating, explicit heat, series with ensemble team.

## Intercepted — Alexa Martin (2018)

**Sport:** American football (NFL). **Heat level:** Steamy/explicit. **Structure:** Fresh-start romance; female protagonist adjacent to the NFL world. Martin, a former NFL wife, brings authentic insider detail to the team-wife culture, the media dynamics, and the specific social world that surrounds professional football. The protagonist is escaping a relationship with a cheating player and navigating a new connection with his rival. The football world is not just setting — it is the source of every complication. Use as a comp when writing: NFL, female protagonist adjacent to sport, insider culture detail, explicit heat, representation.

## Pucking Around — Emily Rath (2023)

**Sport:** Ice hockey (fictional NHL team). **Heat level:** Explicitly sexual. **Structure:** Why-choose / polyamorous romance — one protagonist develops relationships with multiple NHL players simultaneously. Became an international bestseller driven by BookTok. Unusually long for the genre. Notable for expanding the sports romance readership into non-traditional relationship structures while maintaining the sport-world authenticity. Use as a comp when writing: hockey, why-choose/polyamorous structure, BookTok market, very high heat, long-form.

## Icebreaker — Hannah Grace (2022/2023)

**Sport:** Ice hockey and figure skating. **Heat level:** Explicit. **Structure:** Enemies to roommates to lovers; college setting. A figure skater is forced to share training ice time with the hockey team, creating immediate conflict. Became a gateway title for readers new to hockey romance and drove significant BookTok traffic. The dual-sport setting creates a specific tension about ice time, resources, and who has institutional power in an athletic department. Use as a comp when writing: college setting, hockey, enemies to lovers, explicit heat, accessible entry point for new readers.

## The Striker — Ana Huang (2024)

**Sport:** Soccer (Premier League). **Heat level:** Steamy. **Structure:** Forbidden romance / off-limits attraction between a footballer and a woman with a complicated prior history. Debuted as a New York Times, Publishers Weekly, and USA Today bestseller. Signals the emergence of soccer as a commercially viable sport in romance — equivalent to the mainstream validation hockey received five years earlier. The Premier League setting gives the book international scope. Use as a comp when writing: soccer, Premier League setting, forbidden romance, mainstream commercial trajectory, NYT-bestseller tier.

## Consider Me — Becka Mack (2022)

**Sport:** Ice hockey (NHL). **Heat level:** Explicit. **Structure:** Playboy-to-devoted; one-woman man who does not know it yet. The protagonist is a player with a reputation for casual relationships who discovers a specific connection that reorders his understanding of what he wants. Strong team-as-found-family dynamic. Frequently cited as a companion rec alongside Pucking Around for readers who want emotionally invested hockey romance with high heat. Use as a comp when writing: hockey, playboy archetype, emotional depth alongside explicit content, ensemble team, series entry point.
