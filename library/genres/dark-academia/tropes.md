## Dominant Tropes

- Key Themes, Motifs, Tropes, and Symbols
- Class, Privilege, and Elitism: A recurring theme involves the exploration and critique of academic elitism and the privilege inherent in prestigious institutions. Many dark academia narratives examine how these institutions were built on systems of oppression, including slavery and systematic exclusion of marginalized groups. The disintegration of the academic elite often serves as a central narrative arc.[5][14]
- Common Tropes
- Secret Societies: Exclusive, ritualistic societies operating beneath the surface of schools are among the most prevalent tropes. Membership is a coveted, dangerous prize, and these groups heighten the sense of exclusivity and danger. Examples include the houses in Leigh Bardugo's Ninth House and the classical studies group in The Secret History.[13][14][15][20]
- Dark Academia Romance/Romantasy: These works incorporate romantic relationships—often forbidden or complicated—as central plot elements alongside mystery and magic. Common romantic tropes include professor/student dynamics, rivals to lovers, and "touch her and die" protective relationships.[16][25]
