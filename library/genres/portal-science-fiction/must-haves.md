*These elements separate portal science fiction from fantasy; without them, you lose the scientific rigor readers expect.*

- **Functional Physics for Portals** — readers must understand how portals work (energy requirements, stability, distance effects, theoretical basis); consistent rules apply throughout
- **Scientific Investigation** — protagonist(s) approach portal as scientists, engineers, or analysts; experimental method and evidence-based reasoning drive plot
- **Unintended Consequence Exploration** — portal technology inevitably creates unforeseen problems; story explores both anticipated and surprise effects
- **Multi-World Consistency** — each world follows its own logic consistently; alternate reality isn't random but follows its own rules deterministically
- **Political/Economic Realism** — portal access creates genuine power struggles; resource competition, military advantage, economic disruption play central roles
- **Communication Barriers** — different worlds speak different languages, use incompatible technology, hold conflicting values; genuine understanding requires work
- **Technological Authenticity** — portal technology should reflect contemporary science understanding or clear explanation of theoretical advancement
- **Systemic Scope** — effects of portal contact ripple through civilization; economics, military, science, politics all transformed by multi-world access
