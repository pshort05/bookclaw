*Weird West Fantasy requires these core elements to function: a frontier setting where the supernatural is integral, morally complex protagonists, and magic that costs something essential.*

- **Frontier Setting (circa 1865-1890 or alternate analog)** — specific geographic markers (desert, mountains, prairie, badlands); named settlements or landmarks; period-appropriate or clearly anachronistic with justification; landscape is character
- **Fused Supernatural Elements** — the weird must be organic to the setting, not grafted on; fantasy, horror, occult, steampunk, or cosmic elements integrated seamlessly into frontier logic
- **Morally Ambiguous Protagonist** — cannot be purely good or evil; driven by survival, greed, redemption, code, or vengeance; makes compromises; readers accept and expect anti-heroes
- **Magic/Power with Cost** — using supernatural forces exacts toll: bleeding from eyes, lost memories, attracted hostile spirits, literal years of life, spiritual corruption, physical exhaustion; cost maintains tension and prevents power creep
- **Visceral, Consequential Action** — gunfights, chases, and physical confrontations are sharp, quick, dangerous; stakes are personal and physical; violence has realistic aftermath
- **Atmospheric Dread** — slow-burn tension punctuated by sharp eruptions; sensory details anchor the weird: smell of ozone and blood, sound of unnatural silence, feel of grit and heat; uncanny intrudes on familiar
- **Sparse, Lean Prose** — dialogue is terse, laden with subtext; description is focused, avoiding flowery excess; observant, slightly worn voice
- **Isolated or Fragile Community** — protagonist encounters settlements or companions dependent on fragile systems threatened by the weird; explores community survival and individual sacrifice
- **The Frontier Remains** — ending is not redemptive restoration; victory is pyrrhic; the land remains vast, hostile, indifferent; protagonist changed, scarred, or lost; cycle continues
- **Blend of Frontier and Supernatural Climax** — final confrontation must integrate both: bullets and magic, gunslinger and ghost, shootout and ritual; feels inevitable outcome of journey, not separate elements
