*Weird West Fantasy employs frontier archetypes transformed by the supernatural, creating protagonists and conflicts that blur the line between grounded and otherworldly.*

**Dominant & Stable Tropes**

- **The Harrowed Gunslinger** — skilled fighter marked by past trauma or supernatural encounter (back-from-dead, cursed, haunted); driven by vengeance, redemption, or unpayable debt; internal struggle with their harrowing as important as external conflicts
- **The Mysterious Stranger** — arrival of enigmatic figure with secret knowledge or power; ambiguous allegiance; may be demon, ancient spirit, time-traveler; represents unpredictable force of the weird entering settled community
- **The Beast in the Desert** — iconic, region-specific monster stalking vast territory; more than animal—manifestation of land's wrath or curse given form (wendigo, giant rattler, shade); hunting or escaping provides plot drive
- **The Frontier Town with Dark Secret** — town appears normal but hides cult, portal, mass grave, ancient power site; revelation is core plot point; microcosm of genre's core conflict
- **Haunted Boomtown** — mining town or railroad camp built on sacred ground or above gateway to hell; plagued by supernatural phenomena; prosperity built on violation
- **The Occult Scholar/Preacher** — character versed in forbidden lore; exorcist or heretic; often morally ambiguous ally or antagonist
- **The Terse Gunfighter Code** — protagonist bound by worn moral code: will always protect innocents, usually honor deals, never kill unarmed—unless circumstances justify exception

**Rising Tropes (Growth)**

- **The Arcane Naturalist/Herbalist** — wields magic from deep, respectful understanding of land; uses local plants, animal spirits, geological formations; power from symbiosis, not domination; counters conquest narrative
- **The Queer Gunslinger/Outlaw** — LGBTQ+ character in central, morally complex role; outsider status provides unique perspective on frontier hypocrisy; slow-burn romance evolving from adversity
- **Corporate/Industrial Antagonist** — villain is railroad, mining conglomerate, banking syndicate mining land's magic for profit; unleashes weird as supernatural pollution; makes horror systemic, bureaucratic
- **Found Family in Wasteland** — emotionally damaged protagonist accumulates crew of fellow outcasts with weird-specific skills (haunted scout, disgraced preacher, mechanical genius); provides emotional warmth against bleakness
- **Sentient or Cursed Landscape** — entire rivers drown the guilty, forests rearrange themselves, canyons whisper secrets; environment itself intelligent, active antagonist or ally; leans into ecological horror

**Fading Tropes (Avoid)**

- **The Magical Negro** — Black character exists solely to provide mystical aid to white protagonist, often dying sacrificially; generates justified criticism; signals misunderstanding
- **Women as Vessels for Evil** — woman possessed by demon; male protagonist must save (through violent exorcism) or kill her; reduces female characters to passive plot devices
- **The Noble Savage Shaman** — stereotypical Indigenous character providing cryptic guidance and magic before dying; racist reduction; replace with Indigenous protagonists possessing complex agency
- **Uncomplicated Cowboys vs. Indians with Monsters** — simplistic portrayal of Indigenous peoples as savages or mystical obstacle; fails to engage with genocide; being replaced by narratives centering Indigenous perspectives
- **Pure Comedic/Slapstick** — zany comedy clashing awkwardly with genre darkness; alienates core fans; difficult to execute
