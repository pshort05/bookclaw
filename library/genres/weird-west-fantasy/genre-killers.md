*Avoid these elements: they undermine Weird West Fantasy's core appeal and alienate readers seeking grit, nuance, and the integration of frontier and supernatural.*

- **Generic Fantasy Pasted onto Western** — the world feels like a D&D campaign with horses; fantasy tropes (elves, mana, spellbooks, dungeons) replacing frontier-specific logic; lacks historical and cultural grounding; magic feels institutionalized, not discovered
- **Overpowered Protagonist** — protagonist solves problems through magic alone; wizard in a duster who doesn't need frontier skills; breaks tension and violates the cost principle; protagonists must rely equally on shooting, tracking, bartering
- **Uncritical Confederate/Lost Cause Nostalgia** — stories glorifying the Confederacy or romanticizing Old South even with magic; generates intense backlash; signals profound historical ignorance; commercial poison; rejected by savvy editors
- **Stereotypical, One-Dimensional Portrayals** — caricatures of Indigenous peoples, Mexicans, women, LGBTQ+ individuals as sidekicks, victims, or mystical guides; violates authenticity principle; reduces agency; modern readers reject
- **Clean Victory Without Cost** — protagonist wins without sacrifice; survives without scars or loss; breaks contract with readers expecting grittiness; fails to reflect genre's core about survival's price
- **Unearned or Thematically Hollow Climax** — final shootout or showdown lacks weight; doesn't feel inevitable result of journey; stakes aren't personal; action divorced from character arc or supernatural resolution
- **Comedic/Slapstick Tone** — broad zany humor clashing with genre darkness; alienates core fans of both horror and serious Western; very poor sales track record
- **Simple Zombie/Monster Apocalypse** — oversaturated pure "cowboys vs. zombies" concept; lacks novelty; readers now demand zombies to be symptom of deeper cosmic or ecological horror, not sole focus
- **Convenient Supernatural Solutions** — magic replaces earned effort; characters use power to easily overcome obstacles without cost or consequence; contradicts genre's principle that weird complicates, not solves
- **Romanticized Colonialism** — treats frontier expansion as heroic or inevitable without acknowledging genocide and cultural destruction; fails to engage with historical reality; alienates sophisticated readers
- **Dialogue Overloaded with Period Slang** — excessive use of "howdy," "varmint," "sodbuster," frontier speech patterns; feels cartoonish and pulls reader out; selective, purposeful use only
- **Passive Female Characters** — women exist as prizes to rescue, motivators for male violence, or possessed vessels for evil; lack agency or independent goals; modern readers demand active protagonists regardless of gender
