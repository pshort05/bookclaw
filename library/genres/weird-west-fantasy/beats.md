*Weird West Fantasy narratives mirror a frontier journey, balancing atmospheric dread with sharp, consequential action across a hostile landscape.*

**Act I: The Call to the Trail (Setup)**

1. **Establish Ordinary World** — protagonist's harsh but relatively stable frontier life in town, ranch, or isolated outpost; show competence and worn pragmatism
2. **The Weird Incites** — supernatural threat, cursed bounty, mysterious artifact, or phenomenon arrives; intrudes into the familiar; feels wrong and unsettling
3. **Forced Commitment** — protagonist compelled to leave relative safety; driven by need (payment, obligation), duty, vengeance, or protection of someone/something
4. **Leave Familiar Ground** — protagonist commits to the trail; crosses threshold into unknown, more hostile territory

**Act II: The Haunted Journey (Confrontation)**

5. **First Leg Encounters** — journey through physically and supernaturally dangerous territory; meet allies, enemies, and phenomena; landscape itself presents escalating threats
6. **Protagonist Skills Tested** — demonstrate frontier competence (tracking, shooting, bartering, survival) and moral code under pressure; acquire strange tools or forbidden knowledge
7. **Midpoint Reversal** — major setback or revelation at story's center; betrayal, devastating loss, or revelation of threat's true scope; changes protagonist's understanding or approach
8. **The Cost and Regrouping** — protagonist adapts and prepares for final confrontation at significant cost; magic or sacrifice demanded; relationships broken or transformed
9. **Atmospheric Deepening** — supernatural elements intensify; reality grows thin; landscape becomes more actively hostile; dread accumulates

**Act III: The Showdown at Sundown (Resolution)**

10. **Arrive at Final Location** — protagonist reaches source of conflict (ghost town, canyon, mine, sacred site); often iconic, historically or supernaturally significant place
11. **Hybrid Confrontation** — final conflict blends frontier action (shootout, chase, standoff) with supernatural resolution (ritual, bargain, sacrifice); must feel cohesive, not tacked-on
12. **The Price Revealed** — victory carries cost; protagonist succeeds but loses something essential (relationships, innocence, years of life, connection to humanity)
13. **The Changed Frontier** — aftermath shows transformed world or protagonist's place in it; frontier remains vast and unforgiving; safety is temporary; cycle continues

**Mandatory Scene Types**

- **Saloon/Street Standoff** — tense confrontation with dialogue, subtext, and threat of violence; establishes stakes and character code
- **Night Camp Revelation** — intimate scene under strange stars; shared stories, secrets, or betrayals; characters vulnerable before danger resumes
- **Desperate Chase/Flight** — visceral sequence across hostile terrain; protagonists pursued or escaping by necessity; showcases environmental threat
- **Bargain with Weird Power** — protagonist negotiates or is tempted by supernatural force; establishes magic's cost; changes trajectory
- **Final Convergent Showdown** — climax where action, character, and supernatural elements merge into single transformative sequence
