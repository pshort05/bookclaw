# Neurodiverse Romance — Must-Haves (non-negotiable checklist)

Skip these and the manuscript fails the fundamental requirements of authentic neurodivergent
representation. Treat as a revision pass before considering the draft complete.

- [ ] **Write from the inside out** — the neurodivergent protagonist's interiority must be the
  primary vehicle of the story; their internal reasoning, sensory experience, and social processing
  must be rendered with specificity, not summarized from the outside as "different" or "odd."
- [ ] **Earn the diagnosis (or the recognition)** — if a character's neurodivergence is named in the
  text, their behavior, history, and self-understanding must be consistent with that specific
  condition; do not conflate autism, ADHD, OCD, and anxiety as interchangeable quirks.
- [ ] **Show masking as labor** — if the protagonist masks, the reader must feel the cost: the
  post-interaction exhaustion, the scripted conversations, the performed expressions. This is the
  genre's version of "professional competence" — it earns reader empathy before the love story begins.
- [ ] **Make the love interest genuinely curious** — the love interest must demonstrate active,
  non-condescending interest in understanding the protagonist; they ask, they listen, they adjust.
  They do not "fix" the protagonist or congratulate themselves for their patience.
- [ ] **Guarantee the HEA without neurotypical assimilation** — the happy ending cannot require
  the neurodivergent character to become functionally neurotypical; they must be accepted,
  accommodated, and loved as they are, not as a project that succeeded.
- [ ] **Ground the black moment in the neurodivergent character's internal logic** — the breakup
  or retreat must arise from the character's specific fear (of being a burden, of eventual
  rejection, of exhausting someone they love) — not from a generic miscommunication that any
  character could have.
- [ ] **Validate without pathologizing** — the text must distinguish between traits that cause the
  character genuine distress (which may be worth addressing) and traits that are simply different
  (which are not problems requiring solutions). Never frame neurodivergence itself as the obstacle.
- [ ] **Let the neurodivergent character drive their own arc** — self-knowledge, self-acceptance,
  and the romantic choice must be the protagonist's decisions; the love interest can be a catalyst
  but not the author of the protagonist's healing.
- [ ] **Research the specific condition** — autistic readers, ADHD readers, and OCD readers will
  recognize accuracy and will not forgive stereotypes deployed as shorthand. Own-voices consultation,
  community sensitivity reading, or direct lived experience is the baseline.
