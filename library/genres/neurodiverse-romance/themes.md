# Neurodiverse Romance — Themes

The emotional spine of the manuscript. Without a unifying theme, the neurodivergent representation
becomes a surface feature rather than a structural one. Choose two or three to anchor the story.

- **Masking and the terror of being seen** — the daily performance of neurotypicality as armor;
  the romance arc is the gradual, terrifying decision to let the mask slip in front of one specific
  person, and the discovery that what lies beneath is not a liability but the most lovable part.
- **Love as a language you have to learn together** — the premise that neurotypical conventions of
  flirtation, courtship, and affection are not universal; the couple must build their own
  communication system from scratch, and that act of building is itself the love story.
- **Self-knowledge as a prerequisite for intimacy** — the late-diagnosed or newly self-aware
  character cannot give themselves to another person until they stop grieving the neurotypical they
  thought they were supposed to be; the romantic arc and the identity arc are inseparable.
- **The difference between accommodation and acceptance** — being tolerated is not being loved;
  the romantic partner who makes adjustments with visible effort is not the same as the partner for
  whom adjustment is simply how they show up; the arc forces the protagonist to demand the latter.
- **Intensity as a feature, not a defect** — the hyperfocus, the loyalty, the emotional depth, the
  obsessive passion that a neurotypical world reads as too much; the love interest is the one person
  who experiences that intensity as a gift rather than a burden.
- **Community and the end of isolation** — neurodivergent characters frequently carry histories of
  social exclusion; the romance is embedded in the larger arc of finding a community — a workplace,
  a neighborhood, a friend group — where belonging is possible.
- **Advocacy without saviorhood** — the love interest champions the neurodivergent protagonist in
  a world that misreads them, but the protagonist's battles are ultimately their own to fight and
  win; the partner amplifies rather than rescues.
