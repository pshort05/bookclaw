## Comparable Titles

- A comprehensive guide to the story devices and their subgenre variations.
- The protagonist initially refuses to get involved, citing their lack of qualification, but is compelled by circumstance, moral duty, or personal stakes.
- The sleuth repeatedly points out they are not a real detective, even as their competence grows.
- The Skeptical Authority Figure
- A crucial piece of evidence the sleuth (and reader) encountered early on but failed to recognize until the final reveal.
- A friend, relative, or colleague who provides companionship, specialized knowledge, or comic relief, often acting as a sounding board.
- A character who appears guilty—or even confesses—to misdirect the sleuth and the reader from the true culprit.
- The sleuth physically or mentally compiles lists of suspects, clues, timetables, or motives, visually representing their deductive process.
- Symbols: The teacup (comfort, domesticity), the knitting needle (creating order from threads), the herb garden (healing, home remedies).
- Tropes: The sleuth must operate within strict social hierarchies and without modern technology. The skeptical authority figure is often tied to institutional power (the church, the aristocracy).
