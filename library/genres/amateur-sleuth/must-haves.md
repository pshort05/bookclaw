Every book in this genre needs these essential elements.

## Essential Checklist

-  [ ] **Chapter 1-5:** Introduce sleuth, world, and the victim. Establish personal connection or call to action.
-  [ ] **Chapter 6-12:** First round of suspect interviews. Introduce key clues (A, B, C) and red herrings.
-  [ ] **Chapter 13-18:** Stakes rise. Sleuth faces opposition. A suspect is eliminated or becomes a victim.
-  [ ] **Chapter 19-22:** Midpoint reversal. Sleuth's theory shatters. They must re-group.
-  [ ] **Chapter 23-28:** Investigation narrows. Sleuth understands the *real* motive. Personal danger emerges.
-  [ ] **Chapter 29-30:** Darkest hour. All seems lost.
-  [ ] **Chapter 31-33:** Epiphany. Sleuth pieces it together.
-  [ ] **Chapter 34-35:** Gathering and confrontation. Solution is explained.
-  [ ] **Chapter 36:** Restoration of order. Loose ends tied. New normal.
