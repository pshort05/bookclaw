# Urban Fantasy Romance Genre-Killers

Things that break the genre and alienate readers.

- Whimsical fairy-tale tone or overly formal Shakespearean dialogue; breaks gritty noir atmosphere
- Insta-love without magical justification (fated mate bond, blood bond, or magical compulsion); romance must be earned
- Inconsistent magic rules or magic appearing conveniently to solve plot problems; breaks immersion and stakes
- Info-dumping worldbuilding through long exposition passages or "As you know, Bob" dialogue; halt narrative momentum
- TSTL (Too Stupid To Live) heroine who makes illogical decisions; protagonist should be competent and strategic
- Ignoring real-world consequences of magic; fireball hitting Starbucks must trigger police/news/cleanup response or Masquerade threat
- Excessive sentimentality or surface-level attraction ("OMG he's so hot"); replace with emotional depth and earned chemistry
- Purely comedic lightness without genuine stakes; humor without danger loses tension
- Romance subplot appearing only in final act; relationship needs time to develop throughout story
- Love interest shallow or defined solely by attractiveness; romantic lead must be multidimensional character
- Purple prose, clichéd romance language, or impossible descriptions ("tasted like heaven"); use concrete, evocative sensory detail
- POV slips or head-hopping within scenes; maintain consistent limited third or first person
- All characters sounding the same in dialogue and internal narration; must differentiate voices by vocabulary and speech patterns
- Underdeveloped setting or insufficient sensory grounding; reader cannot visualize urban locations and action
- Romance overshadowing primary plot; external conflict should drive protagonist forward; love enhances but does not replace main story
