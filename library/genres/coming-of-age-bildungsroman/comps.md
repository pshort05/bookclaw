## Comparable Titles

- Point of View: A close First-Person or Limited Third-Person POV is most effective, as it maximizes reader empathy and provides direct access to the protagonist's inner turmoil and growth.
- Loss of Innocence: The painful transition from idealism to a more complex, often cynical, understanding of the world.
- The "Firsts": First love, first heartbreak, first major failure, first rebellion, first moral compromise.
- The Preachy Moral: The "lesson" should be learned by the protagonist through their experience, not stated overtly to the reader. Show the change, don't explain it.
