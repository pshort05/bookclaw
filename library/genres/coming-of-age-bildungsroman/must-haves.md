- The second section, termed the "apprenticeship," "journey," or "trials and tribulations," constitutes the novel's emotional and thematic center. During this extended middle section, the protagonist ventures away from home—either literally, through physical journey to a city or unfamiliar realm, or metaphorically, through psychological exploration and inner transformation. Throughout this phase, the protagonist encounters formative experiences, relationships with mentors and peers, and challenges that test their developing worldview. Importantly, the bildungsroman protagonist often experiences profound disappointment during the apprenticeship, discovering that the world does not align with youthful expectations. This disillusionment constitutes an essential pedagogical element, teaching the protagonist lessons about reality, human nature, social structures, and their own capacities and limitations.[2][11][9][8]
