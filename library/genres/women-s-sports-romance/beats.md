# Women's Sports Romance — Beats

The dominant structure is a **dual-arc beat sheet** — a romance arc and a competition arc running in
parallel, each milestone in one creating pressure on the other, so that athletic highs generate
romantic complications and romantic breakthroughs release into athletic performance.

## Phase 1 — Establish the Athlete (0–15%)
- **Heroine in her sport (1–5%)** — open in training or competition; establish her competence, discipline, and the specific demands of her discipline before a word is spoken to the love interest.
- **The competitive stakes (5–10%)** — name the external goal: the championship, the Olympic berth, the comeback season, the contract extension. Readers must know what she stands to win or lose athletically.
- **Introduce the love interest (10–15%)** — enter as rival, coach, teammate, or cross-sport encounter; the first interaction is charged, often adversarial. Crucially, the love interest recognises her ability immediately.

## Phase 2 — Forced Proximity (15–35%)
- **Adhesion (15–20%)** — the narrative locks them together: a shared training facility, a joint camp, a roster change, a mandatory partnership.
- **First athletic respect (20–28%)** — the love interest witnesses her at her best. Attraction initiates through admiration of competence, not despite it.
- **Rising tension / banter (28–35%)** — charged interaction, competitive friction, or professional disagreement that masks attraction; the first moment of genuine non-athletic intimacy (a late meal, a vulnerability after a loss).

## Phase 3 — Falling (35–55%)
- **Athletic setback (35–45%)** — a loss, an injury scare, a selection snub, or a rival's surge strips away confidence. The love interest responds with support that crosses from professional to personal.
- **Midpoint of love (~50%)** — a false high: the romantic relationship advances (first kiss, first night, first open admission) at the same moment athletic prospects appear to crest. Both believe they can manage both arcs simultaneously.

## Phase 4 — Collision of Arcs (55–75%)
- **The conflict made explicit (55–65%)** — the two arcs collide: the relationship threatens her athletic focus, or the competitive grind creates jealousy, distance, or a perception that he (or she) is a distraction. Teammates, coaches, or sponsors may weaponise this perception.
- **Retreat (65–75%)** — she pulls back to her athletic identity to protect her goals; the love interest is held at arm's length or the relationship is suspended. Her athletic performance does not recover.

## Phase 5 — Crisis and Breakthrough (75–95%)
- **Black moment (75–80%)** — the double loss: she faces the biggest athletic challenge of the book with her emotional support severed, and a misunderstanding or genuine values clash threatens the relationship permanently.
- **Dark night of the soul (80–88%)** — she competes (or fails to compete) from a place of isolation, and recognises that the relationship did not weaken her — its absence did. The internal flaw that generated the conflict surfaces and is named.
- **Grand gesture / reconciliation (88–95%)** — a public or private act that makes the romantic stakes as explicit as the athletic ones. Crucially, the athletic arc must also resolve: she competes, performs, or earns the goal on her own terms.

## Phase 6 — HEA (95–100%)
- **Dual resolution** — both arcs close. She wins (or achieves a meaningful athletic outcome), and the relationship is confirmed as sustainable; neither was sacrificed for the other.

## Obligatory Scenes (readers would feel cheated without these)
- **The competence-first scene** — readers see the heroine execute her sport at a high level before any romantic action; athletic skill must be established, not described after the fact.
- **The love interest's respect moment** — an explicit beat where the love interest demonstrates he (or she) sees her as an athlete first; not flattered by her strength but genuinely awed by it.
- **The training montage of intimacy** — a scene in which the sport itself becomes the vehicle for emotional closeness (spotting, pacing, sparring, shared exhaustion after practice).
- **The athletic crucible scene** — the competition or performance moment with the highest stakes in the book, which the heroine enters and exits on her own terms.
- **The sacrifice test** — a moment where one party is offered something (a spot, a contract, an out) that would require abandoning the other; the choice made here is the emotional climax.
