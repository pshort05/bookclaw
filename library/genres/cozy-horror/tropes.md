Tropes are the recurring patterns that readers expect and enjoy in this genre.

## Dominant Tropes

- The Inciting Space**: A specific, detailed setting (inherited house, small town, magical establishment) serves as the story's heart. The setting should have personality and history that protagonist gradually uncovers.
- The Mystery**: Clear but not complex. "What is this supernatural phenomenon?" suffices as mystery structure. Plot momentum comes from discovery and relationship-building, not convoluted whodunit plotting.
- The Emotional Arc**: The protagonist's emotional journey—from fear/confusion to acceptance/connection—is more important than plot complications. Emotional stakes should outweigh supernatural stakes.
- Community Connection**: Multiple scenes showing protagonist bonding with secondary characters through supernatural shared experience. Found family is a structural anchor, not subplot.
- The Payoff**: Resolution should provide emotional satisfaction. This may involve:

## Rising Tropes

-  Understanding the haunting's origin story
-  Achieving coexistence with supernatural being
-  Discovering protagonist's own supernatural connection
-  Solving the localized mystery
-  All of the above through emotional acceptance rather than defeat
