Every book in this genre needs these essential elements.

## Essential Checklist

- [ ] A historical setting with period-accurate specificity that serves the story — details of dress, speech, class, and daily life that create atmosphere and generate plot complications, not merely decorate the prose.
- [ ] A cozy register maintained throughout: no graphic violence, no on-page gore, no nihilistic darkness — death may occur but it happens at a remove, and the world retains warmth and legibility.
- [ ] A mystery and a romance that intersect, not run in parallel — the investigation must draw the protagonist and romantic interest together, create shared risk, and deepen their understanding of each other through what they discover.
- [ ] A romantic partner who participates actively in the investigation — with skills, access, or knowledge the protagonist lacks, so the partnership is functional as well as emotional.
- [ ] Period-specific constraints that create real romantic obstacles — not just social awkwardness but genuine structural limits (chaperonage, propriety, gender restrictions, class difference, professional rules) that the protagonists must navigate or subvert together.
- [ ] A warm community of secondary characters who anchor the protagonist in a social world — neighbors, staff, shopkeepers, rivals, or friends who have opinions, secrets, and stakes in both the mystery and the romance.
- [ ] An amateur investigator whose involvement is motivated and credible — the protagonist must have a reason to pursue the mystery beyond curiosity, and the police or official apparatus must be shown as inadequate or obstructive.
- [ ] Clues that are fair-play: the reader has access to the same information as the protagonist and can, in principle, solve the mystery ahead of the reveal.
- [ ] An HEA (happily ever after) or HFN (happy for now) that is earned through the events of the investigation — the romantic resolution should feel like a consequence of shared danger and discovery, not a separate track.
- [ ] A resolution that restores the community — the mystery is solved, justice is served in some form appropriate to the period, and the world is demonstrably better or at peace by the final pages.
