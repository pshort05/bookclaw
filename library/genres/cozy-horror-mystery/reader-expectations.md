Readers expect a crime puzzle in which the wrongdoing has a supernatural or horror dimension, but the investigation is warm and community-driven, the violence is kept off-page, and the overall reading experience is closer to comfort than fright.

## Tone & Mood

The tone balances genuine strangeness against cozy warmth. The mystery has real stakes and the horror elements — unexplained occurrences, a possible supernatural perpetrator or victim, a history of uncanny events — are treated with seriousness rather than dismissed as quirky color. But the framing never tips into the disturbing or graphically violent. The reader is unsettled in the way a ghost story unsettles, not traumatised. What separates cozy horror mystery from paranormal thriller is the warmth of the community and the safety of the reading experience; what separates it from standard cozy mystery is that the horror elements are genuinely eerie, not merely atmospheric window dressing.

## Pacing

The investigation drives pacing: crime discovered, suspects assembled, clues accumulated, misdirection lands, resolution reached. The horror layer adds a second track — what is this supernatural element, and is it connected to the crime? — which may resolve alongside the mystery or remain partially open. Between investigation beats, the narrative slows for community interaction: the protagonist's relationships, domestic rituals, and the small reassurances of ordinary life that make the uncanny elements more effective by contrast. Action and violence are minimal; the drama is intellectual and atmospheric.

## Setting Conventions

A small, contained community with a history of strangeness: a village with annual unexplained disappearances, a town built on a cursed site, an isolated community that has its own relationship with the supernatural. The setting's history is part of the mystery — what happened here before, and is this crime connected to it? Physical setting details should emphasise warmth and familiarity (the pub, the churchyard, the high street) against which the uncanny registers effectively. Unlike pure cozy mystery, the setting carries genuine darkness in its past, though the present-day community is warm and functional.

## Character Archetypes

The Sensitive Amateur: a protagonist who is open to supernatural explanations — often because they have personal experience with them — making them effective where official investigators are not. The Pragmatic Official: a detective or constable who investigates the crime without accepting the supernatural layer, creating productive tension with the protagonist. The Keeper of Local Lore: an elderly resident, a local historian, or a practicing folk practitioner who holds knowledge about the community's uncanny history. The Horror-Adjacent Victim or Perpetrator: someone whose involvement with the supernatural element — as practitioner, target, or inadvertent trigger — sets the crime apart from an ordinary murder.

## Length & Format

70,000–90,000 words for a standalone; series are common. Each book should deliver a fully resolved mystery with a satisfying explanation of the horror element, even if larger supernatural questions extend across the series. First-person or close third-person point-of-view maintains the cozy register. A cast of recurring community characters, developed across installments, is a major reader investment point. The horror element should escalate across a series — readers who stay expect each book to push the strangeness slightly further while maintaining the warmth.
