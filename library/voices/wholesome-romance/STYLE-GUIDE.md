# Style Guide: Wholesome Romance

Clean, emotionally-focused contemporary romance. This guide codifies the prose mechanics for writing emotionally authentic, intimate stories where the deepest connection emerges from vulnerability and emotional safety rather than physical intensity. Write for readers who seek comfort, hope, and genuine human connection.

---

## Core Identity

This narrative approach prioritizes emotional authenticity over sensationalism. The story's engine is internal—character growth, healing through understanding, and the gradual building of trust and intimacy. Prose should feel warm and intimate without being cloying; pacing should be unhurried but never dull. The reader should feel welcomed into the character's inner life and leave with a sense of peace and belonging.

---

## Prose Mechanics

### Sentence Rhythm
- **Mix short declarative sentences with longer, flowing ones.** Short sentences (5–10 words) deliver emotional beats and create intimacy. Longer sentences (15–20 words) reflect contemplation, setting, and internal processing.
- **Precision over embellishment.** Every word earns its place. Avoid purple prose, overwrought metaphor, and decorative language. A simple image ("his hands shaking") lands harder than elaborate description.
- **Vary rhythm intentionally.** In moments of high emotion or discovery, tighten sentences and increase frequency of short statements. In reflective passages, allow longer, flowing constructions.

### Vocabulary
- **Family-friendly language.** No explicit profanity; mild exclamations only ("damn," "hell").
- **Accessible, conversational register.** Write as if speaking to an intelligent friend, not a audience at a lecture.
- **Avoid jargon and pretension.** Choose Anglo-Saxon words over Latinate equivalents ("get" not "obtain," "help" not "facilitate").

### Dialogue
- **Authentic, relationship-revealing.** Characters speak like real people—with stutters, hesitations, non-sequiturs, and moments of silence. Let conversations breathe.
- **No exposition-dumping via dialogue.** Avoid having characters explain plot or backstory unnaturally. Information should emerge through action, setting, and natural interaction.
- **Subtext matters.** What characters don't say—and how they avoid saying it—reveals as much as their words.
- **Dialogue drives connection.** Use it to show how characters understand (or misunderstand) each other, tease, support, and gradually lower defenses.

## Narrative Structure

### Point of View & Interiority
- **Deep third-person limited.** Inhabit one character's consciousness per chapter or section. Show their thoughts, feelings, sensations, and interpretations directly.
- **Avoid head-hopping.** Do not switch to another character's POV within a chapter unless there's a clear structural break (new scene, time jump, visual shift).
- **Show character thought with tenderness.** Internal monologue should feel intimate and honest, occasionally self-aware or humorous, but never judgmental of the character.

### Pacing
- **Steady and comfortable, never stalling.** Tension comes from internal conflict and misunderstanding, not manufactured drama or external threat.
- **Resolve conflict through conversation and growth.** When characters disagree, they work toward understanding. No third-act breakup born of a five-minute miscommunication that could have been solved with one honest talk.
- **Avoid melodrama.** Characters should behave consistently with their established selves. If a plot twist requires a character to act out of character, the plot needs revision, not the character.

### Scene Construction
- **Open with a character moment or decision.** Establish who, where, what they want, and what's stopping them.
- **Include sensory detail specific to this character's perspective.** Not ornamental description, but the details this particular person would notice: the smell of coffee, the weight of exhaustion, the warmth of proximity.
- **Build toward emotional revelation or decision.** Most scenes should end with characters understanding each other better, learning something about themselves, or committing to a next step.
- **Close chapters on emotional notes.** End on a question that pulls readers forward ("Did she hear his confession or was she already gone?"), a moment of connection, or a discovery—not a manufactured cliffhanger.

### Chapter Length
- **Target 2,500–3,500 words per chapter.** This length allows for setup, character interaction, emotional depth, and a satisfying close without requiring readers to rush through or lose momentum.

### Intimate Moments
- **Build tension through longing, yearning, and emotional vulnerability.** Anticipation and emotional stakes matter more than the physical act itself.
- **Fade to black gracefully.** Anticipate the moment (racing hearts, trembling hands, the first touch), create emotional resonance, then close the door before physical details. The aftermath—tangled sheets, a shared breath, the vulnerability of post-intimacy—often matters more than the act.
- **Use metaphor and suggestion, not graphic description.** "His hands found her waist" creates more intimacy than clinical detail. Focus on what the character *feels* (safe, wanted, open) rather than a detailed choreography.
- **Connect intimacy to character growth.** Each intimate moment should reveal something about the characters' relationship or their individual development—a barrier overcome, a trust deepened.

## Dialogue Conventions

- **Characters address each other naturally.** Use names sparingly but authentically. In conversation, people often use names to emphasize, comfort, or get attention: "Sarah, I need to tell you something."
- **Action beats humanize speech.** Place character action between dialogue lines to show body language and emotion: *"I can't do this anymore," she said, turning toward the window.* (Not every exchange needs a beat, but frequent ones prevent static dialogue.)
- **Subtext is the heart of the conversation.** What characters mean often differs from what they say. A fight about dishes might really be about feeling unsupported; an awkward joke might mask fear.
- **Silence and hesitation.** Not all dialogue is continuous. Pauses, interrupted sentences, and unfinished thoughts are realistic and often more affecting than complete thoughts.

## What to Avoid

### Emotional & Narrative Failures
- **Head-hopping or multiple POVs in a chapter.** Choose one consciousness per chapter and stay loyal to it. Readers trust what they're told from a single viewpoint.
- **Purple prose or overwrought metaphor.** Precision beats embellishment. "His hands shook" is more powerful than "his fingers trembled like wind-tossed leaves."
- **Graphic language or explicit content.** Family-friendly means no f-words, sexual descriptions, or detailed violence.
- **Characters behaving out of character to create drama.** Plot twists must arise from character truth, not authorial convenience. If a character acts inconsistently, the reader's trust fractures.

### Pacing & Structure Problems
- **Unearned time skips or plot conveniences.** If important time has passed or a problem is suddenly solved, show the work. Don't skip from conflict to resolution without depicting the journey.
- **Third-act breakup born of miscommunication.** Conflict rooted in a simple misunderstanding feels cheap if characters don't simply talk to each other. Real couples communicate; if they don't, that's the character work, not a plot device.
- **Manufactured drama for "tension."** Manufactured problems (secrets kept without reason, needless separation, arbitrary obstacles) feel false. Tension should emerge from real emotional stakes.

### Relationship & Character Issues
- **Mean-spirited banter disguised as chemistry.** Teasing should be warm and affectionate, not cruel or undermining. True intimacy includes kindness.
- **Excessive angst or wallowing.** Characters can suffer, but indulging in suffering without movement feels self-pitying. Provide glimpses of hope or moments of humor even in dark passages.
- **Infidelity or cheating as plot element.** This violates the core trust of wholesome romance. If a character strays, the story must reckon with the breach honestly—no redemption without real work.
- **Love triangles or forced conflict.** If a triangle appears, it should be mild and resolved with grace and communication. Prolonged love triangles generate toxicity rather than tension.
- **Graphic violence.** Action can exist, but visceral descriptions of harm undermine the emotional safety this genre promises.

### Voice & Prose Problems
- **Telling instead of showing.** "She was nervous" is weaker than "Her coffee went cold between her hands." Let readers infer emotion from action and detail.
- **Passive voice or filter words.** Avoid "she saw him" or "he heard the door close." Show directly: "He stood in the doorway. The door closed behind him."
- **Excessive internal monologue.** Character thought should be intimate and selective, not a running commentary on everything. Let action and dialogue do work too.
- **Clichéd expressions or tropes treated as fresh.** "Her heart raced," "butterflies in her stomach," "his eyes darkened with desire"—these are safe but overused. Find specificity instead.

### Pacing Killers
- **Scenes that don't advance plot or character.** Every chapter should move the relationship forward, deepen understanding, or escalate stakes.
- **Lengthy descriptive passages unmoored from character experience.** Description is welcome only if the POV character is actively noticing it and it matters to them.
- **Static dialogue or exposition-heavy scenes.** Conversations should reveal character and advance relationship dynamics, not deliver backstory or world-building lectures.

---

## Success Checklist

Use this checklist to verify wholesome-romance prose:

- [ ] **Sentence rhythm varies intentionally.** Short beats for emotion, longer flows for reflection.
- [ ] **POV is singular and intimate.** One character's consciousness per chapter/section; no head-hopping.
- [ ] **Dialogue reveals character and relationship.** Words show how they connect, misunderstand, or support each other.
- [ ] **Emotional stakes drive the story.** External obstacles exist, but the real tension is internal—can they open their hearts?
- [ ] **Intimate moments build through anticipation.** Fade to black gracefully; focus on emotion and vulnerability.
- [ ] **Tone is warm but not saccharine.** Humor, kindness, and authenticity coexist. No cruelty disguised as chemistry.
- [ ] **Conflict resolves through communication and growth.** Characters work toward understanding, not endless misunderstanding or manufactured obstacles.
- [ ] **Every chapter ends on an emotional note.** Satisfaction, discovery, connection, or a question that pulls readers forward.
- [ ] **Language is precise and family-friendly.** Avoid purple prose, explicit language, and graphic content.
- [ ] **Characters behave consistently.** Plot twists arise from character truth, not authorial convenience.
