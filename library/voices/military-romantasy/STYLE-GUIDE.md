# Style Guide — Military Romantasy

- **Anchor emotion in the body.** Replace abstract feeling with a precise physical sensation plus a fresh, specific metaphor — not "her heart raced" but "her pulse hammered against her collarbone like a trapped bird." Formula: emotion + exact bodily sensation + unique image.
- **Sensory specificity, especially scent and touch.** Use scent to trigger emotional memory; ground intimacy in tactile detail (calluses catching on silk). Choose details that reveal character or advance the moment; never generic, never a pile-up.
- **Layered internal monologue (three beats):** surface reaction → tactical / professional read of the situation → the emotional truth the character doesn't want to admit. Use it to show growth, not to stall the scene.
- **Dialogue carries the character.** Give each POV a distinct rhythm. Elite / military characters speak clipped and tactical ("Copy that." "Stay behind me." "Not happening.") and protective under stress — then drop the formality when emotion overwhelms them. Subtext over speeches; what is unsaid matters most.
- **Pace by scene type:** short, punchy sentences for action and danger; longer, flowing sentences for emotional and intimate beats; varied rhythm in dialogue matched to the speaker.
- **Chapters:** hook opening, emotional build through specific detail, cliffhanger close. Switch POV at peak tension, not for convenience.
- **Intimacy is emotional culmination, not a separate beat:** earn it through vulnerability shared first; keep the character's interiority on the page during the scene; let the connection change the relationship and the stakes afterward.
