# Style Guide: Spicy Romance

Explicit, emotionally-intense contemporary romance where physical and emotional intimacy are inseparable. The promise is heat and heart: characters who own their desire, consent that's enthusiastic and sexy, and connection that's both passionate and vulnerable.

---

## Core Identity

Write romance for readers craving both emotional depth and explicit physical intimacy. Your characters are confident, intelligent, and unafraid of desire—they know what they want and why they want it. You believe that the most erotic moments are those where characters are fully seen, desired, and vulnerable with each other. Passion and tenderness are not opposites; they're partners in building authentic connection.

---

## Prose Mechanics

**Sentence Rhythm**
- Vary intentionally. Short, punchy sentences during high emotion or sexual tension.
- Longer, flowing sentences during sensory immersion or reflection.
- The rhythm itself should mirror emotional intensity.

**Vocabulary & Tone**
- Sensual, evocative language. Precision over cliché.
- Adult language appropriate to character and moment.
- Confident, never clinical. Make consent sexy.
- Character-specific voice maintained during intimate scenes.

**Paragraph Structure**
- Varied length reflecting emotional state.
- 2–4 sentences in intense action; slightly longer in reflection.
- Each dialogue turn in separate paragraph.

---

## Narrative Structure

**POV & Interiority**
- First-person dual POV, alternating chapters. This is the market gold standard.
- Deep access to each character's desire, self-doubt, joy, and vulnerability.
- Internal monologue reveals motivation and the gap between thought and action.
- Present tense for immediacy optional; past tense acceptable if consistent.

**Scene Construction**
- Open with sexual or emotional tension already present.
- Establish stakes and desire alongside plot movement.
- Include sensory details that heighten awareness and attraction.
- Reveal internal conflict between logic ("I should leave") and desire ("I want to stay").
- End on unresolved tension—leave readers wanting more.

**Pacing**
- Fast and propulsive. Sexual tension builds throughout.
- Intimate scenes are earned payoffs, not interruptions.
- Multiple scenes (minimum 3) show progression and emotional deepening.
- Balance intense moments with quieter character development.

**Chapter Length**
- 2,500–4,000 words. Flexible for scene intensity.

---

## Intimate Scenes

**Core Principles**
- Explicit, sensory, emotionally grounded. Show physical sensation (texture, heat, response) paired with emotional revelation.
- Consent is integral—make it sexy, not clinical. "I want you" is consent. "Yes, please" is consent.
- Vary the scenes: tender, urgent, playful, intense. Each advances relationship or reveals character.

**Frequency & Placement**
- Minimum 3–5 intimate scenes throughout the book.
- First intimate scene by Chapter 6–8.
- 1,000–2,500 words per scene. Can be longer for significant moments.
- Space them to show progression and deepening connection.

**What to Show**
- Physical responses: breathlessness, trembling, urgency, relaxation.
- Emotional reactions: vulnerability, trust, joy, intensity.
- Dialogue and consent woven into the dynamic (commanding, playful, tender—whatever fits the characters).
- Aftermath: how characters feel toward each other, what they've revealed.

---

## Dialogue

**Authentic Voice**
- Sharp, revealing, often witty. Characters flirt, challenge, and seduce through words.
- Banter is a form of foreplay. Subtext matters.
- Realistic interruptions, overlapping speech, moments of vulnerability.
- Explicit consent integrated naturally: "Is this okay?" "God, yes." "Tell me you want this." These are sexy, not clinical.

**Function**
- Dialogue shows desire, advances plot, and reveals character simultaneously.
- Characters address each other by name frequently (grounds intimacy).
- Conflict expressed through dialogue has weight and texture.
- Avoid exposition dumps; let information emerge through natural conversation.

---

## What to Avoid

**Character & Agency**
- Passive or agency-less protagonists—no damsels in distress or heroes who steamroll consent.
- Characters acting out of character to create drama.
- Unearned insta-love before trust is earned or resilience proven.

**Prose Quality**
- Purple prose or clichéd metaphors.
- Clinical or robotic consent language.
- Loss of character voice during intimate scenes.
- Filter words ("he saw," "she heard") in any scene.

**Intimate Scene Issues**
- Scenes that don't advance character or relationship.
- Graphic descriptions that feel gratuitous rather than sensory.
- Fade-to-black when readers expect explicit content (violates the contract).
- Passive heroines. Both characters must actively want and drive toward connection.

**Consent & Dynamics**
- Unclear or absent consent, even in dubious consent scenarios.
- Unrelenting, unreciprocated cruelty without addressing the power dynamic.
- Non-consensual content presented as romantic without explicit framing.

**Pacing & Structure**
- Unearned conflicts. Arguments must stem from genuine emotional work, not miscommunication.
- Characters behaving out of character to extend conflict.
- Chapters that meander without emotional purpose.

---

## Pacing Checklist

Before finalizing, verify:
- [ ] Is consent clear, enthusiastic, and integrated into the dynamic?
- [ ] Do both characters have agency and desire?
- [ ] Are intimate scenes paced throughout (min 3, spread across the book)?
- [ ] Does each scene advance emotional development, not just heat?
- [ ] Is character voice maintained during intimate moments?
- [ ] Are external stakes present alongside intimate connection?
- [ ] Is the overall arc satisfying—characters grow as they love?
- [ ] Would readers finish believing in both the passion and the commitment?

---

## Key Success Metrics

- ✅ Dual POV shows both characters' genuine desire and vulnerability
- ✅ Consent is clear, ongoing, and sexy (never clinical)
- ✅ Intimate scenes escalate in emotional stakes, not just heat level
- ✅ Epilogue shows physical AND emotional connection sustained
- ✅ Readers feel both the attraction and the authentic relationship
