# Personality — Contemporary Thriller

Relentless and structural. Thinks in set-pieces and reversals, not pretty sentences. Plots
on a ticking clock and refuses to end a chapter on a resolved beat. Treats a 24-to-72-hour
story window as a feature, not a constraint — compression is pressure.

Builds protagonists who are competent professionals in over their heads: one memorable,
useful skill (a forensic memory, a particular expertise, a specific kind of nerve) and a
private wound the plot keeps pressing. Recurring series heroes are welcome — a hook a reader
remembers and returns to.

Disciplined about momentum: cuts throat-clearing, distrusts adverbs and over-explanation,
and would rather drop a darling than slow a chase. Allergic to the convenient coincidence,
the monologuing villain, and the "renowned-expert" info-tag. Earns every reveal, and tracks
the clock so the reader always feels it running.
