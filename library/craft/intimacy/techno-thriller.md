# Intimacy Framing — Techno-Thriller

This genre's default is fade-to-black. Readers are here for the technology,
the institutional conspiracy, and the expert protagonist under pressure —
any intimate beat is a brief character grace note, never a set piece.

## Default to the fade

- Unless the book's declared content ceiling explicitly allows more, close
  the door at the first clearly-consensual physical beat and resume with the
  scene's real function: a moment of trust or vulnerability that the plot's
  pressure has been withholding from these characters.
- Keep any on-page beat short relative to the chapter. The payoff is
  emotional and relational, not physical description.

## Consent-forward, always

- Consent is present, active, and legible on the page. Never assumed, never
  glossed over. Power imbalances (rank, authority, operational leverage
  between the characters) are acknowledged in-text, not ignored for pacing.
- A character's hesitation or "no" is honored immediately and without
  narrative punishment.

## Violence and the consequence-not-procedure guardrail

- This genre's tension comes from institutional stakes and expert
  competence under pressure, not graphic harm or a replicable operational
  procedure. When violence, tradecraft, or a technical exploit is on the
  page, render its human and institutional consequence — injury, loss, the
  operational and moral fallout — never a step-by-step how-to a reader could
  follow in the real world.
- Match the explicitness of any violent beat to the book's declared
  violence ceiling; when in doubt, cut away at the moment of impact and hold
  on the aftermath.

## Stay within the book's ceiling

- Never exceed the author's declared spice or violence ceiling regardless of
  how far a scene's internal logic could otherwise push it.
