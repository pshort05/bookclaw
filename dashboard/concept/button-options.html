Button treatments

Six directions, all in the Atelier theme. Hover and press each. They're not mutually exclusive — a common pattern is one treatment for primary actions and a quieter one for secondary. Tell me a number (or a mix).

Option 1

Letterpress

Tactile, embossed. Physically presses down on click. The most "object-like" — reads as a crafted control.

click to feel the press
Option 2

Engraved hairline

Mono, letter-spaced caps inside a thin rule. Architectural and restrained — fills faintly with ember on hover.

quiet by default, warms on hover
Option 3

Editorial brackets

Typographic, literary. The bracket marks (or corner ticks) close in on hover. Almost no chrome — the label is the button.

two flavours: [brackets] and corner-ticks
Option 4

Ink stamp

Matte ember, no gloss — a pressed stamp rather than a glossy pill. Mono caps. Feels like a mark made on the page.

matte, not glossy
Option 5

Outline → ember fill

An outline that floods with the ember gradient from the bottom on hover. Keeps the current energy but calmer at rest.

hover to flood
Option 6

Keyed action

Carries its keyboard shortcut as a baked-in chip — leans into the "studio / control surface" feel where everything is fast and addressable.

good for a keyboard-driven studio
