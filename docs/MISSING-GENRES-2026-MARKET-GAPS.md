# Missing Genres: 2026 Market Analysis & Strategic Gaps

## Executive Summary

This document compares BookClaw's current genre library (182 built-in genres) against the 2026 publishing market analysis. **10 genres** identified as commercially critical are either missing or underrepresented. **All 10 are now complete** — each with a full 8-file genre guide (reader-expectations, tropes, themes, beats, must-haves, genre-killers, comps, meta.json).

The source analysis tracks quantitative sales data from Circana BookScan, K-lytics, Amazon Kindle bestsellers, and Kindle Unlimited page-read metrics. Genres are evaluated based on three metrics: (1) the overall Amazon sales rank of the #1 book, (2) the depth of the category (rank of the #50 book), and (3) recency of titles driving sales.

## Critical Market Gaps — Romance (Underserved Subgenres)

### 1. Neurodiverse Contemporary Romance

**Market Position:**
- #1 book ranks ~5,200 overall (strong, healthy market)
- #50 book ranks ~18,000 overall (excellent depth)
- 41 recent releases in top 100 (actively rewarding new authors)

**Gap Analysis:**
This category applies established, highly popular tropes—such as grumpy/sunshine dynamics or workplace rivals—with neurodiversity (Autism, ADHD, anxiety disorders) as a core, accurately depicted character element. The market is in the ideal sweet spot: consistent reader demand, manageable competition, and proven rewarding of fresh content.

With only 41 titles in the top 100, new authors with small backlists achieve consistent four-figure monthly earnings. The subgenre bridges representation and commercial viability, appealing to readers seeking both emotional authenticity and their preferred romance tropes.

**Current BookClaw Coverage:** ✗ MISSING (no neurodiverse-specific romance)

**Recommendation:** Create `neurodiverse-romance` genre.

---

### 2. Blue-Collar & Rural Romance

**Market Position:**
- #1 book ranks ~8,500 overall (highly profitable)
- #50 book ranks ~22,000 overall (excellent depth and reader loyalty)
- 34 recent releases in top 100 (demonstrable market momentum)

**Gap Analysis:**
The decade-long dominance of billionaire romance has created reader fatigue. A growing segment actively rejects narratives centered on extreme corporate wealth and power imbalances, seeking instead grounded, self-sufficient heroes. This opens a massive market gap for romance featuring realistic, labor-intensive occupations: farmers, mechanics, construction workers, and firefighters.

The subgenre capitalizes on the visual aesthetics of the "cottagecore" movement without endorsing regressive gender politics. Readers want the cabin-in-the-woods aesthetic, the capable outdoorsman, and a return to nature. The 34 recent releases prove readers are actively hunting for these hero archetypes.

**Current BookClaw Coverage:** ⚠ PARTIAL (`small-town-romance` exists but doesn't specifically emphasize blue-collar, labor-focused protagonists)

**Recommendation:** Create `blue-collar-romance` or `labor-focused-romance` genre to distinguish from generic small-town romance.

### 3. Women's Sports & Alternative Athletics Romance

**Market Position:**
- #1 book ranks ~7,800 overall (impressive, high-profit position)
- #50 book ranks ~21,000 overall (excellent depth)
- 36 recent releases in top 100 (particularly strong for female protagonists)

**Gap Analysis:**
While mainstream men's hockey and football romance titles are flooding the market at maximum saturation, a massive gap exists for sports romance focused on women's sports or non-mainstream men's sports: soccer, volleyball, rugby, track and field, skiing, climbing.

The narrative mechanics that make mainstream sports romance successful—high-pressure environments, physical excellence, intense rivalries—apply perfectly to alternative sports with virtually no competition from established mega-authors. Data shows particularly strong success for female sports protagonists and athletic disciplines outside North American big-three sports.

**Current BookClaw Coverage:** ✗ MISSING (sports-romance exists but is unsegmented; no women's-specific or alternative-sports variants)

**Recommendation:** Create `women's-sports-romance` and/or `alternative-sports-romance` genres to capture these distinct market segments.

## Critical Market Gaps — Romantasy (Underserved Subgenres)

### 1. Clean & Low-Spice Romantasy

**Market Position:**
Market gap driven by **saturation with explicit content**; massive underserved segment craving epic worldbuilding and intense emotional yearning without explicit sexual content.

**Gap Analysis:**
The virality of romantasy has been heavily driven by high-heat, explicit content, saturating the market and alienating readers who prefer closed-door or clean romance. There is particularly acute demand for "clean dark romance"—exploring intense themes like mafia entanglements, survival, and moral ambiguity while maintaining conservative or Christian values regarding explicit content.

Authors such as Valicity Elaine (Run, Darling) demonstrate commercial viability of tackling dark themes with a faith-focused, clean approach. Authors who deliver sprawling, high-stakes plotting of epic romantasy without relying on explicit sexual content to drive character dynamics will find a deeply loyal, highly evangelical readership currently starved for content.

This represents the inverse of the spicy-romantasy trend: readers want the stakes, the emotional intensity, and the magic—just without the heat.

**Current BookClaw Coverage:** ✗ MISSING (no clean/low-spice romantasy; heat levels are not currently categorized as genres)

**Recommendation:** Create `clean-romantasy` or `low-spice-romantasy` genre.

---

### 2. Science Fiction Romance & Space Fantasy

**Market Position:**
Analyzer assessment: "This is currently where fantasy romance was a decade ago—a niche market waiting for a breakout hit to legitimize it to mainstream."

**Gap Analysis:**
While fantasy romance has consumed the publishing industry, science fiction romance has lagged significantly, creating a massive opportunity for an inevitable market correction. Industry trackers note growing demand for "grounded speculative fiction" and space themes.

Readers express desire for high-stakes space operas utilizing the exact romantasy structural blueprint: forced proximity, fated mates, political marriages. The aesthetic of spaceships, alien diplomacy, and futuristic technology provides a fresh backdrop for established tropes.

April 2026 releases like *First Mage on the Moon* are already being cited by industry trackers as revitalizing the heavily underserved space fantasy subgenre. As the traditional fantasy market approaches total aesthetic saturation, readers will pivot toward sci-fi romantasy hybrids seeking a new visual and thematic backdrop.

This is positioned as the **next major trend cycle** as fantasy saturates.

**Current BookClaw Coverage:** ⚠ PARTIAL
- `science-fiction-romance` exists (generic)
- `space-opera` exists (action/plot-focused, not romance-focused)
- `space-opera-sf` exists (same)
- **MISSING:** A specific space-fantasy or space-romantasy genre emphasizing romance + alien worldbuilding

**Recommendation:** Create `space-romance` or `space-fantasy-romance` to capture the sci-fi romantasy hybrid market specifically.

---

### 3. Literary & High-Prose Romantasy

**Market Position:**
Market gap driven by **aesthetic and quality degradation**; high demand for lyrical prose + romantic fantasy tropes.

**Gap Analysis:**
The rapid expansion of romantasy has been accompanied by severe prose quality degradation. Many authors employ "rapid release" strategies, prioritizing output over editorial refinement. The flood of AI-generated content has resulted in retail platforms choked with algorithmically generated "junk books."

This dynamic has created a highly lucrative market gap for **literary romantasy**. Readers increasingly vocalize desire for high-quality, lyrical prose combined with romantic fantasy tropes. They want political intrigue, slow-burn romance, and magical systems delivered with stylistic care of traditional literary fiction.

2026 London Book Fair scouts report a distinct uptick in appetite for speculative literary fiction. Books that bridge the gap—offering pacing and emotional satisfaction of commercial romance with prose quality of literary fiction—are uniquely positioned to capture both the rabid romantasy fanbase AND the prestige-oriented reading demographic, effectively bypassing the AI-generated noise.

**Current BookClaw Coverage:** ✗ MISSING (no literary-romantasy or upmarket-romantasy genre)

**Recommendation:** Create `literary-romantasy` or `upmarket-romantasy` genre.

## Additional Gap: Gaslamp Romantasy (Specialization)

**Current Status:** `historical-romance` exists as a catch-all but doesn't specifically highlight the gaslamp fantasy subgenre.

**Market Context:** Gaslamp fantasy (historical romantasy set in 19th century with magical elements) is a primary vehicle for queer and LGBTQ+ romance within fantasy. April 2026 releases like *Thistlemarsh* demonstrate commercial viability. The subgenre appeals to readers who appreciate elevated prose, intricate manners, and atmospheric tension, and is a particularly strong demographic for LGBTQ+ representation.

**Recommendation:** Consider specializing `historical-romance` or creating `gaslamp-romantasy` to explicitly serve this demographic and aesthetic.

### Space Romance ✓ CREATED

**What makes this distinct from Space Fantasy Romance:**
- **Emotional tone:** Contemporary-level romantic intimacy despite futuristic setting
- **Worldbuilding complexity:** Lower-stakes magic/tech; grounded speculative fiction focused on the romance
- **External conflict:** Survival, mission objectives, isolation—practical dangers, not cosmic destiny
- **Pacing:** Faster, propulsive; often standalone or part of a smaller series
- **Length:** 70,000–100,000 words (standard romance length)
- **Genre distinctiveness:** Sci-fi romance where the space setting amplifies emotional stakes, not magical/political complexity

### Space Fantasy Romance ✓ CREATED

**What makes this distinct from Space Romance:**
- **Emotional tone:** Epic, operatic, fate-driven; cosmic destiny matters
- **Worldbuilding complexity:** High-stakes magic systems, political intrigue, alien civilizations, inter-species dynamics
- **External conflict:** Political courts, cosmic warfare, prophecy, moral quandaries on a galactic scale
- **Pacing:** Can be slower; readers expect immersive worldbuilding despite fast emotional escalation
- **Length:** 90,000–150,000+ words (higher end of commercial romance/fantasy)
- **Series potential:** Often first in a series; worldbuilding demands sustained depth
- **Genre distinctiveness:** Romantasy (load-bearing romance) set in space; combines the *size* of space opera with the *emotional intensity* of romantasy tropes

**Market positioning:** Analysts identify this as the **next major trend cycle** as traditional fantasy approaches saturation and readers seek fresh backdrops for established romantasy tropes.

**Files created:** `reader-expectations.md`, `tropes.md`, `themes.md`, `meta.json`

### LGBTQ+ Romance ✓ CREATED

**What makes this a distinct genre (not a modifier):**
- **Centering:** LGBTQ+ protagonists and queer identity are central, not incidental
- **Cross-genre applicability:** Can be contemporary, historical, fantasy, paranormal, sports, workplace—any romance subgenre
- **Market demand:** Strong reader base actively seeking authentic queer representation; distinct purchasing patterns and community engagement
- **Authenticity requirement:** Representation quality matters; sensitivity and lived-experience consultation are essential
- **Thematic depth:** Explores identity, authenticity, chosen family, systemic barriers, and the particular joy of queer love
- **Heat flexibility:** LGBTQ+ readers have diverse heat preferences; heat level should never be apologized for
- **Genre distinctiveness:** A standalone genre encompassing gay, lesbian, bisexual, trans, non-binary, asexual-spectrum, and queer romance

**Market context:** Historical romantasy (e.g., gaslamp) has already proven strong LGBTQ+ representation; this genre centralizes queer identity as the primary market signal.
