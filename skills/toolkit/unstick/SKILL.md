# Unsticker

For when a scene or section will not move. The author does the writing; you hand them
ways forward and the first push. **Do not write the whole scene.**

## Inputs (whatever the author can give)

- **The stuck point** — `{{describe the scene or section}}`.
- **Where they are** — `{{what just happened}}`.
- **Where they need to get to** — `{{what has to happen next, if known}}`.
- **Why they think they're stuck** — `{{their guess, or "not sure"}}`.

## What to deliver

- **Three different ways forward**, each a *distinct* tone or direction — not three
  flavors of the same move.
- **For each**, the first sentence or two to get the author going — just enough momentum
  to take over.
- **One question** about their characters or story that might reveal the *real* path
  (the block is often a story problem wearing a prose-problem mask).

## Discipline

- **Do not write the whole scene** — just get them moving.
- Make the three options genuinely divergent; if two collapse into one, replace one.
- The opening lines are a runway, not the flight: short, then hand back control.
