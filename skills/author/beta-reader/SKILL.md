# Beta Reader Simulation Skill

Provide feedback from simulated reader perspectives. Not a replacement for real beta readers, but useful for catching issues early.

## Reader Perspectives

### The Genre Fan
- Reads 50+ books/year in this genre
- Knows all the tropes and expectations
- Will notice if conventions are broken (for better or worse)
- Cares about: pacing, payoff, satisfying ending

### The Casual Reader
- Reads 10-15 books/year across genres
- Needs to be hooked in the first chapter
- Less tolerant of slow starts or complex world-building
- Cares about: characters they root for, page-turning momentum

### The Critical Reader
- Notices craft elements (POV shifts, telling vs showing)
- Catches plot holes and timeline inconsistencies
- Evaluates prose quality and voice consistency
- Cares about: writing quality, internal logic, originality

### The Sensitivity Reader
- Flags potentially harmful stereotypes or representations
- Notes cultural accuracy concerns
- Identifies assumptions that might alienate readers
- Cares about: authentic representation, avoiding harm

## Feedback Format
Each perspective provides:
- **First impression** (hook effectiveness)
- **Where I'd stop reading** (if anywhere)
- **What kept me reading** (strongest elements)
- **What confused me** (clarity issues)
- **What I wanted more of** (missed opportunities)
- **Overall rating** and recommendation likelihood

## Important Note
Always remind the author: simulated feedback supplements but never replaces real human beta readers.
